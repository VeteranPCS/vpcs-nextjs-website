If you have orders for a Permanent Change of Station (PCS) to Marine Corps Air Station (MCAS) Beaufort, welcome to the South Carolina Lowcountry. Tidal marshes, live oaks draped with Spanish moss, and a military community that runs deep — this 2026 guide is built for the Marines and families who are about to call it home.

We cover what the installation does, where families live, how housing pay works, schools, healthcare, climate, and things to do. A move is a lot to manage. Our goal is to make the MCAS Beaufort part feel a little smaller and a lot clearer.

## What and Where Is MCAS Beaufort?

Marine Corps Air Station Beaufort is located in Beaufort County, South Carolina, about 70 miles southwest of Charleston and roughly 40 miles northeast of Savannah, Georgia. According to the [MCAS Beaufort official site](https://www.beaufort.marines.mil/), the installation's mission is to support establishment operations for 2nd Marine Aircraft Wing (2nd MAW), attached II Marine Expeditionary Force (II MEF) units, and Marine Corps Recruit Depot (MCRD) Parris Island and the Eastern Recruiting Region.

The flying mission at MCAS Beaufort belongs to [Marine Aircraft Group 31 (MAG-31)](https://www.2ndmaw.marines.mil/Units/MAG-31/), a component of the 2nd MAW. MAG-31 is in the middle of a significant transition from the F/A-18 Hornet to the F-35B Lightning II. Marine Fighter Attack Squadron (VMFA) 533 received the first F-35B jets assigned to an operational squadron at MCAS Beaufort in 2024, and VMFA-224 was redesignated as an F-35B squadron in 2025. The fleet replacement squadron, VMFAT-501, trains F-35B pilots at the station. VMFA-312 is also based here. If you work on the flight line, expect a fast-moving program with a mix of legacy Hornet operations and a growing F-35B presence.

## Where to Live When PCSing to MCAS Beaufort

Most families who move to MCAS Beaufort either live in on-post housing or settle in one of the surrounding communities. Your choice usually comes down to commute, budget, schools, and the feel you want. Ask your sponsor and the housing office about on-post options and wait times before committing to renting or buying off post.

### Beaufort

The city of Beaufort is the closest off-post option, about 10 minutes from the main gate. It has a walkable historic downtown, waterfront parks, restaurants, and a strong military community. If you want to be close to the base and close to local amenities, Beaufort is a natural first choice for many families. Housing ranges from established neighborhoods to newer construction in the surrounding area.

### Port Royal

Port Royal is adjacent to Beaufort and sits equally close to the installation, roughly 10 minutes away. It is a small, tight-knit town with a waterfront and a growing restaurant scene. For families who want the short commute but a slightly quieter feel than downtown Beaufort, Port Royal is worth a look.

### Bluffton

Bluffton is about 30 minutes south of the installation, between Beaufort and Hilton Head Island. It has grown rapidly and now offers a wide range of neighborhoods, shopping, and dining. Many families choose Bluffton for newer construction and easy access to Hilton Head beaches without Hilton Head prices.

### Ridgeland

Ridgeland sits roughly 30 minutes inland to the northwest, in Jasper County. It is a more rural option that can offer more home for the money, though you will be farther from the coast. Families who prioritize budget and space over beach proximity sometimes settle here.

### Hilton Head Island

Hilton Head Island is about 40 minutes from the main gate. Housing costs run higher than the mainland, but some families accept the commute for the beaches, golf, and bike trails. The bridge and main corridor can slow during peak tourist seasons, so check drive times at the hours you will actually travel.

## MCAS Beaufort BAH and the Cost of Living

If you live off post, the military helps cover your housing through Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH). BAH is a monthly, tax-free payment. The amount depends on three things: your rank, whether you have dependents, and your duty station's Military Housing Area (MHA). An MHA is the geographic zone the Department of Defense uses to set local housing pay.

Because rates change each year and vary by rank and dependent status, we do not publish a rate table here. To find your exact figure, use the official DoD BAH calculator at [travel.dod.mil BAH Rate Lookup](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/). Enter your rank and ZIP code to see the current rate for the Beaufort area. Always verify your rate before signing a lease or making an offer.

For a sense of buying costs, the typical home value in Beaufort was about $365,910 in June 2026, according to [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/44081/beaufort-sc/). That is a typical value, not a median sale price. As of the week of June 18, 2026, [Freddie Mac](https://www.freddiemac.com/pmms) reported the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate at 6.47 percent. Whether buying or renting makes more sense depends on how long you expect to be stationed here, so run the numbers for your own situation.

A VA loan (a home loan backed by the Department of Veterans Affairs) can be a strong option for eligible service members. ## Schools Near MCAS Beaufort

The schools your children attend depend on where you live. [Beaufort County School District](https://www.beaufortschools.net/) serves the communities around the installation, including Beaufort, Port Royal, and Bluffton, covering pre-kindergarten through grade 12 across 32 schools, according to [NCES data](https://nces.ed.gov/ccd/districtsearch/district_detail.asp?ID2=4501110).

Beaufort County School District is a designated Military Purple Star District. According to the district's [military families page](https://www.beaufortschools.net/families/military-families), it employs full-time Military Family Life Counselors (MFLCs) to support military-connected children with counseling and transition services. Attendance zones can shift, so confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit.

## Healthcare: Naval Hospital Beaufort

On-post medical care is centered at [Naval Hospital Beaufort](https://beaufort.tricare.mil/), located at 1 Pinckney Boulevard, Beaufort, South Carolina. Opened in 1949, the hospital provides general medical, surgical, and urgent care services to eligible active duty Navy and Marine Corps personnel, retired military, and their dependents — a total population of approximately 35,000 beneficiaries, according to the hospital's [About Us page](https://beaufort.tricare.mil/About-Us). Naval Hospital Beaufort also operates branch health clinics at MCRD Parris Island and MCAS Beaufort.

Your military health coverage runs through TRICARE, the health care program for service members and their families. Set up your TRICARE plan early, update your information in DEERS (Defense Enrollment Eligibility Reporting System), and arrange any referrals before you arrive so there is no gap in care. Review services through the [Naval Hospital Beaufort website](https://beaufort.tricare.mil/).

## Climate and Things to Do

Beaufort sits in the Lowcountry coastal zone, and the climate reflects it. The [National Weather Service Charleston office](https://www.weather.gov/chs/climate) covers the region: warm, humid summers with high humidity from June through September, and mild winters that rarely dip below freezing. The most important seasonal factor: the Atlantic hurricane season runs from June through November, and Beaufort's coastal geography means evacuation routes and storm plans are something every family should know when they arrive.

The payoff is one of the most beautiful stretches of the American coast. [Hunting Island State Park](https://southcarolinaparks.com/hunting-island), located about 16 miles east of downtown Beaufort, is South Carolina's most visited state park, with five miles of beach, salt marsh, tidal creeks, maritime forest, and a historic lighthouse that is the only publicly accessible historic lighthouse in the state. Camping is available at 100 sites with hookups, and walk-in tent camping is also offered. Reservations fill fast, especially in summer.

The ACE Basin — named for the Ashepoo, Combahee, and Edisto rivers — is one of the largest undeveloped estuaries on the East Coast and a destination for kayaking, fishing, and birdwatching. Downtown Beaufort has antebellum architecture, a waterfront park, and strong local restaurants. Hilton Head is 40 minutes away for beaches, trails, and golf. Savannah, Georgia, with its historic district and food scene, is about an hour's drive.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What MHA is MCAS Beaufort in for BAH purposes?

MCAS Beaufort falls within the Beaufort, South Carolina Military Housing Area for BAH purposes. Your rate depends on your rank and whether you have dependents. Look up your exact figure with the official [DoD BAH calculator](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/) using your rank and ZIP code.

### Where do military families typically live near MCAS Beaufort?

Common choices include Beaufort and Port Royal for the shortest commutes, Bluffton for newer construction and proximity to Hilton Head, and Ridgeland for a more rural, budget-conscious option. Hilton Head Island is popular with some families despite a longer drive. On-post housing is also available; ask the housing office about wait times.

### What flying aircraft does MAG-31 operate at MCAS Beaufort?

MAG-31 is transitioning from the F/A-18 Hornet to the F-35B Lightning II. VMFA-533 and VMFA-224 are now designated as F-35B squadrons, and VMFAT-501 trains F-35B pilots at the station. VMFA-312 is also based at MCAS Beaufort.

### Which school district serves families near MCAS Beaufort?

Beaufort County School District serves the communities around the installation, including Beaufort, Port Royal, and Bluffton. The district is a designated Military Purple Star District with Military Family Life Counselors to support transition. Confirm the assigned school for your specific address before committing to a home.

### Where do I get healthcare at MCAS Beaufort?

Healthcare for eligible service members, retirees, and dependents in the Beaufort area is centered at Naval Hospital Beaufort, at 1 Pinckney Boulevard, Beaufort. Set up your TRICARE plan, update DEERS, and arrange referrals before you arrive so your care continues without a gap.

### What is hurricane season like in Beaufort?

The Atlantic hurricane season runs from June through November. Beaufort's coastal location means it can be in the path of tropical systems. Learn the local evacuation routes and procedures when you arrive. The [NWS Charleston office](https://www.weather.gov/chs/) publishes tropical storm outlooks and warnings for the region.
