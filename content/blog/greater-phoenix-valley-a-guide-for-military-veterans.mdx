The Greater Phoenix Valley continues to attract military veterans and active-duty service members at every career stage. From those currently stationed at Luke Air Force Base to highly skilled veterans transitioning into civilian leadership roles, the region offers a unique blend of affordability, economic growth, and community infrastructure that supports long-term settlement for individuals and families alike.

Whether you are PCSing to Luke AFB or choosing where to put down roots after your service, Phoenix and its surrounding cities deserve a close look. If you are still in the early stages of your move, the [VeteranPCS Ultimate PCS Checklist](https://www.veteranpcs.com/blog/the-ultimate-pcs-checklist-and-timeline-for-active-duty-military-personnel) can help you plan every step of the way.

## Tax Advantages That Benefit Military Households

Arizona provides meaningful financial advantages for military families. Active-duty military pay is exempt from Arizona state income tax, and the state does not tax military retirement income. According to the [Arizona Department of Revenue](https://azdor.gov/individual-income-tax-filing-assistance/military-filing-information), these policies can significantly reduce your cost of living and create additional flexibility for homeownership, investment, and retirement planning.

These tax benefits are especially valuable for veterans moving into second careers or private practice. Combined with Arizona's relatively low property tax rates, the financial picture for military families in the Phoenix area is compelling.

If you are considering buying a home with your VA loan benefit, understanding [VA loan eligibility requirements](https://www.veteranpcs.com/blog/va-loan-eligibility-requirements-how-to-know-if-you-qualify-for-the-va-loan) is a smart first step. You can also learn [how a zero-down VA loan works](https://www.veteranpcs.com/blog/how-does-a-0-down-va-loan-work) to maximize your purchasing power.

## A Strong Job Market for Experienced Veterans

The Valley's economy is especially attractive to senior-level veterans, including physicians, attorneys, engineers, and executives. A strong and diverse job market offers opportunities across expanding healthcare systems, microchip manufacturing, technology and aerospace firms, legal and financial services, and engineering-driven industries.

Well-known companies like Boeing, Northrop Grumman, GoDaddy, and Phoenix Defense bring major employment opportunities for veterans across the region. The [Department of Labor's Veterans' Employment and Training Service](https://www.dol.gov/agencies/vets) provides additional resources for veterans entering the civilian workforce. Arizona's growing defense and technology sectors make the Phoenix Valley one of the most promising job markets in the country for military-experienced professionals.Neighborhoods That Fit Every Lifestyle

The Greater Phoenix Valley spans a wide range of communities, each with its own character and advantages.

Communities like Scottsdale, Gilbert, and Paradise Valley are well known for their professional networks, high-quality schools, and upscale housing options. These areas attract veterans who are established in their civilian careers and looking for strong resale value and top-rated school districts.

West Valley cities like Surprise, Goodyear, Litchfield Park, and Buckeye provide convenient access to Luke AFB and growing employment centers. These areas offer newer construction, larger lots, and more affordable price points, making them popular with active-duty families who want space and value without a long commute to base.

Use the [VeteranPCS BAH Calculator](https://www.veteranpcs.com/bah-calculator) to estimate your Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH) for the Phoenix area and see what you can afford. In many cases, your BAH can cover a mortgage on a property that builds equity rather than paying rent.

## Lifestyle and Quality of Life

Beyond career and financial considerations, the Greater Phoenix Valley offers an exceptional lifestyle. With over 300 days of sunshine each year, mild winters, and year-round outdoor recreation, the region supports a healthy and active way of life. Hiking, golf, mountain biking, and water sports are all accessible within a short drive from most Valley neighborhoods.

The area is also home to strong veteran support organizations, including local [Veterans Affairs medical facilities](https://www.va.gov/phoenix-health-care/) and community groups that help veterans transition into civilian life. Combined with a wide range of housing opportunities, from family-friendly neighborhoods to high-end estates, the Valley remains a compelling place for veterans to put down roots.
