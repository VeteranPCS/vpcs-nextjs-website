If you have orders for a Permanent Change of Station, or PCS, to Malmstrom Air Force Base, welcome to Big Sky Country. Malmstrom AFB sits at the foot of the Rocky Mountain Front, about three miles from downtown Great Falls, Montana, and it carries one of the most consequential missions in the Air Force. This 2026 guide walks you through what to expect — where families live, how housing pay works, schools, healthcare, and life in north-central Montana.

## What and Where Is Malmstrom AFB?

Malmstrom Air Force Base is located in Cascade County in north-central Montana, about three miles northeast of downtown Great Falls. The installation is home to the [341st Missile Wing](https://www.malmstrom.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/346869/341st-missile-wing/), which is one of three U.S. Air Force wings that operates, maintains, and secures the Minuteman III intercontinental ballistic missile, or ICBM.

According to the Malmstrom AFB fact sheet, the 341st Missile Wing's mission is to "defend America with combat-ready Airmen and ICBM forces." The wing reports to 20th Air Force at F.E. Warren Air Force Base and is part of Air Force Global Strike Command. Approximately 4,000 people serve under the wing, including more than 3,300 active-duty personnel and about 600 civilians.

The wing is organized into five groups: the 341st Operations Group, 341st Maintenance Group, 341st Mission Support Group, 341st Security Forces Group, and 341st Medical Group. Three missile squadrons — the 10th, 12th, and 490th — each manage five missile alert facilities and 50 launch facilities spread across a 13,800-square-mile complex covering nine Montana counties. The 40th and 550th Helicopter Squadrons support security and transport throughout the missile field. This is a mission-focused installation with continuous deterrence operations since 1962.

## Where to Live When PCSing to Malmstrom AFB

Most families who move to Malmstrom either live in on-post housing through [Balfour Beatty privatized housing](https://www.malmstromafbhomes.com/) or settle in one of the communities around Great Falls. Your choice usually comes down to commute, schools, budget, and the feel you want. Ask your sponsor and the Military Housing Office about current on-post availability and wait times before you commit to an off-post search.

### Great Falls

Great Falls is the city that wraps around Malmstrom and is the most common choice for families stationed here. Commutes from most Great Falls neighborhoods run about 10 minutes to the main gate. You get a full range of grocery stores, restaurants, a regional hospital, parks, and the Missouri River trail system, all within a few minutes of home. If you want city amenities and a short drive, Great Falls is the straightforward pick.

### Black Eagle

Black Eagle is a small community immediately north of Great Falls and east of the river, with a similar commute of about 10 minutes to the base. It has a quiet, residential feel with older established homes and is a solid option for families who want to be close to both the base and the city.

### Vaughn

Vaughn is a small agricultural town roughly 20 minutes northwest of Malmstrom, off U.S. Highway 89. It sits near the Sun River and offers a rural feel and more land for the money. Many families find the commute short enough to be worth the trade, especially if they want space for animals or proximity to hunting and fishing country.

### Belt

Belt is a small town in the Belt Mountains, about 25 minutes southeast of the base. It sits along Belt Creek with a tight-knit community and more affordable housing than Great Falls. The surrounding country — rolling foothills, creek fishing, and big-sky views — appeals to outdoor-minded families willing to drive a bit.

## Malmstrom AFB BAH and the Cost of Living

If you live off post, the military helps cover your housing through BAH, which stands for Basic Allowance for Housing. BAH is a monthly, tax-free payment determined by your rank, whether you have dependents, and your duty station's Military Housing Area, or MHA. An MHA is the geographic zone the Department of Defense uses to set local housing pay. Malmstrom AFB falls within the Great Falls, Montana MHA.

Because rates change each year and vary by rank and dependent status, we do not publish a rate table here. To find your exact figure, use the official DoD BAH calculator at [travel.dod.mil BAH Rate Lookup](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/). Enter your rank and ZIP code to see the current rate. Exact BAH rates are verified separately, so always confirm yours before signing a lease or making an offer.

For a sense of buying costs, the average home value in Great Falls was about $342,702 in May 2026, according to [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/52308/great-falls-mt/). That is a typical value, not a median sale price. As of the week of June 18, 2026, [Freddie Mac](https://www.freddiemac.com/pmms) reported the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate at 6.47 percent. Whether buying or renting makes more sense depends on how long you expect to be stationed here, so run the numbers for your own situation.

## Schools Near Malmstrom AFB

The schools your children attend depend on where you live. Great Falls and the surrounding area are served by two separate public school entities.

[Great Falls Public Schools](https://gfps.k12.mt.us/schools) operates the public schools in Great Falls and the immediate area. According to [NCES data](https://nces.ed.gov/ccd/districtsearch/district_detail.asp?ID2=3013050), the district includes multiple elementary schools, middle schools, and two high schools — Great Falls High School and C.M. Russell High School — serving thousands of students across the city. For families living in Great Falls or Black Eagle, this is the most likely district.

Families who settle in Vaughn or other rural areas outside city boundaries may fall under smaller county or rural districts. And in Belt, students typically attend Belt Public Schools, a separate small district. Confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit — attendance boundaries do not always match city lines.

## Healthcare: 341st Medical Group

On-post medical care at Malmstrom is provided by the [341st Medical Group](https://malmstrom.tricare.mil/), which is responsible for medical and dental care for nearly 15,000 beneficiaries throughout north-central Montana.

Your military health coverage runs through TRICARE, the health care program for service members and their families. Set up your TRICARE plan early in your PCS — update your information in DEERS, choose or transfer your plan, and arrange any referrals through the [341st Medical Group Appointments and Referrals page](https://malmstrom.tricare.mil/Getting-Care/Appointments-Referrals) before you arrive so there is no gap in care.

## Climate and Things to Do

Great Falls sits at about 3,330 feet above sea level on the northern Great Plains, just east of the Rocky Mountain Front. The [National Weather Service Great Falls office](https://www.weather.gov/tfx) serves this region and is the right place to check climate normals and current outlooks before your move.

The climate is a northern plains continental pattern — cold, dry winters and warm summers with wide temperature swings. Great Falls is known for chinook winds, the warm, dry downslope winds that can raise temperatures 30 degrees or more in a matter of hours and rapidly melt snow. Fast-moving weather is the norm here: clear mornings can become blizzards by afternoon. Come prepared for cold winters, and keep your vehicle stocked for winter conditions.

The payoff for that climate is the setting. [Giant Springs State Park](https://fwp.mt.gov/stateparks/giant-springs), managed by Montana Fish, Wildlife and Parks, sits just outside Great Falls along the Missouri River shoreline. It features the legendary Giant Springs — one of the largest freshwater springs in the country, flowing more than 156 million gallons per day at a constant 54 degrees Fahrenheit, first recorded by the Lewis and Clark Expedition in 1805. The park is the most visited state park in Montana and connects to nearly 60 miles of the River's Edge Trail System for running, cycling, and walking with kids.

The Missouri River also produced five waterfalls along a 10-mile stretch that gave Great Falls its name. Beyond the city, the Rocky Mountain Front offers hunting, fishing, and backcountry hiking. The Lewis and Clark National Forest and the Bob Marshall Wilderness are within reach for weekend trips.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What MHA or area is Malmstrom AFB in for BAH?

Malmstrom AFB falls within the Great Falls, Montana Military Housing Area for BAH purposes. Your rate depends on your rank and whether you have dependents. Look up your exact figure with the official [DoD BAH calculator](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/) using your rank and ZIP code.

### Where do military families live near Malmstrom AFB?

Common choices include Great Falls and Black Eagle, which are closest to the base; Vaughn for a rural feel with a short commute; and Belt for more space and lower housing costs with a modest drive. On-post privatized housing through Balfour Beatty is also an option — ask the Military Housing Office about availability.

### What is the 341st Missile Wing's mission?

### What is the weather like in Great Falls, Montana?

Great Falls has a northern plains continental climate with cold winters, warm summers, and strong winds. The Rocky Mountain Front generates chinook winds that can rapidly warm temperatures and melt snow. The [National Weather Service Great Falls office](https://www.weather.gov/tfx) has local climate data and forecasts. Come prepared for winter driving conditions.

### Which school district will my children attend near Malmstrom AFB?

### Where do I get healthcare at Malmstrom AFB?
