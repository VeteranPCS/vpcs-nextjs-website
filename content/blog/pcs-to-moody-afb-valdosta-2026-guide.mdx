If you have orders for a PCS to Moody Air Force Base, you are heading to a corner of South Georgia where live oaks draped in Spanish moss shade quiet lakes and longleaf pine forests stretch to the horizon. PCS stands for Permanent Change of Station, the military term for moving from one duty station to another. Moody AFB sits just north of Valdosta, a mid-sized city in Lowndes County, Georgia, and it is the home of the 23rd Wing. This 2027 guide is written for the airmen, families, and civilians who are getting ready to make the move. We will cover the wing's mission, where to live, how housing pay works, schools, healthcare, climate, and things to do once you are settled.

## What and Where Is Moody Air Force Base?

Moody Air Force Base is a U.S. Air Force installation located about nine miles north of downtown Valdosta in Lowndes County, Georgia. It falls under Air Combat Command, or ACC.

The host unit is the [23rd Wing](https://www.moody.af.mil/About-Us/Units/23rd-Wing/), whose stated mission is to "optimally organize, train, equip and deploy precision attack, personnel recovery, and combat support to win today's fight." The wing organizes, trains, and employs combat-ready A-10C Thunderbolt II, HC-130J Combat King II, HH-60W Jolly Green II, and Guardian Angel Weapons System aircraft, with more than 4,000 airmen and over 700 civilian employees across two locations, including Avon Park Air Force Range in Florida.

Two major subordinate groups handle the flying mission. The [23rd Fighter Group](https://www.moody.af.mil/About-Us/Units/23rd-Wing/23d-Fighter-Group/) directs operations for the Air Force's largest A-10C fighter group, providing close air support, forward air control, and combat support to combatant commanders worldwide. The [347th Rescue Group](https://www.moody.af.mil/About-Us/Units/23rd-Wing/347th-Rescue-Group/) is the oldest active-duty operations group in the Air Force dedicated to personnel recovery, flying HC-130J Combat King IIs, HH-60W Jolly Green IIs, and Guardian Angel weapon systems to recover isolated personnel and conduct humanitarian and medical evacuation missions. The 23rd Wing also traces its lineage to the legendary "Flying Tigers" of World War II.

## Where to Live When PCSing to Moody AFB

Most families who PCS to Moody choose between on-post housing and one of the communities around Valdosta. Your decision usually comes down to commute, schools, budget, and the feel you want. Talk to your sponsor and the housing office about on-installation availability and wait times before you commit.

### Hahira

Hahira is the closest off-post city, about 10 minutes from the main gate. It is a small, tight-knit community with newer subdivisions, a quiet feel, and short commutes. Families who want a simple drive to work without the traffic of a larger city tend to like Hahira.

### Valdosta

Valdosta, the county seat, is about 15 minutes from the base and offers the most in terms of amenities: shopping, restaurants, Valdosta State University, and a wider range of housing at various price points. It is the most popular off-base community for Moody families.

### Lake Park

Lake Park sits about 20 minutes south, near the Georgia–Florida state line. It is a small community with easy interstate access and a quieter, more rural feel. Some families choose it for more land or a lower price point while keeping a manageable commute.

### Lakeland

Lakeland is roughly 25 minutes to the northeast. It is a small Georgia town with open land and a slower pace, a good fit for families who want space and are comfortable with a slightly longer drive.

### Nashville

Nashville, Georgia — about 25 minutes north of Moody in Berrien County — offers a small-town setting, lower prices, and easy access to the base via US-129.

## BAH and the Cost of Living Near Moody AFB

If you live off installation, the military helps cover your housing through BAH, which stands for Basic Allowance for Housing. BAH is a monthly, tax-free payment. The amount depends on your rank, whether you have dependents, and your duty station's Military Housing Area, or MHA — the geographic zone the Department of Defense uses to set local housing pay. Moody AFB falls within the Valdosta, Georgia MHA.

Because rates change each year and vary by rank and dependent status, we do not publish a rate table here. Use the official DoD calculator at [travel.dod.mil BAH Rate Lookup](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/) and enter your rank and ZIP code. Always confirm your rate before signing a lease or making an offer.

For a sense of buying costs, the typical home value in Valdosta was about $214,662 as of May 2026, according to [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/54902/valdosta-ga/). That is a typical value, not a median sale price. As of the week of June 19, 2026, [Freddie Mac](https://www.freddiemac.com/pmms) reported the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate at 6.47 percent. Run the numbers for your own timeline before deciding whether to buy or rent.

## Schools Near Moody AFB

The schools your children attend depend on where you live. Two separate civilian systems serve most of the Moody AFB community.

[Lowndes County Schools](https://www.lowndes.k12.ga.us/) covers unincorporated Lowndes County and communities outside Valdosta's city limits. According to the National Center for Education Statistics, the district serves roughly 10,600 students in grades PK–12, including Lowndes High School. Families in Hahira typically fall under this district.

[Valdosta City Schools](https://nces.ed.gov/ccd/districtsearch/district_detail.asp?ID2=1305310) is a separate system serving families within the city limits of Valdosta. Confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit, as boundaries can shift.

## Healthcare: 23rd Medical Group

On-installation medical care at Moody AFB is provided by the [23rd Medical Group](https://moody.tricare.mil/), located at 3278 Mitchell Blvd. According to the group's official page, the 23rd MDG provides outpatient medical, dental, occupational, environmental, and preventive healthcare services to more than 24,300 eligible beneficiaries. Appointment and referral information is at [moody.tricare.mil](https://moody.tricare.mil/Getting-Care/Appointments-Referrals).

Your military health coverage runs through TRICARE, the health care program for service members and their families. Set up your TRICARE plan early, update your information in DEERS, and line up any referrals before you arrive so there is no gap in care.

## Climate and Things to Do Near Valdosta

Valdosta sits in South Georgia in a humid subtropical climate zone, with long, hot, humid summers and short, mild winters. The [National Weather Service in Tallahassee](https://www.weather.gov/tae/climate), which covers South Georgia, maintains local climate normals for month-by-month averages. Expect heat and humidity from May through September, comfortable winters with occasional frost, and a landscape that stays green most of the year.

The payoff for those warm summers is the outdoor setting. [Wild Adventures Theme Park](https://www.wildadventures.com/) in Valdosta is a theme park, water park, and animal park in one, spanning more than 170 acres. It is rated the number-one theme park in Georgia by TripAdvisor and hosts live concerts and seasonal events throughout the year.

For time outdoors, [Reed Bingham State Park](https://gastateparks.org/ReedBingham) in Adel, about 24 miles north of Valdosta, offers a 375-acre lake with excellent fishing for bass, crappie, and catfish, plus canoe and kayak rentals, seven miles of trails, and abundant wildlife including nesting bald eagles in winter and American alligators along the shoreline.

[Grand Bay Wildlife Management Area](https://www.georgiawildlife.com/grand-bay-wma) near Valdosta provides hunting and wildlife viewing in a large wetland and upland habitat. The area is a good spot for birding, with a diverse mix of species found in the longleaf pine flatwoods and wetland edges typical of South Georgia.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What MHA is Moody AFB in for BAH?

Moody AFB falls within the Valdosta, Georgia Military Housing Area. Look up your exact rate with the [DoD BAH calculator](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/) using your rank and ZIP code.

### What is the 23rd Wing's mission at Moody AFB?

According to the [23rd Wing's official page](https://www.moody.af.mil/About-Us/Units/23rd-Wing/), the mission is to "optimally organize, train, equip and deploy precision attack, personnel recovery, and combat support to win today's fight." The wing flies A-10C Thunderbolt IIs for close air support, plus HC-130J Combat King IIs, HH-60W Jolly Green IIs, and Guardian Angel weapon systems for combat search and rescue and personnel recovery.

### Where do military families live near Moody AFB?

Common choices include Hahira (closest, about 10 minutes), Valdosta (15 minutes, most amenities), Lake Park to the south, and Lakeland or Nashville for smaller-town options at about 25 minutes. On-installation housing may also be available; ask your sponsor and the housing office.

### Which school district will my children attend?

Families in unincorporated Lowndes County and communities like Hahira typically attend [Lowndes County Schools](https://www.lowndes.k12.ga.us/). Families within Valdosta city limits fall under Valdosta City Schools. Confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit.

### What is the weather like near Valdosta?

Valdosta has a humid subtropical climate — long, hot, humid summers and short, mild winters. The [National Weather Service in Tallahassee](https://www.weather.gov/tae/climate) maintains monthly climate normals for the region. Expect occasional frost in winter but rarely significant snow.
