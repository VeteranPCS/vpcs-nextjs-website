Nebraska has a long military history, dating back to the establishment of early forts used to protect trade routes and interact with Native American tribes. The state has played a role in every major U.S. conflict, and in 1881, it formally organized its National Guard. While historic military sites like **Fort Atkinson, Fort Kearny, and Fort Omaha** once operated in Nebraska, they have all closed, with **Fort Omaha ceasing operations in 1974**. Today, **Offutt Air Force Base** stands as the state's only active military installation.

### **Offutt Air Force Base**

**A Legacy of Aviation and National Defense**

Offutt Air Force Base, located in **Bellevue, Nebraska**, has been at the center of U.S. military aviation and strategic defense for over a century. Originally established in the **1890s as Fort Crook**, it became a key site for **early military aviation** in **1918**. During **World War II**, Offutt transformed into a major production hub, assembling over **1,500 B-26 Marauder bombers** and numerous **B-29 Superfortresses**. Notably, the **Enola Gay**, the aircraft that dropped the atomic bomb on Hiroshima, was produced here.

Following the war, Offutt AFB evolved into a **critical command center for U.S. strategic defense**. Today, it serves as the headquarters for **United States Strategic Command (USSTRATCOM)**, overseeing the **nation’s nuclear arsenal, missile defense systems, and global strategic deterrence operations**.

#### **Key Functions of Offutt AFB**

- **Headquarters of USSTRATCOM**, responsible for **nuclear deterrence and global strike operations**.
- **Home to the 55th Wing**, which conducts **reconnaissance, intelligence, and surveillance missions** worldwide.
- **Supports the National Airborne Operations Center (NAOC)**, ensuring command and control continuity in national emergencies.
- **A major employer in Nebraska**, with thousands of active-duty personnel, civilians, and contractors supporting operations.

For more information, visit the [**Offutt Air Force Base website**](https://www.offutt.af.mil/).
