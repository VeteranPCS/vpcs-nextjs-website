If you have orders for a PCS (Permanent Change of Station) to Seymour Johnson Air Force Base, welcome to eastern North Carolina. Seymour Johnson AFB sits inside the city of Goldsboro, in Wayne County, and it is home to one of the Air Force's most capable fighter wings. This 2026 guide covers what you need to know before you arrive: the base's mission, where families tend to live, how housing pay works, schools, healthcare, the local climate, and things to do once you are settled.

A PCS move brings a lot of moving parts. Our goal is to make the Goldsboro part feel manageable.

## What and Where Is Seymour Johnson AFB?

Seymour Johnson Air Force Base is located within the city limits of Goldsboro, North Carolina, roughly 50 miles southeast of Raleigh. The base is the home of the [4th Fighter Wing](https://www.seymourjohnson.af.mil/), known as Team Seymour, which flies the F-15E Strike Eagle.

According to the [official F-15E fact sheet on seymourjohnson.af.mil](https://www.seymourjohnson.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/662283/f-15e-strike-eagle/), the F-15E Strike Eagle is a dual-role fighter designed to perform air-to-air and air-to-ground missions. It carries a pilot and a weapon systems officer (WSO). The 4th Fighter Wing operates the Combat Air Forces' largest F-15E flying hour program.

Seymour Johnson is also home to the [916th Air Refueling Wing](https://www.seymourjohnson.af.mil/), an Air Force Reserve Command unit that flies the KC-46A Pegasus aerial refueling tanker. The 916th was the first Reserve-led location to receive the KC-46A Pegasus, accepting its first aircraft in June 2020. The two wings operate side by side on the same flightline.


## Where to Live When PCSing to Seymour Johnson AFB

Most families who move to Seymour Johnson either live in on-post housing or settle in one of the surrounding communities. Your choice usually comes down to commute time, schools, budget, and the feel you want. Ask your sponsor and the housing office about current availability and wait times early in your process.

### Goldsboro

Goldsboro is the host city and the closest off-base option, with a commute of about 10 minutes to the main gate. Many families choose Goldsboro for the short drive, a mix of established neighborhoods and newer subdivisions, and the convenience of having services, restaurants, and stores close to home.

### Pikeville

Pikeville is a small town about 15 minutes from the base. It sits northwest of Goldsboro and has a quiet, rural feel. Families who want a little space without a long commute often look here.

### La Grange

La Grange is roughly 20 minutes east of the installation, in Lenoir County. It offers a slower pace and a close-knit community, and home prices tend to reflect the more rural setting.

### Mount Olive

Mount Olive is about 25 minutes to the south, in Wayne and Duplin counties. It is a college town and has some retail and dining options, and the drive to the base is still manageable.

### Kinston

Kinston sits about 30 minutes east of Seymour Johnson, in Lenoir County. It is one of the larger cities in the region, with more housing inventory, restaurants, and local events. The drive is longer, but some families find the added amenities worth it.

### Smithfield

Smithfield is roughly 35 minutes northwest, in Johnston County. It puts you closer to the Raleigh metro and its job opportunities for military spouses, at the cost of a longer daily commute to the gate.

## Seymour Johnson AFB BAH and the Cost of Living

If you live off base, the military helps cover your housing through BAH, which stands for Basic Allowance for Housing. BAH is a monthly, tax-free payment. The amount depends on three things: your rank, whether you have dependents, and your duty station's Military Housing Area (MHA). An MHA is the geographic zone the Department of Defense uses to set local housing pay.

Because rates change each year and vary by rank and dependent status, we do not publish a rate table here. To find your exact figure, use the official DoD BAH calculator at [travel.dod.mil BAH Rate Lookup](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/). Enter your rank and ZIP code to see the current rate. Always confirm your rate before signing a lease or making an offer.

For a sense of buying costs, the typical home value in Goldsboro was $198,171 in May 2026, according to [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/45473/goldsboro-nc/). That is a typical value, not a median sale price. Goldsboro home values were up 1.6 percent over the prior year as of that date. As of the week of June 18, 2026, [Freddie Mac](https://www.freddiemac.com/pmms) reported the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate at 6.47 percent. Whether buying or renting makes more sense depends on how long you expect to be stationed here, so run the numbers for your own situation.

## Schools Near Seymour Johnson AFB

The schools your children attend depend on where you live. [Wayne County Public Schools](https://www.waynecountyschools.org/) serves families living in Goldsboro and most of the surrounding county. The district serves approximately 17,355 students across 33 schools, according to data from the [National Center for Education Statistics](https://nces.ed.gov/ccd/districtsearch/district_detail.asp?ID2=3704880). Families settling in Kinston fall under the Lenoir County Public Schools district, while those choosing Smithfield are in Johnston County Schools.

School attendance zones and boundaries can shift, so confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit to a neighborhood.

## Healthcare: 4th Medical Group

On-base medical care at Seymour Johnson is provided by the [4th Medical Group](https://seymourjohnson.tricare.mil/), also known as the Thomas Koritz Clinic and Paul Kiecker Dental Clinic. According to the 4th Medical Group's official site, the group serves a prime service area population of more than 35,000 beneficiaries. It provides dental and outpatient primary and specialty care through 310 military, civilian, and contractor personnel.

Services include family health, flight medicine, preventive care, mental and behavioral health, physical therapy, pediatrics, women's health, pharmacy, and dental care.

Your military health coverage runs through TRICARE (the health care program for service members and their families). Set up your TRICARE plan early in your PCS, update your information in DEERS (the Defense Enrollment Eligibility Reporting System), and request any referrals before you arrive so there is no gap in care. You can review options and contact information on the [4th Medical Group website](https://seymourjohnson.tricare.mil/).

## Climate and Things to Do

Goldsboro has a humid subtropical climate, the dominant pattern for eastern North Carolina. According to the [National Weather Service in Raleigh](https://www.weather.gov/rah/), the region sees warm, humid summers with afternoon highs regularly in the upper 80s and low 90s, mild springs and falls, and winters that are cool but rarely severe. Rainfall is spread fairly evenly through the year, and the area can see tropical weather impacts during Atlantic hurricane season, which runs from June through November. The base publishes hurricane readiness information on its official site.

For outdoor recreation, [Cliffs of the Neuse State Park](https://www.ncparks.gov/state-parks/cliffs-neuse-state-park), located about 15 miles southeast of Goldsboro in Wayne County, is one of the region's standout natural sites. According to NC State Parks, the park sits on 90-foot bluffs overlooking the Neuse River, where layers of sand, clay, seashells, and shale create a striking face along the cliff. The park includes longleaf pine restoration areas, an 11-acre lake with boat rentals and swimming, camping, and paddling on the Neuse River.

Downtown Goldsboro has a historic center with local shops and restaurants. Raleigh is about an hour northwest by car and puts you within reach of big-city dining, museums, sports, and the Research Triangle's amenities. For families who like the outdoors, the coastal plain of eastern North Carolina offers fishing, kayaking, and easy drives to the Outer Banks beaches.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What MHA is Seymour Johnson AFB in for BAH?

Seymour Johnson AFB falls within its own Military Housing Area for BAH purposes. Your rate depends on your rank and whether you have dependents. Look up your exact figure with the official [DoD BAH calculator](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/) using your rank and ZIP code.

### Where do military families live near Seymour Johnson AFB?

Common choices include Goldsboro, which is closest to the main gate; Pikeville and La Grange for a quieter rural feel; Mount Olive and Kinston for more inventory and amenities; and Smithfield for proximity to the Raleigh metro. On-base housing is also an option — ask your sponsor and the housing office about current availability and wait times.

### Which school district will my children attend near Seymour Johnson?

Most families living in Goldsboro and the surrounding Wayne County area fall under Wayne County Public Schools. Families in Kinston are served by Lenoir County Public Schools, and those in Smithfield fall under Johnston County Schools. Confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit.

### Where do I get healthcare at Seymour Johnson AFB?

On-base care is centered at the 4th Medical Group (Thomas Koritz Clinic), which provides primary care, specialty services, dental care, mental health support, pharmacy, and more. Set up your TRICARE plan, update DEERS, and arrange referrals before you arrive. Visit [seymourjohnson.tricare.mil](https://seymourjohnson.tricare.mil/) for clinic details and contact information.

### What is the weather like in Goldsboro?

Goldsboro has a humid subtropical climate with warm, humid summers (upper 80s and low 90s), mild spring and fall seasons, and cool but rarely harsh winters. The region can see tropical weather effects during Atlantic hurricane season (June through November). The [National Weather Service in Raleigh](https://www.weather.gov/rah/) tracks local climate data and forecasts.

### What aircraft does the 4th Fighter Wing fly?

The 4th Fighter Wing flies the F-15E Strike Eagle, a dual-role fighter designed for both air-to-air and air-to-ground missions. It carries a crew of two: a pilot and a weapon systems officer. The 916th Air Refueling Wing, an Air Force Reserve unit also based at Seymour Johnson, flies the KC-46A Pegasus aerial refueling tanker.
