Alabama is home to over **100,000 active-duty military personnel and their families**. The military community in Alabama is **diverse and close-knit**, bringing together service members from across the country and around the world.

The state hosts **five major military installations**, each playing a crucial role in **national defense, aviation training, logistics, and missile development**.

## **List of Military Bases in Alabama**

- **Anniston Army Depot**
- **Fort Rucker (Formerly Fort Novosel)**
- **Maxwell Air Force Base & Gunter Annex**
- **Redstone Arsenal**
- **USCG Sector Mobile**

## **1. Anniston Army Depot**

📍 **Location:** Anniston, AL
🎯 **Mission:** Vehicle storage, maintenance, and chemical weapons disposal

Established in **1941**, Anniston Army Depot was originally built as a **storage depot for ammunition and supplies**. Over time, it evolved into the **Army’s largest depot**, specializing in:

- **Repair and overhaul of tracked and wheeled combat vehicles**
- **Storage and disposal of chemical weapons** (Anniston Chemical Activity)
- **Maintenance of military equipment and logistics support**

The depot employs over **3,800 personnel**, making it a significant contributor to **local tax revenue and economic growth**.

[Visit Anniston Army Depot](https://www.anad.army.mil/)

## **2. Fort Rucker (Formerly Fort Novosel)**

📍 **Location:** Dale County, AL
🎯 **Mission:** **Primary flight training center for Army aviators**

Spanning **63,000 acres**, **Fort Rucker** is one of the **largest military installations in the U.S.** and is home to:

- **United States Army Aviation Center of Excellence (USAACE)** – Trains all **Army helicopter pilots**
- **Advanced aviation schools** specializing in **attack and reconnaissance helicopters**
- **Over 20,000 personnel, including soldiers, civilians, and contractors**

## **3. Maxwell Air Force Base & Gunter Annex**

📍 **Location:** Montgomery, AL
🎯 **Mission:** **Air Force education, research, and logistics**

Originally established in **1910** as the **Wright Flying School**, Maxwell AFB became a key **training facility for Army Air Corps pilots** before evolving into a **center for Air Force education and research**.

### **Key Facilities:**

- **Air University** – The **Air Force’s primary educational institution** for officers and enlisted personnel
- **Air Force Research Laboratory** – Focused on **aerospace innovation and advanced technology**
- **National Museum of the U.S. Air Force** – The **world’s largest air and space museum**
- **Gunter Annex** – A major **logistics and support facility**

[Visit Maxwell Air Force Base](https://www.maxwell.af.mil/)

## **4. Redstone Arsenal**

📍 **Location:** Huntsville, AL
🎯 **Mission:** **Missile development, space research, and defense systems**

Covering **38,000 acres**, Redstone Arsenal is home to over **40,000 employees**, including **military personnel, defense contractors, and NASA scientists**. Originally established in **1941** as a **chemical weapons storage facility**, it has since evolved into a **global hub for missile and space research**.

### **Key Units & Commands:**

- **U.S. Army Missile Command** – Develops **rockets and spacecraft**
- **Space and Missile Defense Command** – Oversees **missile defense forces**
- **NASA’s Marshall Space Flight Center** – Works on **space exploration and rocket testing**

Visit Redstone Arsenal

## **5. USCG Sector Mobile**

📍 **Location:** Mobile, AL
🎯 **Mission:** **Coast Guard operations for Alabama and Mississippi Gulf Coasts**

USCG Sector Mobile is responsible for **maritime safety, security, and environmental protection** along the **Alabama and Mississippi Gulf Coast**. It oversees operations at the **ports of Mobile and Pascagoula**, as well as key **inland waterways**.

### **Key Missions:**

- **Search and rescue operations**
- **Maritime law enforcement and border security**
- **Environmental protection and spill response**
- **Navigation safety and port security**
