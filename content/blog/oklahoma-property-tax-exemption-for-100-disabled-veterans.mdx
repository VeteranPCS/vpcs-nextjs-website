_From the VeteranPCS Team_

_Updated March 2026_

If you are a 100% permanent and total (P&T) service-connected disabled veteran living in Oklahoma, you may qualify for a full property tax exemption on your primary residence. This benefit can save you thousands of dollars each year. The process requires some paperwork and specific timing, but it is well worth the effort. Here is a clear, step-by-step walkthrough to help you claim what you have earned.

## Who Qualifies for the Oklahoma Property Tax Exemption

Oklahoma offers a real property (ad valorem) tax exemption on the homestead of veterans with a 100% permanent and total service-connected disability rating. According to the [Oklahoma Department of Veterans Affairs (ODVA)](https://oklahoma.gov/odva.html), eligible veterans must be Oklahoma residents with a current Oklahoma address. The exemption also extends to qualifying surviving spouses.

To be eligible, you must own and occupy the home as your primary residence. You must also have received (or be eligible for) the standard Oklahoma homestead exemption. The exemption covers the full fair cash value of your homestead, which means you could owe zero property taxes on your home.

## Step 1: Register with the Oklahoma Department of Veterans Affairs

Your first stop is the ODVA State Benefit Request page. Before requesting any state veteran benefits, you must complete the Oklahoma Veterans Registry. Visit the ODVA website at [oklahoma.gov/odva](https://oklahoma.gov/odva.html) and click the registration link to create your profile. Use your current Oklahoma address, since ODVA requires Oklahoma residency to process benefits.

Once registered, submit your State Benefit Request for the property tax exemption documentation. If ODVA holds your Power of Attorney for claims representation, they may be able to verify your eligibility faster.

## Step 2: Understand OTC Form 998

The [Oklahoma Tax Commission (OTC)](https://oklahoma.gov/tax.html) requires eligible veterans to complete OTC Form 998, titled "Application for 100% Disabled Veterans Real Property Tax Exemption." You will submit this form to the county assessor where your property is located. This is the key document that officially applies the exemption to your home.

## Step 3: Confirm You Meet the Timing Requirements

Form 998 includes a few important deadlines that can cause delays if missed. You must own and occupy the home as your primary residence on January 1 of the year you are applying. If you recently purchased the home, your deed or other ownership document must be recorded with the County Clerk no later than February 1. You must also be eligible for (or already have) the standard Oklahoma homestead exemption on the property.

In simple terms, this exemption is meant for your primary residence and the timing of your purchase matters. If you are PCSing to Oklahoma and plan to buy a home, keep these dates in mind as you plan your move. ## Step 4: Gather Your Documents

Before visiting your county assessor, prepare the following items. You will need a completed OTC Form 998. You will also need your VA benefits award letter or a document from the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs certifying your 100% service-related disability (or that you are receiving compensation at the 100% rate). Some counties may have additional requirements. For example, Tulsa County asks for a "Property Tax letter" with a raised seal and the property address listed.

Call your county assessor before you visit and ask whether they require an original or embossed letter, or if a copy is acceptable. Also ask whether you need proof that the homestead exemption is already on file.

## Step 5: Complete and Submit Form 998

Form 998 is short but requires careful attention. The form includes sections for your property and contact information, ownership and occupancy questions (confirming you owned and occupied the home on January 1 and that the deed will be recorded by February 1), qualification details (head of household status, honorable discharge, and certified 100% permanent disability), and your signature.

Bring the completed form to your county assessor's office along with your VA qualifying letter, photo ID, and any supporting ownership documentation. Ask for a date-stamped copy or receipt as proof of submission.

## Step 6: Follow Up After Filing

After submitting your application, stay in touch with the county assessor. If you filed close to a tax deadline, ask when the exemption will appear on your account. If you have a mortgage with an escrow account, ask how the exemption will be communicated for tax billing purposes. You are also required to notify the assessor if you no longer meet the qualifications, such as if you move or stop occupying the home as your primary residence.

## Key Takeaways

The Oklahoma property tax exemption for 100% disabled veterans is a valuable benefit that can eliminate your annual property tax bill entirely. Start by registering with ODVA, gather your VA documentation, complete OTC Form 998, and file it with your county assessor. Pay close attention to the January 1 and February 1 deadlines, and follow up to confirm your exemption is in place.
