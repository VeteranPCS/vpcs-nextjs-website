Living near Fort Riley offers a surprising mix of history, local charm, and weekend-worthy adventures. Whether you just received PCS orders to the area or have lived in the Flint Hills for years, there are countless nearby destinations that make your time off feel intentional and memorable. From meaningful historical sites to standout dining and outdoor escapes, this region truly has something for everyone.

## Abilene: History, Trains, and Comfort Food

A must-see stop near Fort Riley is Abilene, home to the [Eisenhower Presidential Library, Museum and Boyhood Home](https://www.eisenhowerlibrary.gov/). This beautifully designed campus offers an in-depth look at the life and leadership of President Dwight D. Eisenhower, including museum exhibits, his boyhood home, and peaceful walking paths across the grounds. It is a great destination for families and history buffs alike.

Just a short drive from the museum is the Brookville Hotel, famous for its family-style fried chicken dinners that have been a Kansas tradition for generations. While you are in Abilene, you can also experience the Abilene and Smoky Valley Railroad, which operates historic train rides departing from the Rock Island Depot and traveling through the Smoky Hill River Valley. The combination of history, rail adventures, and classic comfort food makes Abilene an easy place to spend a full and memorable day.

## Council Grove: Classic Kansas Dining

For a classic Kansas dining experience, a trip to Council Grove is well worth the drive. [Hays House 1857 Restaurant](https://www.hayshouse.com/) is one of the oldest continuously operating restaurants west of the Mississippi River. The steaks are incredible, the atmosphere is warm and historic, and the experience feels like stepping back in time. Council Grove itself is a charming small town with deep ties to the Santa Fe Trail, making it a great stop for those who enjoy exploring local history alongside a great meal.

## Riley, Kansas: Authentic German Cuisine at Schatzi's

Another local favorite worth the short drive is Schatzi's Schnitzel Haus, located in the small town of Riley just outside Manhattan. This family-owned restaurant is known for authentic German cuisine, including classic schnitzel, spaetzle, and hearty comfort dishes you do not find everywhere. It is a fun and memorable stop for a relaxed night out or a place to take visiting friends and family who want a taste of something different.

## Milford Lake: Outdoor Recreation Close to Home

Outdoor enthusiasts will love spending time at [Milford Lake](https://ksoutdoors.com/State-Parks/Locations/Milford), one of the largest lakes in Kansas and a favorite for boating, fishing, hiking, and relaxing by the water. Located just north of Junction City, Milford Lake is an easy day trip and a popular warm-weather escape. Whether you prefer casting a line from the shore or spending the afternoon on a boat, it is one of the best outdoor spots within reach of Fort Riley.
