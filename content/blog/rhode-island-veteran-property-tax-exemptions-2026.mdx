If you serve in the military or have served, Rhode Island gives qualifying veterans a property tax break, with larger relief for those who carry a service-connected disability. Rhode Island is home to Naval Station Newport, which trains officers at the Naval War College, Officer Candidate School, and the Surface Warfare Officers School, so the state draws many military families after a PCS, which is short for Permanent Change of Station, the official military move from one duty station to another. This guide explains the main rules in plain language and points you to the state's own sources. Tax law changes, and Rhode Island cities and towns set many of these amounts locally, so treat these figures as a starting point and confirm the current rules before you file.

## How Rhode Island Helps Disabled Veterans With Property Taxes

Rhode Island gives qualifying veterans a property tax exemption that removes a set amount of value from the home before taxes are figured, and it lets each city and town add to the state minimums. The U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, known as the VA, is the federal agency that rates service-connected disabilities, and that rating can unlock a larger exemption.

As the [Rhode Island Office of Veterans Services explains](https://vets.ri.gov/i-am-find-your-benefits/world-war-ii-korean-war-veteran/property-tax-exemptions), state law, found at Rhode Island General Laws section 44-3-4, gives property tax exemptions to veterans and to the surviving spouses of veterans, and the exact amounts vary by town.

### Who Qualifies and How Much

Rhode Island starts with a basic veterans' exemption for those who served during a recognized wartime or conflict period, then layers larger exemptions on top:

- A totally disabled veteran can receive an exemption of at least $10,000 of assessed value as a state minimum.
- A veteran who received a federal grant for specially adapted housing can receive an additional exemption of at least $10,000 of assessed value.
- Larger exemptions also apply for veterans the VA rates totally disabled, for those rated unemployable, for former prisoners of war, and for Gold Star parents under a related law.

Because these are minimums, your city or town may grant more. The state keeps a published list of what each community has adopted, so the dollars you receive depend on where you settle.

### Surviving Spouses

The benefit can carry to a surviving spouse under Rhode Island's rules. A surviving spouse who has not remarried may be able to continue a veteran's exemption, and a related law provides exemptions for Gold Star parents. Because the details depend on your situation and your town, confirm them with your local tax assessor before you rely on the benefit.

## How It Works With Your Local Tax Bill

Property tax in Rhode Island is set and collected by your city or town, not the state, and you claim every veteran exemption through your local tax assessor. Near Naval Station Newport, that office is the City of Newport tax assessor. You file your discharge papers, usually your DD-214, along with the town's application and any VA disability documentation the larger exemptions require.

Because each city and town sets its own tax rate and can adopt amounts above the state minimum, the same service record can save different dollars in different communities. The state maintains a [report of veteran and senior exemptions by city and town](https://municipalfinance.ri.gov/sites/g/files/xkgbur546/files/documents/data/exemptions/Veterans-Senior-Exemptions-Report.pdf) that is worth checking before you buy. ## Military Pay and Rhode Island State Income Tax

Rhode Island has a graduated state income tax, but it fully protects military retirement pay. As the [Rhode Island Division of Taxation confirms](https://tax.ri.gov/misc/military.php), military service pension income is exempt from Rhode Island income tax, with no age or income limit, through a modification on the state return. That makes Rhode Island a friendlier state for military retirees than it once was.

Active-duty pay is treated under the usual residency rules: a service member who keeps another home state and is stationed in Rhode Island on orders is generally not taxed by Rhode Island on military pay. VA disability compensation is not part of your federal income, so Rhode Island does not tax it either. Because rates and rules can change, confirm the current details with the Division of Taxation before you file.

## A Note for Military Spouses: MSRRA

If you are a military spouse, the Military Spouse Residency Relief Act, known as MSRRA, may matter to you. MSRRA is a federal law that lets a military spouse keep a home state for tax and voting purposes even after moving on military orders. You do not automatically become a Rhode Island resident just because your service member got orders here.

Under the related federal rules, a service member, the spouse, or both may choose the service member's home state, the spouse's home state, or the service member's permanent duty station for residency. Because that choice affects both states' taxes, confirm yours before you file. For the property tax exemptions in this guide, what usually matters most is that the qualifying veteran owns and lives in the home as the primary residence.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do disabled veterans pay property taxes in Rhode Island?

Often a reduced amount. Rhode Island removes a set amount of assessed value before taxes are figured, with a state minimum of at least $10,000 of value for a totally disabled veteran and an added $10,000 for those with a specially adapted housing grant. Cities and towns may grant more. Confirm your eligibility with your local tax assessor.

### How much is the Rhode Island veteran property tax exemption?

It varies by town. The state sets minimums, including at least $10,000 of assessed value for a totally disabled veteran and an added $10,000 for a specially adapted housing grant recipient, and each city or town may adopt larger amounts. Check the state's published exemption report for your community.

### Does Rhode Island tax military retirement pay?

No. Military service pension income is exempt from Rhode Island income tax, with no age or income limit, through a modification on the state return. VA disability compensation is not taxed either.

### How do I apply for the Rhode Island veteran exemption?

You apply through your local city or town tax assessor, with your discharge papers, usually your DD-214, and any VA disability documentation the larger exemptions require. Because deadlines and amounts vary, confirm them with the assessor.

### Can a surviving spouse claim the Rhode Island exemption?

In many cases, yes. A surviving spouse who has not remarried may continue a veteran's exemption, and a related law provides exemptions for Gold Star parents. Confirm the conditions with your local tax assessor.

Property tax rules and dollar amounts change from year to year, cities and towns set many of these amounts locally, and your situation may have details a general guide cannot cover.
