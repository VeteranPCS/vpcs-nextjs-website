Kentucky's strategic location along the Ohio River has made it a key military region throughout U.S. history. The state played a pivotal role in early conflicts, including the **French and Indian War**, and has remained a vital part of national defense ever since. Over time, Kentucky has been home to multiple military installations, but today, it hosts two major bases: **Fort Campbell and Fort Knox**.

### **Fort Campbell**

Fort Campbell was established during World War II in response to the growing need for expanded training facilities after the attack on **Pearl Harbor**. Named after **Brigadier General William Bowen Campbell**, the base was originally used to train **armored divisions**, including the 12th, 14th, and 20th Armored Divisions, which played key roles in the European theater of the war.

#### **Current Role and Units**

Today, Fort Campbell is known for housing some of the U.S. Army’s most elite units, including:

- **101st Airborne Division (Air Assault)** – Specializes in rapid air assault operations and large-scale combat.
- **5th Special Forces Group (Airborne)** – Focuses on unconventional warfare and special operations.
- **160th Special Operations Aviation Regiment (Airborne)** – Also known as the **Night Stalkers**, this elite aviation unit specializes in nighttime missions, supporting **Special Operations Forces** worldwide.

Fort Campbell is one of the most active bases in the country, continuously training and deploying troops to global hotspots. It remains a cornerstone of **rapid deployment and special operations training**.

For more information, visit the **Fort Campbell Website**.

### **Fort Knox**

Fort Knox was established in **1918** and named after **Major General Henry Knox**, the first U.S. Secretary of War. Initially, the base served as a **training ground for infantry divisions** preparing for deployment to Europe during **World War I**.

#### **Home of the U.S. Gold Bullion Depository**

In 1933, Fort Knox was selected as the site for the **U.S. Bullion Depository**, housing much of the nation’s gold reserves. The location was chosen due to its **central positioning**, **existing military infrastructure**, and **heavily secured terrain**. The depository remains one of the most **secure locations in the world**, known for its **reinforced vaults** and **layers of military protection**.

#### **Training and Operational Focus**

While Fort Knox is famous for its gold reserves, it is also an important military training facility. The base is home to:

- **U.S. Army Cadet Command**, overseeing the **ROTC program** that trains future Army officers.
- **U.S. Army Human Resources Command**, which manages **personnel operations for the entire Army**.
- **General George Patton Museum**, which preserves the history of the **U.S. Armored Corps** and honors the legacy of one of America’s most famous generals.

For more information, visit the **Fort Knox Website**.
