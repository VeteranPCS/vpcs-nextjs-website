If you have orders for a PCS to Maxwell Air Force Base, welcome to Montgomery, Alabama. PCS stands for Permanent Change of Station, the military term for moving from one duty station to another. Maxwell AFB is home to Air University, the Air Force's center for professional military education, making it one of the most academically distinctive assignments in the service. This 2026 guide covers where to live, how housing pay works, schools, healthcare, and daily life in the Montgomery area.

## What and Where Is Maxwell AFB?

Maxwell Air Force Base is located in Montgomery, Alabama, along the banks of the Alabama River, about two miles west of downtown. Gunter Annex, a satellite facility, sits about five miles to the east in Montgomery.

According to the [Air University About Us page](https://www.maxwell.af.mil/About-Us/), Air University — headquartered at Maxwell — is the Air Force's center for professional military education (PME). Its mission is to educate and develop Air, Space, and Cyberspace warrior leaders in support of the National Defense Strategy. The 42nd Air Base Wing serves as the host wing for the installation, providing base operations and support.

Air University's resident programs include the [Air War College](https://www.airuniversity.af.edu/AWC/), the senior PME school for colonels and lieutenant colonels; the [Air Command and Staff College](https://www.airuniversity.af.edu/ACSC/), the intermediate school that prepares majors for operational leadership; and [Squadron Officer School](https://www.airuniversity.af.edu/SOS/), the company-grade officer development program. Each year, hundreds of officers, allied nation representatives, and government civilians attend in residence at Maxwell.

The Wright Brothers established the first U.S. civilian flying school in Montgomery in 1910. The airfield was later named for 2nd Lt. William C. Maxwell in 1922, and the institution's tradition of airpower education has continued unbroken since.

## Where to Live When PCSing to Maxwell AFB

Most families who move to Maxwell choose between on-post housing and one of the surrounding communities. Your choice usually comes down to commute, schools, budget, and the feel you want. Ask your sponsor and the Maxwell housing office about current on-post availability and wait times.

### Montgomery

Montgomery is the city Maxwell AFB calls home, and many service members choose to live close to base. Neighborhoods range from established historic districts to newer developments on the east and south sides of town. A commute from most parts of Montgomery runs about 10 minutes. Living in the city puts you close to restaurants, parks, the riverfront, and the state capitol district.

### Prattville

Prattville sits about 20 minutes northwest of Maxwell in Autauga County. It is one of the most popular choices for Maxwell families, with newer subdivisions, strong schools with dedicated military-family support, and a quieter small-city feel. Nine Autauga County schools carry the Alabama Purple Star designation for military-connected students, according to the [Autauga County Board of Education](https://www.acboe.net/military).

### Millbrook

Millbrook is in Elmore County, roughly 20 minutes north of the base. It offers a mix of established neighborhoods and newer construction, with easy access to Interstate 65 and the Elmore County school system.

### Pike Road

Pike Road is a growing town southeast of Montgomery, about 25 minutes from Maxwell. Families here are drawn to newer homes and the Pike Road City School System, an independent district with a strong local reputation.

### Wetumpka

Wetumpka, the seat of Elmore County, sits about 25 minutes northeast of Maxwell along the Coosa River. It has a small-town feel, newer subdivisions, and a lower price point than some closer communities.

## Maxwell AFB BAH and the Cost of Living

If you live off post, the military helps cover your housing costs through BAH, which stands for Basic Allowance for Housing. BAH is a monthly, tax-free payment. The amount depends on three things: your rank, whether you have dependents, and your duty station's Military Housing Area, or MHA. An MHA is the geographic zone the Defense Department uses to set local housing pay. Maxwell AFB falls within the Montgomery, Alabama MHA.

Because rates change each year and vary by rank and dependent status, we do not publish a rate table here. To find your exact figure, use the official DoD BAH calculator at [travel.dod.mil](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/). Enter your rank and ZIP code to see the current rate. Always confirm your number before signing a lease or making an offer.

For a sense of buying costs in Montgomery, the typical home value in the city was about $136,716 as of mid-2026, according to [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/32931/montgomery-al/). That is a typical value, not a median sale price. Home values in outlying communities like Prattville and Millbrook generally run higher. As of the week of June 18, 2026, [Freddie Mac](https://www.freddiemac.com/pmms) reported the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate at 6.47 percent. Whether buying or renting makes more sense for your family depends on how long you expect to be stationed here and your personal financial picture.

## Schools Near Maxwell AFB

The schools your children attend depend on where you live.

[Montgomery Public Schools](https://www.mps.k12.al.us/) serves the city and has a dedicated Military Family Resources program.

Families in Prattville fall under the [Autauga County Board of Education](https://www.acboe.net/military). Nine Autauga County schools have earned the Alabama Purple Star designation for military-connected students, and the district partners directly with Maxwell and Gunter Annex, with Military Family Life Counselors at several campuses.

Families in Millbrook or Wetumpka are typically zoned to [Elmore County Schools](https://www.elmoreco.com/education/), which has a military-family open-enrollment policy. Pike Road residents are served by the independent Pike Road City School System.

School boundaries can shift, so always confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit.

## Healthcare: 42nd Medical Group

On-post medical care at Maxwell AFB is centered at the [42nd Medical Group](https://maxwell.tricare.mil/), which provides care for service members, families, and other eligible beneficiaries at Maxwell AFB and Gunter Annex.

According to the [42nd Medical Group About Us page](https://maxwell.tricare.mil/About-Us), the group includes two squadrons covering primary care, family health, pediatrics, behavioral health, dental, optometry, physical therapy, and pharmacy, among other services. The group's stated mission is "Ready Medics, Maximizing Wellness" and it is accredited by The Joint Commission.

Your military health coverage runs through TRICARE, the health care program for active-duty service members and their families. MTF stands for Military Treatment Facility; the 42nd Medical Group is the MTF for Maxwell. Set up your TRICARE plan early, update your information in DEERS (the Defense Enrollment Eligibility Reporting System), and request any referrals before you arrive so there is no gap in care.

## Climate and Things to Do

Montgomery has a humid subtropical climate, with hot, humid summers, mild winters, and mild springs and falls. The [National Weather Service Birmingham office](https://www.weather.gov/bmx/climo_mgmclimograph) maintains climate normals and a climograph for the Montgomery area. Expect summer highs in the low-to-mid 90s, occasional thunderstorms, and winters that rarely bring significant snow. If you are coming from a colder or drier climate, the summer heat and humidity will be the biggest adjustment.

The payoff for that climate is a long outdoor season and a city with deep history. Montgomery's [riverfront district along the Alabama River](https://alabama.travel/places-to-go/riverfront-park-2) offers Riverwalk Stadium (home of the Montgomery Biscuits, a Double-A baseball team), a splash pad, an amphitheater, and walkable parks. The broader downtown is anchored by the Alabama State Capitol and a walkable civil-rights history trail that includes the Dexter Avenue King Memorial Baptist Church and the National Memorial for Peace and Justice.

About 40 miles east of Montgomery, the [Tuskegee Airmen National Historic Site](https://www.nps.gov/tuai/index.htm) at Moton Field commemorates the Army Air Corps test program that trained the first African American military aviators during World War II. For an Air Force family assigned to Maxwell — the institution that carries that same airpower education mission forward — a visit to Tuskegee is well worth the drive.

Alabama state parks round out the outdoor options. Oak Mountain State Park near Birmingham and Cheaha State Park, the highest point in Alabama, are both within a few hours and offer camping, hiking, and swimming.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What MHA is Maxwell AFB in for BAH?

Maxwell AFB falls within the Montgomery, Alabama Military Housing Area for BAH purposes. Your rate depends on your rank and whether you have dependents. Look up your exact figure with the official [DoD BAH calculator](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/) using your rank and ZIP code.

### Where do military families live near Maxwell AFB?

The most common choices are neighborhoods in Montgomery itself (10-minute commute), Prattville in Autauga County (20 minutes), and Millbrook in Elmore County (20 minutes). Pike Road and Wetumpka are also popular, each about 25 minutes from the installation. On-post housing is available; ask the housing office about current availability and wait times.

### What schools serve families near Maxwell AFB?

It depends on your address. Montgomery Public Schools serves the city. Autauga County Schools (including Purple Star-designated campuses) serves Prattville. Elmore County Schools covers Millbrook and Wetumpka. The Pike Road City School System serves Pike Road. Confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you sign a lease or purchase a home.

### Where do I get healthcare at Maxwell AFB?

On-post care is centered at the [42nd Medical Group](https://maxwell.tricare.mil/) at Maxwell AFB, the MTF (Military Treatment Facility) for the installation. Set up your TRICARE plan early, update DEERS, and arrange any referrals before you arrive so your care has no gap.

### What is the weather like in Montgomery?

Montgomery has a humid subtropical climate — hot, humid summers with highs typically in the low-to-mid 90s, mild winters, and a long spring and fall. The [National Weather Service Birmingham office](https://www.weather.gov/bmx/climo_mgmclimograph) maintains detailed climate data for the area. Thunderstorms are common in spring and summer.

### What makes Maxwell AFB different from most Air Force bases?

Maxwell is the home of Air University, the Air Force's center for professional military education. Most people assigned here are students in PME programs (Air War College, Air Command and Staff College, Squadron Officer School, and others) or faculty and support staff. Student tours are typically one to two years, which shapes the community feel and the rental market.
