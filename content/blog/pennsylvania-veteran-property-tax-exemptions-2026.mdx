If you serve in the military or have served, a Pennsylvania veteran property tax exemption can erase the real estate tax bill on your home, though the benefit comes with rules worth understanding. Pennsylvania is home to installations like Carlisle Barracks and Fort Indiantown Gap, and it draws many military families after a PCS, which is short for Permanent Change of Station, the official military move from one duty station to another. This guide walks through the state's disabled veterans real estate tax exemption in plain language and points you to Pennsylvania's own sources. Tax law changes, so treat this as a starting point and confirm the current rules before you apply.

## The Pennsylvania Disabled Veterans Real Estate Tax Exemption

Pennsylvania offers a strong benefit: a full exemption from real estate tax on a qualifying veteran's principal residence. Unlike some states, though, Pennsylvania does not hand it to every 100 percent disabled veteran automatically. It is a need-based program, so eligibility depends on your disability, your service, and your finances.

According to the [Pennsylvania Department of Military and Veterans Affairs](https://www.dmva.pa.gov/veteransaffairs/Pages/Programs%20and%20Services/Real-Estate-Tax-Exemption.aspx), the exemption is available to an honorably discharged veteran who is a resident of the Commonwealth, has a financial need, and meets the disability test. The veteran must have a 100 percent permanent service-connected disability, or be rated totally disabled because of individual unemployability, blindness, paraplegia, or the loss of two or more limbs. Individual unemployability, often shortened to IU, means the VA pays you at the 100 percent level because service-connected conditions keep you from holding steady work. The VA is the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, the federal agency that rates service-connected disabilities. The veteran must also have served during a wartime period as recognized by the VA.

### The Financial Need Test

Because the program is need-based, your income matters. The state's [Department of Military and Veterans Affairs reports](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dmva/pennsylvania-veterans/forms-and-pubs/together-we-serve/together-we-serve---january-2025/together-we-serve-january-2025-full-length-articles) that the State Veterans' Commission raised the presumptive need income level to $114,637 as of January 1, 2025. A veteran with annual income at or below that level is presumed to have financial need and does not have to prove it. A veteran whose income is higher can still qualify if monthly household expenses exceed monthly income, after a cost-of-living allowance. The Commission adjusts this income level over time, so confirm the current figure before you apply.

One recent change helps many families. A state law that took effect in 2024 excludes all veterans benefits, including VA disability compensation, from the income used in the need assessment. Veterans and surviving spouses who were turned down before because of income are encouraged to apply again under the new rules.

### Surviving Spouses

The exemption can carry over to a surviving spouse. The unremarried surviving spouse of a qualifying 100 percent permanently disabled wartime veteran may receive the exemption on the home, subject to the same need rules, which the 2024 law also eased by excluding veterans benefits from the income count.

## How It Works With Local Property Taxes

In Pennsylvania, real estate tax is levied locally by counties, municipalities, and school districts. The state sets the rules for the disabled veterans exemption, but the benefit applies to those local taxes on your principal home. That is why the dollar value of a full exemption is larger in a high-tax district than in a low-tax one.

Pennsylvania does not offer a broad statewide homestead exemption that every owner can claim, though many school districts provide a separate homestead or farmstead reduction funded by gaming revenue. The disabled veterans exemption is the major property tax break tied to military service. ## Military Pay and Pennsylvania State Income Tax

Pennsylvania has a flat state income tax, but it treats military income kindly. The state does not tax retirement income that meets its rules, and military pension income is not taxable in Pennsylvania.

Active-duty pay also gets a break. As the [Pennsylvania Department of Revenue explains on its military pay page](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/revenue/resources/tax-types-and-information/personal-income-tax/military-pay), military pay earned by a Pennsylvania resident while on federal active duty or active duty for training outside Pennsylvania is not taxable. Pay for federal active duty performed inside Pennsylvania is generally taxable, unless it is for active state duty during an emergency. VA disability compensation is not part of your federal income, so Pennsylvania does not tax it either. Keep copies of your orders, since the Department may ask for proof that the pay was earned on federal active duty outside the state.

## A Note for Military Spouses: MSRRA

If you are a military spouse, the Military Spouse Residency Relief Act, known as MSRRA, may matter to you. MSRRA is a federal law that lets a military spouse keep a home state for tax and voting purposes even after moving on military orders. You do not automatically become a Pennsylvania resident just because your service member got orders here.

Under the related federal rules, a service member, their spouse, or both may choose the service member's home state, the spouse's home state, or the service member's permanent duty station for residency. Because that choice affects both states' taxes, confirm yours before you file. For the property tax exemption in this guide, what usually matters most is whether the home is the veteran's principal residence.

## How to Apply and Where to Verify

You do not apply for the disabled veterans real estate tax exemption through the state tax office. Instead, you start with your County Director of Veterans Affairs, who helps you file with the State Veterans' Commission. You will provide your VA paperwork showing the 100 percent disability rating or the qualifying condition, proof of wartime service and honorable discharge, and information about the home and your finances.

Because the income level and rules can change, confirm the current details with your county office and the [Pennsylvania Department of Military and Veterans Affairs](https://www.dmva.pa.gov/veteransaffairs/Pages/Programs%20and%20Services/Real-Estate-Tax-Exemption.aspx) before you apply. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do 100 percent disabled veterans pay property taxes in Pennsylvania?

A qualifying veteran can receive a full exemption from real estate tax on a principal residence, which can bring that bill to zero. It is need-based, though, so you must meet the income and financial-need rules in addition to the 100 percent disability and wartime-service tests. Confirm your eligibility with your County Director of Veterans Affairs.

### What is the income limit for the Pennsylvania veterans exemption?

The State Veterans' Commission set the presumptive need income level at $114,637 as of January 1, 2025. Income at or below that level is presumed to show need; a higher income can still qualify if monthly expenses exceed monthly income. A 2024 law also excludes veterans benefits from the income count. The figure is adjusted over time, so verify the current number.

### Does Pennsylvania tax military retirement pay?

No. Pennsylvania does not tax military pension income. Active-duty pay earned by a resident while on federal active duty outside Pennsylvania is also not taxable, though pay for federal active duty inside the state is generally taxable.

### Can a surviving spouse keep the exemption?

Yes, in many cases. The unremarried surviving spouse of a qualifying 100 percent permanently disabled wartime veteran may receive the exemption, subject to the same need rules, which the 2024 law eased by excluding veterans benefits from the income count.

### Where do I apply for the Pennsylvania veterans exemption?

You apply through your County Director of Veterans Affairs, who helps you file with the State Veterans' Commission. Bring your VA disability paperwork, proof of wartime service and honorable discharge, and details about the home and your finances.

Property tax rules and dollar amounts change from year to year, and your situation may have details a general guide cannot cover. Please consult a tax professional or attorney before making decisions based on this exemption, and verify the current rules and amounts with your County Director of Veterans Affairs and the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania before you apply.
