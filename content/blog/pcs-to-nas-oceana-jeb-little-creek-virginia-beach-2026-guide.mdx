If you have orders to Naval Air Station Oceana or Joint Expeditionary Base Little Creek-Fort Story, you are headed to one of the most military-dense regions in the country. PCS stands for Permanent Change of Station, the military term for moving from one duty station to another. Virginia Beach sits at the heart of Hampton Roads, and this 2026 guide is built for the sailors, officers, and families arriving here to live and work. We cover where to live, how housing pay works, schools, healthcare, and daily life on the Virginia coast.

## What and Where Are NAS Oceana and JEB Little Creek-Fort Story?

Virginia Beach is home to two major Navy installations that serve very different missions. Both are managed under [Commander, Navy Region Mid-Atlantic](https://cnrma.cnic.navy.mil/).

**Naval Air Station Oceana** is the Navy's East Coast Master Jet Base, according to the [NAS Oceana history page](https://cnrma.cnic.navy.mil/Installations/NAS-Oceana/About/History/) on cnic.navy.mil. The station sits on 5,916 acres inside Virginia Beach and is home to F/A-18E/F Super Hornet strike fighter squadrons. According to the [NAS Oceana aircraft page](https://cnrma.cnic.navy.mil/Installations/NAS-Oceana/About/), the F/A-18E/F Super Hornet is capable across the full mission spectrum — air superiority, fighter escort, aerial refueling, close air support, and day and night precision strike. The installation is also home to Fleet Logistics Support Squadron VR-56, the "Globemasters," flying the C-40A Clipper. NAS Oceana is the largest employer in Virginia Beach, with more than 14,600 military personnel and 2,000 civilians.

**Joint Expeditionary Base Little Creek-Fort Story** (JEB Little Creek-Fort Story) was established October 1, 2009, as the first joint base in Hampton Roads. According to the [JEB Little Creek-Fort Story history page](https://cnrma.cnic.navy.mil/Installations/JEB-Little-Creek-Fort-Story/About/History/) on cnic.navy.mil, it is the country's premier installation for housing and training the nation's Expeditionary Forces. One command with two properties — the former Naval Amphibious Base Little Creek and the former Army post of Fort Story near Cape Henry — it supports the Navy's amphibious and expeditionary mission.

## Where to Live When PCSing to Virginia Beach

Most military families choose off-installation housing in one of the surrounding cities. Your choice usually comes down to commute, schools, budget, and neighborhood feel. Both bases have on-base housing — ask your sponsor about availability and wait times.

The chart below shows approximate one-way drive times to NAS Oceana. JEB Little Creek-Fort Story sits near the Virginia Beach–Norfolk border on Shore Drive; drive times from these communities to that base will differ.

### Virginia Beach

Virginia Beach is the most common choice for families assigned to either installation. The city is large — neighborhoods range from the oceanfront resort strip to quiet inland areas near the Princess Anne corridor. Families near NAS Oceana often settle in Kempsville, Princess Anne, or Landstown for short commutes. Note that F/A-18 Super Hornets are loud; neighborhoods under the flight path hear jet noise throughout the day. Review the base's [Air Installation Compatible Use Zone](https://cnrma.cnic.navy.mil/Installations/NAS-Oceana/Operations-and-Management/Air-Operations/Air-Installation-Compatible-Use-Zone/) map before signing a lease or making an offer.

### Chesapeake

Chesapeake sits just south of Virginia Beach and draws families who want newer construction and larger lots at slightly lower prices. Commutes to NAS Oceana run about 20 minutes from the Great Bridge and Greenbrier areas. Chesapeake has its own city school system.

### Norfolk

Norfolk is the military hub of Hampton Roads and home to Naval Station Norfolk. For families at JEB Little Creek, neighborhoods like Ocean View and Larchmont put you close to the base. Commutes to NAS Oceana from most of Norfolk run about 20 minutes. Housing ranges from established older neighborhoods to newer waterfront construction.

### Suffolk

Suffolk is a growing city west of Chesapeake for families who want more land at a lower price. Commutes to NAS Oceana stretch to about 35 minutes depending on traffic on Route 58 and Interstate 64.

## BAH and the Cost of Living in Virginia Beach

If you live off base, the military helps cover your housing through BAH, which stands for Basic Allowance for Housing. BAH is a monthly, tax-free payment. The amount depends on three things: your rank, whether you have dependents, and your duty station's Military Housing Area, or MHA. An MHA is the geographic zone the Defense Department uses to set local housing pay. Both NAS Oceana and JEB Little Creek-Fort Story fall within the Virginia Beach-Norfolk-Newport News MHA, which covers the broader Hampton Roads region.

Because rates change each year and vary by rank and dependent status, we do not publish a rate table here. To find your exact figure, use the official DoD calculator: [travel.dod.mil BAH Rate Lookup](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/). Enter your rank and ZIP code to see the current rate.

For a sense of buying costs, the typical home value in Virginia Beach City was about $429,580 as of May 2026, according to [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/3286/virginia-beach-city-va/). That is a typical value, not a median sale price. As of the week of June 18, 2026, [Freddie Mac](https://www.freddiemac.com/pmms) reported the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate at 6.47 percent. Whether buying or renting makes more sense depends on how long you expect to be stationed here, so run the numbers for your own situation.

## Schools Near NAS Oceana and JEB Little Creek

The schools your children attend depend on which city you live in.

**Virginia Beach City Public Schools** is the largest district in the area. According to the [Virginia Beach City Public Schools military families page](https://www.vbschools.com/families/military), approximately 25 percent of the student population is active-duty military-dependent. The district serves grades pre-kindergarten through 12 across 88 schools, per [NCES data](https://nces.ed.gov/ccd/districtsearch/district_detail.asp?Search=2&details=1&ID2=5103840&DistrictID=5103840), and participates in the Interstate Compact on Educational Opportunity for Military Children to ease PCS transitions. **Chesapeake Public Schools** serves Chesapeake, and **Norfolk Public Schools** serves Norfolk. Boundaries can shift, so confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit.

## Healthcare: Naval Medical Center Portsmouth and Branch Clinics

The regional Military Treatment Facility, or MTF, for Hampton Roads is [Naval Medical Center Portsmouth](https://portsmouth.tricare.mil/) in Portsmouth, Virginia, which provides inpatient and specialty care for eligible beneficiaries across the region.

For day-to-day care at NAS Oceana, the [Oceana Branch Health Clinic](https://portsmouth.tricare.mil/Clinics/Oceana-BHC) is located at 1550 Tomcat Blvd., Suite 150, inside Gate 1. It provides primary care, aviation medicine, pharmacy, physical therapy, radiology, and optometry. For families at JEB Little Creek-Fort Story, the [Boone Branch Health Clinic](https://portsmouth.tricare.mil/Clinics/Boone-BHC) offers primary care, pharmacy, laboratory, mental health, physical therapy, and immunizations. Both clinics operate under Naval Medical Center Portsmouth.

Your military health coverage runs through TRICARE, the health care program for service members and their families. Update your information in DEERS, choose or transfer your plan, and coordinate referrals before you arrive so care continues without a gap.

## Climate and Things to Do

Virginia Beach has a humid coastal climate. The [National Weather Service office in Wakefield](https://www.weather.gov/akq/) covers Hampton Roads — expect hot and humid summers, mild and occasionally rainy winters, and pleasant springs and falls. The Atlantic and Chesapeake Bay moderate temperatures year-round, but summer humidity is real. Hurricanes and tropical storms are a seasonal consideration; installations have well-established evacuation procedures.

The outdoor options are excellent. [First Landing State Park](https://www.dcr.virginia.gov/state-parks/first-landing) is Virginia's most-visited state park, with 20 miles of trails, 1.5 miles of Chesapeake Bay beach, bald cypress swamps, and maritime forest. Built in part by an all African-American Civilian Conservation Corps crew from 1933 to 1940 and listed in the National Register of Historic Places, it offers cabins, campgrounds, and bike rentals. At the southern end of the city, [Back Bay National Wildlife Refuge](https://www.fws.gov/refuge/back-bay), managed by the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service, provides about seven miles of barrier island trail and beach for hiking, kayaking, and wildlife watching — over 300 bird species documented. The Virginia Beach boardwalk runs three miles along the Atlantic oceanfront and is free to use any time.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What MHA is NAS Oceana in for BAH purposes?

Both NAS Oceana and JEB Little Creek-Fort Story fall within the Virginia Beach-Norfolk-Newport News Military Housing Area for BAH. Your rate depends on your rank and whether you have dependents. Look up your exact figure with the official [DoD BAH calculator](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/) using your rank and ZIP code.

### Where do military families live near NAS Oceana and JEB Little Creek?

Common choices include Virginia Beach for the shortest commute to both bases, Chesapeake for more space and newer construction, Norfolk for proximity to the JEB Little Creek side and the broader naval community, and Suffolk for those who want more land at a lower price point with a longer drive.

### Will I hear jet noise near NAS Oceana?

Yes, if you live under or near the flight path. NAS Oceana operates F/A-18 Super Hornet squadrons, and the jets are loud. The base publishes an [Air Installation Compatible Use Zone map](https://cnrma.cnic.navy.mil/Installations/NAS-Oceana/Operations-and-Management/Air-Operations/Air-Installation-Compatible-Use-Zone/) that shows which neighborhoods are most affected. Review it before choosing a home.

### Which school district serves Virginia Beach military families?

Most families living in Virginia Beach are served by [Virginia Beach City Public Schools](https://www.vbschools.com/families/military), which has a dedicated military family support program. About 25 percent of students are military-connected. Families in Chesapeake fall under Chesapeake Public Schools, and families in Norfolk fall under Norfolk Public Schools. Confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit.

### Where do I get healthcare at NAS Oceana or JEB Little Creek?

Day-to-day primary care at NAS Oceana is through the [Oceana Branch Health Clinic](https://portsmouth.tricare.mil/Clinics/Oceana-BHC), and at JEB Little Creek through the [Boone Branch Health Clinic](https://portsmouth.tricare.mil/Clinics/Boone-BHC). Both operate under [Naval Medical Center Portsmouth](https://portsmouth.tricare.mil/), the regional MTF. Set up your TRICARE plan early and update DEERS so your care continues without a gap after you check in.

### What is the weather like in Virginia Beach?

Virginia Beach has a mild coastal climate with hot, humid summers and mild winters. The [National Weather Service Wakefield office](https://www.weather.gov/akq/) covers Hampton Roads. The Atlantic and Chesapeake Bay moderate temperatures year-round. Hurricanes are a seasonal consideration; your installation will brief you on evacuation procedures.
