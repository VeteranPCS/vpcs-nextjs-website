Pennsylvania has a long and proud military history. The state is home to **seven major active installations**, supporting the **Army, Navy, and Air Force**, plus a major Defense Logistics Agency site and reserve and Guard units that train alongside them. Whether you are **PCSing to Pennsylvania** (Permanent Change of Station, the military term for an official move to a new duty station), thinking about a move, or just curious about military history, this guide gives you a clear overview of the **key military bases** in the state.

## Overview of Military Installations in Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania military bases cover a wide range of missions. They include **strategic leader education, depot-level repair of electronics and missile defense systems, naval supply and ship engineering, airlift, special operations, and large-scale supply distribution**. The state trains senior leaders, fixes the gear that keeps the joint force ready, and keeps thousands of military families moving in and out each year on PCS orders.

### Active Military Bases in Pennsylvania

- **Carlisle Barracks** (home of the U.S. Army War College)
- **Tobyhanna Army Depot**
- **Letterkenny Army Depot**
- **Naval Support Activity Philadelphia**
- **Pittsburgh International Airport Air Reserve Station** (911th Airlift Wing)
- **193rd Special Operations Wing** (Pennsylvania Air National Guard)
- **DLA Distribution Susquehanna** (New Cumberland)

## Army Bases in Pennsylvania

### Carlisle Barracks

Carlisle Barracks, in **Carlisle** near Harrisburg, was established in **1757** and is the **second oldest Army post** in the United States. Since **1951** it has been the home of the **U.S. Army War College**, the Army's highest educational institution. The post's job is to support the War College as it educates senior military, civilian, and international leaders for the responsibilities of strategic leadership.

**Key Missions:**

- Strategic-level leader education at the U.S. Army War College
- Base and life support for students and faculty
- Preparing military, civilian, and allied leaders for joint and multinational service

Learn more from the official [Carlisle Barracks mission and vision page](https://home.army.mil/carlisle/about/mission) and the [Carlisle Barracks history page](https://home.army.mil/carlisle/about/history).

### Tobyhanna Army Depot

Tobyhanna Army Depot sits in the **Pocono Mountains** of northeastern Pennsylvania, near Scranton. It is the **Department of Defense's largest center** for the repair, overhaul, and fabrication of electronics systems, from tactical field radios to ground terminals for satellite communications. About **3,700 people** work there, and its missions support all branches of the armed forces.

**Key Missions:**

- Repair and overhaul of military electronics and communications systems
- Fabrication, systems integration, and post-production software support
- Full-spectrum logistics support for the joint warfighter

Read more on the [Tobyhanna Army Depot website](https://www.tobyhanna.army.mil/) and in the Army's [full-spectrum support overview](https://www.army.mil/article/280374/21st_century_maintenance_tobyhanna_army_depot_provides_full_spectrum_support_to_the_joint_warfighter).

### Letterkenny Army Depot

Letterkenny Army Depot, in **Chambersburg**, is the Department of Defense's premier center for **air and missile defense** ground support equipment and **Patriot missile recertification**. More than **1,500 people** direct repair and modernization work there on systems such as the Avenger and Patriot, supporting U.S. and allied forces.

**Key Missions:**

- Overhaul and repair of air and missile defense systems
- Patriot missile recertification and precision fires support
- Modernization of tactical systems for multi-domain operations

See the official [Letterkenny Army Depot website](https://www.letterkenny.army.mil/) and the [Letterkenny About page](https://www.letterkenny.army.mil/About/).

## Navy Bases in Pennsylvania

### Naval Support Activity Philadelphia

Naval Support Activity Philadelphia, in **South Philadelphia**, is the Navy's main footprint in Pennsylvania. It is home to **NAVSUP Weapon Systems Support (WSS) Philadelphia**, which provides program and supply support for aircraft such as the **F/A-18** and **V-22** along with engines and avionics. It is also home to the **Naval Surface Warfare Center (NSWC) Philadelphia Division**, which engineers and tests shipboard machinery, propulsion, and electric power systems for the fleet.

**Key Missions:**

- Supply and weapon systems support for Navy and Marine Corps aviation
- Engineering and testing of shipboard machinery and propulsion systems
- Cyber-secure solutions and fleet readiness support

Learn more from the [NAVSUP WSS Philadelphia welcome page](https://www.navsup.navy.mil/NAVSUP-Enterprise/NAVSUP-Weapon-Systems-Support/Welcome-Philadelphia/) and the [NSWC Philadelphia Division page](https://www.navsea.navy.mil/Home/Warfare-Centers/NSWC-Philadelphia/).

## Air Force Bases in Pennsylvania

### Pittsburgh International Airport Air Reserve Station

Pittsburgh International Airport Air Reserve Station, in **western Pennsylvania**, is home to the **911th Airlift Wing**, an Air Force Reserve unit. The wing flies the **C-17 Globemaster III** after converting from the smaller C-130 cargo plane. It provides airlift, aeromedical evacuation, and combat support for missions around the world.

**Key Missions:**

- C-17 airlift operations and aircrew training
- Aeromedical evacuation
- Agile combat support for the Air Force Reserve

See the official [911th Airlift Wing fact sheet](https://www.afrc.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/4404947/911th-airlift-wing/) and the [Pittsburgh Air Reserve Station website](https://www.pittsburgh.afrc.af.mil/).

### 193rd Special Operations Wing

The 193rd Special Operations Wing is a **Pennsylvania Air National Guard** unit based at **Harrisburg International Airport** in Middletown, with training at nearby **Fort Indiantown Gap**. It became the first Air National Guard unit to fly the **MC-130J Commando II** as part of its mission with Air Force Special Operations Command. The wing provides trained combat and support forces anytime, anywhere.

**Key Missions:**

- MC-130J special operations flying mission
- Combat and support forces for Air Force Special Operations Command
- Special operations training at Fort Indiantown Gap

Read more on the [193rd Special Operations Wing fact sheet](https://www.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/439262/193rd-special-operations-wing/) and the [193rd Special Operations Wing website](https://www.193sow.ang.af.mil/).

## Defense Logistics Agency in Pennsylvania

### DLA Distribution Susquehanna

DLA Distribution Susquehanna, with primary operations in **New Cumberland** and added facilities in Mechanicsburg, is a major **Defense Logistics Agency** distribution hub. It provides over **nine million square feet** of covered storage and ships military repair parts, medical supplies, hardware, and many other items to keep the Department of Defense ready.

**Key Missions:**

- Receipt, storage, and shipment of military materiel
- Distribution of repair parts, medical supplies, and hardware
- Distribution services that support readiness across the force

See the official [DLA Distribution Susquehanna page](https://www.dla.mil/Distribution/Locations/Susquehanna.aspx) and the [DLA Distribution overview](https://www.dla.mil/Distribution/).

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### 1. What is the largest military base in Pennsylvania?

**Tobyhanna Army Depot**, in the Pocono Mountains near Scranton, is one of the largest military installations in Pennsylvania. It is the Department of Defense's largest electronics maintenance depot, with about **3,700 employees**. **Carlisle Barracks**, home of the U.S. Army War College, is also a well-known post and the second oldest Army post in the country.

### 2. How many military bases are in Pennsylvania?

Pennsylvania is home to **seven major active installations**, supporting the Army, Navy, and Air Force, along with a major Defense Logistics Agency site and many reserve and Guard units.

### 3. What is the main Army base in Pennsylvania?

**Carlisle Barracks** is the best-known Army post in Pennsylvania as the home of the U.S. Army War College. **Tobyhanna Army Depot** and **Letterkenny Army Depot** are two large Army depots that repair electronics and missile defense systems.

### 4. What is the main Navy base in Pennsylvania?

**Naval Support Activity Philadelphia** is the Navy's main hub in Pennsylvania. It is home to NAVSUP Weapon Systems Support Philadelphia and the Naval Surface Warfare Center Philadelphia Division.
