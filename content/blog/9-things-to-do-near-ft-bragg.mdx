Whether you're looking to **spend a day on the beach, explore military history, or enjoy outdoor adventures**, there’s plenty to do near **Fort Liberty (formerly Fort Bragg)**. Here are **nine great activities** to check out for your next weekend outing.

A PCS move is a busy season. Between out-processing, house hunting, and getting the kids settled, it helps to know where to go when you have a free Saturday. The good news is that the Fayetteville area has plenty of low-cost, family-friendly options close to base. The list below mixes military history, outdoor time, and indoor fun for rainy days, so you can build a weekend that fits your family and your budget.

## **1. Try Different Brews and Bites at Downtown Fayetteville’s Food Truck Park**

Fayetteville’s **food truck park** is open **Thursday through Sunday** and features a **wide variety of food trucks** from all over North Carolina. Whether you're craving **barbecue, pizza, or international cuisine**, this is a great spot to explore local flavors.

**Tip:** The food is served on a **first-come, first-serve** basis, so arrive early if you want the best selection.

## **2. Visit the Airborne & Special Operations Museum**

The **Airborne & Special Operations Museum**, located in **Pope Field**, showcases the **history and evolution of U.S. airborne troops**. The museum features:

- **Interactive exhibits** on airborne training and operations
- **Historical displays** about military heroes like **General James Doolittle**
- **A collection of aircraft** used in military missions

**Hours:**

- **Monday–Friday:** 10 AM – 5 PM
- **Saturday–Sunday:** 10 AM – 4 PM
- **Admission:** $12 for adults, free for children under 12

[Visit the Airborne & Special Operations Museum](https://www.asomf.org/)

This is a good first stop for families new to the area. If your service member is assigned to one of the airborne units on post, the exhibits help spouses and kids picture the work and history behind the job. The museum sits in downtown Fayetteville, so it is easy to pair with lunch at the food truck park.

## **3. Catch a Show or Event at the Crown Complex**

The **Crown Complex** is a multi-use entertainment venue hosting:

- **Concerts**
- **Sporting events**
- **Trade shows**
- **Minor league baseball games (Fayetteville SwampDogs)**

In addition to events, the complex offers **dining options and an aquarium** for visitors.

[Check the Crown Complex event schedule](https://www.crowncomplexnc.com/)

## **4. Explore Carvers Creek State Park**

If you love the outdoors, **Carvers Creek State Park** is a great spot for:

- **Hiking scenic nature trails**
- **Camping and picnicking**
- **Kayaking and canoeing** on the **Cape Fear River**
- **Fishing and wildlife observation**

**Picnic shelters** are available year-round, making it a great spot for **family gatherings and group outings**.

Plan your visit to Carvers Creek State Park.

A state park is an easy, low-cost outing while you wait for household goods to arrive. Pack a lunch, let the kids burn off energy on the trails, and you have filled an afternoon without spending much. Trails and water levels can change with the seasons, so check current conditions before you drive out.

## **5. Enjoy Live Theater at Cape Fear Regional Theatre**

**Cape Fear Regional Theatre** is one of the **top professional theaters in North Carolina**, offering:

- **Broadway-style productions**
- **Local and national performances**
- **An on-site restaurant ("Stage Door")** for dining before or after a show

[See upcoming shows at Cape Fear Regional Theatre](https://www.cfrt.org/)

## **6. Experience Outdoor Fun at Fantasy Lake Adventure Park**

For an action-packed day, **Fantasy Lake Adventure Park** offers:

- **Scuba diving**
- **Zip-lining**
- **Rock climbing**
- **ATV riding**

The park’s **main attraction** is its **large lake**, where visitors can **swim, fish, or relax by the water**.

**Tip:** Check the park’s website for **seasonal hours and closures due to weather conditions**.

[Learn more about Fantasy Lake Adventure Park](https://www.fantasylakeadventurepark.com/)

## **7. Take the Kids to Fascinate-U Children’s Museum**

**Fascinate-U Children’s Museum** is an **interactive learning center** where kids can explore:

- **Hands-on science and history exhibits**
- **LEGO building stations**
- **Play areas with interactive toys and puzzles**

The museum also features **"Dinosaurs Alive!"**, an exhibit with life-sized dinosaur models.

**Location:** 1001 Embarcadero Road, Fayetteville
**Hours:**

- **Monday–Saturday:** 10 AM – 5 PM
- **Sunday:** 10 AM – 3 PM
- **Free parking available on-site**

[Visit the Fascinate-U Children's Museum](https://www.fascinate-u.com/)

## **8. Relax at Cape Fear Botanical Garden**

Located near **Fort Liberty**, the **Cape Fear Botanical Garden** is a peaceful retreat with:

- **Beautiful walking trails**
- **Birdwatching areas**
- **Seasonal flower displays**

It’s an ideal spot for a **relaxing afternoon, photography, or a quiet picnic**.

[Explore Cape Fear Botanical Garden](https://www.capefearbg.org/)

## **9. Watch a Minor League Baseball Game at J.P. Riddle Stadium**

Enjoy America’s pastime by catching a **Fayetteville Woodpeckers** minor league baseball game at **J.P. Riddle Stadium**.

- **Affordable tickets (under $10 per seat)**
- **Kid-friendly activities between innings**
- **Meet the players and team mascots**

Find game schedules and tickets.

A minor league game is one of the most affordable nights out in the area. With low ticket prices and plenty of between-inning activities for kids, it is an easy way to meet other families and feel at home in your new town.

## **Getting to Know Fort Liberty (formerly Fort Bragg)**

If you are PCSing here, it helps to understand the base itself. Fort Liberty (formerly Fort Bragg) is one of the largest U.S. Army installations by population and spans a large footprint across several counties in the Fayetteville area. It is the home of the [XVIII Airborne Corps and the 82nd Airborne Division](https://home.army.mil/bragg/about/mission), along with major special operations units. A PCS (Permanent Change of Station) is the military's term for a move to a new duty station, and for many soldiers a tour here means an assignment tied to rapid-deployment or airborne work.

Because the base is so large, where you choose to live can change your daily drive. Some families settle close to a specific gate to shorten the commute, while others trade a longer drive for a quieter neighborhood or better-fit schools. Spending a weekend doing the activities above is also a smart way to scout neighborhoods. As you drive to the museum, the ballpark, or the botanical garden, you get a feel for which parts of town fit your family before you sign a lease or make an offer.

## **Cost of Living and Housing Around Fayetteville**

Housing in the Fayetteville area is generally affordable compared with many other parts of the country, which is one reason a lot of families choose to buy here. As of February 28, 2026, the [typical home value in Fayetteville was about $222,766](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/52001/fayetteville-nc/), up 0.1% over the prior year, according to the Zillow Home Value Index. That figure is a typical value, not a median sale price, and it can shift over time, so use it as a rough starting point rather than a fixed number.

For service members, Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH) is the monthly housing allowance the military pays based on your rank, dependents, and duty-station ZIP code. Whether you rent or buy, it helps to know your BAH rate before you set a budget. You can look up current rates for the Fort Liberty area through the [official Defense Travel Management Office BAH calculator](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/). Pairing your BAH with the typical home values above gives you a realistic sense of what you can afford in the local market.

## **Fayetteville-Area Schools for Military Families**

Schools are often the top question for families with kids. Most students near base attend [Cumberland County Schools](https://www.greatschools.org/north-carolina/fayetteville/cumberland-county-school-district/), one of the larger districts in North Carolina, with 87 schools serving roughly 49,661 students as of the GreatSchools district profile reviewed in 2026. GreatSchools notes that many of the schools in the district are rated average in school quality, and that a number of schools have students making strong academic progress.

Ratings vary a lot from school to school, so it is worth looking up the specific schools that serve the neighborhoods you are considering. North Carolina also offers options for military families on the move, and many local schools are used to working with students who transfer mid-year because of a PCS. If continuity matters to your family, ask each school how they handle records, credits, and placement for incoming military students before you commit to an area.
