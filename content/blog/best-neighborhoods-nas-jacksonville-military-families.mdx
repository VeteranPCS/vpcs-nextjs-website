Orders to Naval Air Station Jacksonville (NAS Jax) put you at the largest Navy installation in the Southeast, sitting on the St. Johns River about 8 miles south of downtown at 6801 Roosevelt Boulevard. This guide covers the neighborhoods military families actually buy in, the 2026 numbers, and how to think about commute when you may also be driving family members to Naval Station Mayport on the other side of town.

Quick definitions: **PCS** (Permanent Change of Station) is your move to a new duty station. **BAH** (Basic Allowance for Housing) is the tax-free monthly housing stipend DoD pays based on pay grade, dependents, and your assigned **MHA** (Military Housing Area). **NAS** stands for Naval Air Station; **NS Mayport** (Naval Station Mayport) is the sister installation on the Atlantic. A **VA Loan** is the zero-down, no-PMI mortgage available to most service members and veterans.

## Know Your 2026 Jacksonville BAH Before You Tour a Single House

NAS Jacksonville and NS Mayport sit inside the same Jacksonville Military Housing Area, so your BAH rate is identical whether your gate is on the St. Johns or the Atlantic. A few things worth knowing before you run mortgage math:

- BAH is **tax-free**; many lenders gross it up about 25 percent when sizing your loan.
- With full entitlement, a [VA Loan has no county loan limit](/blog/complete-guide-to-buying-your-first-home-with-a-va-loan)—the ceiling is what your income supports.
- Members with dependents receive roughly 22 percent more BAH. If your family status is changing, update DEERS now.

## How NAS Jacksonville and NS Mayport Shape the Map

NAS Jacksonville sits on the southwest side of the city. The fastest commutes come from communities west and south of the St. Johns River along I-295 and US-17. NS Mayport sits on the east side at the mouth of the river. For dual-base couples, the urban core (San Marco, Riverside) tends to split the difference; the Beaches and Yulee tilt toward Mayport. Rush hour matters: a 20-minute midday drive can become 40 minutes at 0700, so test your potential commute at the time you would actually be driving.

## Neighborhood-by-Neighborhood Breakdown

### Orange Park (Clay County)

Just across the St. Johns River, Orange Park is the most-recommended off-base option for NAS Jax families. Clay County District Schools is A-rated, the commute runs 15–25 minutes via US-17, and the area is full of fellow military families. Median price sits around $340,000, starter ranches in the $250s and newer construction past $400,000. Trade-off: 1970s and 80s housing stock dominates the lower end, so plan careful inspections on roof, HVAC, and plumbing. At E-5 and above, a single-income BAH usually covers a 3-bed, 2-bath payment with a VA Loan.

### Fleming Island (Clay County)

Just south of Orange Park on Highway 17, Fleming Island is a planned, master-built version of its neighbor. Eagle Harbor and Pace Island bring newer construction, and Fleming Island High School ranks among the top in Northeast Florida. The 25–30 minute NAS Jax commute runs a touch longer than Orange Park's, but the resale story and school zoning often make up the difference for 4+ year tours. Median around $430,000 (range $320,000–$600,000+).

### Mandarin (Duval County)

Mandarin sits in southern Duval on the east bank of the St. Johns, 20–30 minutes from NAS Jax via I-295. It is one of Jacksonville's more affluent suburban areas, with quick access to St. Johns Town Center. The Duval district is B-rated overall, but several schools in the Mandarin zone rate well above the average. Median near $500,000 (smaller homes still in the $300s)—a fit for senior enlisted, warrants, and officers whose BAH stretches into the $2,300+ range.

### Argyle Forest (Duval County)

Argyle Forest sits on Jacksonville's southwest side, a 15–25 minute hop to NAS Jax via Blanding Boulevard and one of the strongest BAH-stretchers in the metro. Mostly 1990s-era suburban builds around $177 per square foot, median near $260,000. For a junior enlisted family or single service member at E-4 to E-6, it lets you bank the BAH-mortgage delta. School quality varies by address—confirm the assigned schools before you commit.

### San Marco, Riverside, and Avondale (Urban Duval)

Jacksonville's walkable, character-filled city options. Riverside and Avondale share King Street's restaurant scene and a stock of 1920s bungalows; San Marco has its own square of cafes south of downtown. NAS Jax runs 15–25 minutes; NS Mayport 25–35 via the Hart Bridge. Popular with dual-base couples and single officers. Median prices: Riverside about $320,000, Avondale about $470,000, historic homes past $700,000. Many city families use magnet, charter, or private schools rather than the default Duval assignment.

### Julington Creek and Fruit Cove (St Johns County)

If schools top your list, this is your answer. **St Johns County School District is ranked the #1 district in Florida for 2026 by Niche**, with a 97.1 percent graduation rate and one of only two Florida districts to earn an A every year since 2004. Julington Creek and Fruit Cove feed into Julington Creek Elementary, Switzerland Point Middle, and Bartram Trail High. NAS Jax runs 25–35 minutes via SR-13. Median around $557,000 in Fruit Cove (range $450,000–$750,000+). Budget for HOA dues; pricing typically requires an O-3 or senior enlisted BAH.

<Callout type="info" title="Why St Johns County keeps showing up on every list">
The district has held an A rating for over two decades and posts an average math proficiency score of 73 percent against the 52 percent state average. For families with school-age kids, the price premium reflects that data, not just zip-code marketing.
</Callout>

### Atlantic Beach, Neptune Beach, and Jacksonville Beach

The "Beaches" are the obvious pick for Mayport sailors and ocean-life families. Atlantic Beach is next door to Mayport (10–15 minutes); Neptune Beach is smallest and priciest; Jacksonville Beach is largest and most walkable, with the deepest rental and condo inventory. Medians: Jax Beach around $515,000, Atlantic Beach mid-to-high $600,000s, Neptune Beach often $750,000–$900,000+. Several beachside schools rate above the Duval average. Plan for flood insurance, salt-air maintenance, and a 35–45 minute cross-town commute if NAS Jax is your gate.

### Yulee (Nassau County)

Yulee sits north of Jacksonville off I-95, halfway between NS Mayport and NSB Kings Bay (the submarine base across the Georgia line). For Mayport-assigned families—or joint-service households with one member at Kings Bay—Yulee is one of the most practical splits: lots of new construction, A-rated Nassau schools, median around $370,000. The 40–50 minute NAS Jax commute is real, so it's rarely a fit for NAS Jax-only families.

## Use Your VA Loan and PCS Bonus to Move the Math

The VA Loan does more in Jacksonville than at most coastal duty stations, because Clay County and west-side Duval prices still leave room for the no-down, no-PMI structure to work. Per [VA.gov](https://www.va.gov/housing-assistance/home-loans/):

- **No down payment** with full entitlement. On a $400,000 Fleming Island home, that is $20,000 you keep at closing.
- **No PMI.** Conventional buyers under 20 percent down pay $150–$300/month at this price range. VA buyers do not.
- **Reusable benefit.** Use it more than once; keep a Jacksonville home as a rental at your next PCS if the math works.

If you have a service-connected disability rating, the **VA funding fee is waived in full**—roughly $8,600 on a $400,000 zero-down purchase. Confirm exemption status through [VA.gov](https://www.va.gov/housing-assistance/home-loans/funding-fee-and-closing-costs/) before closing.

## Flood Zones, Insurance, and Other Florida Realities

A meaningful share of Jacksonville neighborhoods—especially in the Beaches, Mandarin, and parts of Orange Park near the river—fall inside FEMA flood zones. Standard homeowner's insurance does not cover flood damage, and a VA Loan in a designated flood zone requires flood insurance, which can add $700–$2,500 a year depending on elevation. Have your agent pull the flood map before you make an offer. ## A 30-60 Day NAS Jacksonville Housing Plan

The families who land best start the plan as soon as orders post.

**Days 1–10: Foundation.** Confirm your 2026 BAH on the [DTMO calculator](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Benefits/Housing-Allowance/BAH-Calculator/). Pull your Certificate of Eligibility from VA.gov and get pre-approved using your PCS orders and gaining-station BAH. Get on the NAS Jax on-base housing waitlist even if you plan to buy.

**Days 11–30: Research.** Narrow to two or three neighborhoods from the table above. Test-drive each commute on Google Maps at 0700 and 1630, not noon. Pull the flood-zone map for any address you are seriously considering. Contact the NAS Jacksonville School Liaison Officer for enrollment and records transfer help.

**Days 31–60: Execution.** Use your permissive TDY (up to 10 days for house hunting) to tour in person. Work with a VeteranPCS agent who has closed VA Loans in Northeast Florida and knows the gates, flood zones, and school zones. Budget for inspections, the VA appraisal, and any seller-credit negotiation on older Orange Park or Westside homes.

<Callout type="note" title="Buy-vs-rent in Jacksonville, in one sentence">
If you expect to be at NAS Jax for 3+ years and can buy where comparable rents cover (or come close to covering) PITI, the VA Loan math almost always beats renting.
</Callout>

## Ready to Pick Your Jacksonville Neighborhood?

Orange Park and Fleming Island for short commutes and Clay County schools. Mandarin and Julington Creek for higher BAH grades. Argyle Forest for stretching every dollar. San Marco and Riverside for walkable city life. The Beaches and Yulee if Mayport is your real gate. The right answer fits *your* rank, *your* family, and *your* timeline.
