Indiana has a deep-rooted military history dating back to the **War of 1812** and has contributed to every major U.S. conflict since. Today, the state is home to **three key military installations**, each serving a distinct mission within the **Army National Guard, Air National Guard, and Coast Guard**. Whether you are **PCSing to Indiana, transitioning to civilian life, or seeking career opportunities**, understanding these bases and their roles is essential.

## **Military Installations in Indiana**

Indiana hosts three primary military installations:

- **Camp Atterbury-Muscatatuck** – A key training and readiness center for the Army National Guard.
- **Fort Wayne Air National Guard Base** – Home to the 122nd Fighter Wing, supporting air defense and combat operations.
- **USCG Station Michigan City** – A Coast Guard station ensuring maritime safety and security on the Great Lakes.

## **Camp Atterbury-Muscatatuck**

Camp Atterbury-Muscatatuck, located in **southern Indiana**, is a combined training facility with a long history of supporting **combat readiness and homeland security training**.

### **Key Missions:**

- **Army and Joint Force Training** – Provides **live-fire ranges, maneuver space, and simulation environments** for military and law enforcement units.
- **Cyber and Electronic Warfare Training** – Muscatatuck specializes in **cybersecurity exercises** and **urban warfare training**.
- **Civil Support and Disaster Response** – Trains units for **domestic emergency response, natural disasters, and humanitarian missions**.

Originally established in **1942**, Camp Atterbury played a **critical role in World War II**, serving as a training site for infantry, armor, and artillery units. It also functioned as a **prisoner-of-war camp** for captured Italian and German soldiers. After the war, the base’s activity slowed until it was transferred to the **Indiana National Guard in 1969**. Today, it remains **one of the most advanced National Guard training centers in the country**.

For more information, visit the **Camp Atterbury-Muscatatuck website.**

## **Fort Wayne Air National Guard Base**

Fort Wayne Air National Guard Base, located **adjacent to Fort Wayne International Airport**, has been a cornerstone of Indiana’s **air defense and combat operations** for decades.

### **Key Missions:**

- **122nd Fighter Wing (Black Snakes)** – Operates **A-10 Thunderbolt II aircraft**, specializing in **close air support and combat air patrol missions**.
- **Homeland Defense** – Plays a **critical role in national security**, conducting **air sovereignty and interception missions**.
- **Disaster Response** – Supports **state and federal emergency relief efforts** during natural disasters and crises.

Originally established as **Baer Field in 1941**, the base was an **important staging and training site** for pilots during **World War II**. After its closure in **1945**, the **Indiana Air National Guard** took control of the installation in **1954**, transforming it into a **permanent fighter base**. Today, the **122nd Fighter Wing continues to train and deploy in support of combat operations worldwide**.

For more information, visit the **Fort Wayne Air National Guard Base website.**

## **USCG Station Michigan City**

USCG Station Michigan City is a **vital maritime safety and security hub** located on the **southern shore of Lake Michigan**. It plays a crucial role in **protecting one of the busiest waterways in the Great Lakes region**.

### **Key Missions:**

- **Search and Rescue (SAR)** – Provides **emergency response for distressed boaters, swimmers, and commercial vessels**.
- **Maritime Law Enforcement** – Conducts **patrols to prevent illegal activities, enforce fishing regulations, and ensure boating safety**.
- **Homeland Security** – Supports **coastal security operations and disaster response efforts**.
- **Environmental Protection** – Monitors and **responds to hazardous spills, pollution, and ecological threats** in the Great Lakes.

Established in **1889** by the **U.S. Life-Saving Service**, Station Michigan City was created to **enhance maritime rescue capabilities** in response to increasing ship traffic on Lake Michigan. Over the years, it has evolved into a **multi-mission Coast Guard unit**, providing **year-round security and search-and-rescue operations**.

For more information, visit the **USCG Station Michigan City website.**
