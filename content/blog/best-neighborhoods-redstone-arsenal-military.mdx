When you get PCS (Permanent Change of Station) orders to Redstone Arsenal, the biggest decision is not the house. It is the address. Around Huntsville, your street decides which of three separate public school systems your children attend. Madison City Schools is consistently top-ranked in Alabama. Huntsville City Schools is large and diverse with strong magnet programs, though quality varies by feeder school. Madison County Schools serves the more rural, unincorporated areas. So picking a neighborhood is really picking a school system and a commute at once.

Many families are arriving now because the U.S. Space Command (SPACECOM) headquarters, a unified combatant command, is relocating to the Huntsville area. (This is the combatant command, not the U.S. Space Force service branch, which stays at the Pentagon.) That growth means more competition for good homes in good school zones, so it helps to know the trade-offs first.

This guide compares the neighborhoods most military families consider, using the [Zillow Home Value Index (ZHVI)](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/12014/huntsville-al/), a typical-value measure, not a median sale price. Each one is paired with a rough commute to its nearest Redstone gate and the school system it feeds.

## Madison: Best for Schools and Resale

Madison sits in zip codes 35758 and 35757, about 12 miles from Redstone, a 15 to 25 minute drive. Its [typical home value is $378,237, up 0.9% over the year](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/25704/madison-al/). Madison is the go-to choice for families who put schools and resale value first. It is served by Madison City Schools, which feeds [two highly rated high schools](https://redstone.armymwr.com/programs/school-liaison-officer), Bob Jones HS and James Clemens HS. Both earn an 8 out of 10 from GreatSchools and an A+ grade from Niche. With strong schools and steady demand, homes here tend to hold their value when it is your turn to PCS again.

## Hampton Cove and Owens Cross Roads: Master-Planned and Spacious

In zip 35763, Hampton Cove and Owens Cross Roads offer master-planned living with golf and larger lots. The [typical home value is $433,724, up 1.3% over the year](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/26348/owens-cross-roads-al/), the highest on this list. The drive runs about 20 to 30 minutes through Gate 9 (Rideout Road). This area draws a strong concentration of military and engineering families, so you will likely find neighbors who understand military life. It feeds Huntsville City Schools.

## Harvest and Monrovia: Best Value for New Construction

If budget is the main driver, look at Harvest and Monrovia in zip 35749. This area has the largest concentration of new construction homes under $400,000, and the [typical home value is $315,317, up 0.5% over the year](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/73588/harvest-al/). The commute is about 22 to 30 minutes to Gate 7 (Martin Road West). Homes here feed the Sparkman cluster within Madison County Schools, where Sparkman HS earns a 6 out of 10 from GreatSchools and a B+ from Niche. It is often the best fit for enlisted families in the E-5 to E-7 range and for junior-civilian budgets.

## Meridianville: Rural and Affordable

Meridianville, in zip 35759, is a rural option about 10 minutes north of downtown Huntsville. Its [typical home value is around $330,504, up 2.7% over the year](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/12014/huntsville-al/), the fastest year-over-year growth on this list. It feeds Madison County Schools and suits families who want more space and a quieter setting while staying close to the city.

## Athens: Lower Taxes, Fast Growth

Athens sits in Limestone County, with a [typical home value of $311,242, up 1.3% over the year](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/23533/athens-al/). The commute runs about 25 to 35 minutes. One draw is the property tax rate, even lower here at roughly 0.35%; confirm current rates through the [Alabama Department of Revenue property tax page](https://www.revenue.alabama.gov/property-tax/property-tax-assessment/). Athens is one of the fastest-growing parts of the region, so new neighborhoods and amenities are arriving quickly.

## South Huntsville, Jones Valley, and Blossomwood: Established and Central

These established neighborhoods sit 10 to 18 minutes from Gate 7 and feed Huntsville City Schools, including Huntsville HS, which earns an 8 out of 10 from GreatSchools and an A from Niche. (Nearby Grissom HS earns a 7 out of 10 and an A-.) You get mature trees, settled streets, and short drives to downtown and the medical district, a good fit for families who value character and central location over new construction.

## A Quick Note on Schools and Living On Post

Children who live on post attend Huntsville City Schools, specifically Williams Elementary and Middle and Columbia HS. There are no DoDEA schools on post. Alabama requires an immunization "Blue Card" to enroll, so handle that early. The [School Liaison Office](https://redstone.armymwr.com/programs/school-liaison-officer) can help with zones and enrollment at Bldg 1500, 256-876-7801.

Gate access matters as much as schools. Redstone uses several gates, including Gate 9 (Rideout Road, Visitor Center), Gate 7 (Martin Road West), Gate 1 (Martin Road East), and Gate 8 (Goss Road). Gate hours change frequently, so verify current hours through [Team Redstone newcomer information](https://home.army.mil/redstone/my-fort/newcomers) before you settle on a daily route.

## How to Decide

Start with the school system you want, then work backward to the neighborhoods that feed it and the gate you will use most. If schools and resale lead, Madison is the safe pick. For space and a master-planned feel, look at Hampton Cove. If budget comes first, Harvest gives you the most new construction for the money.
