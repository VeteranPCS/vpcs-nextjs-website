North Carolina has a **rich and diverse military presence**, with **ten major military installations** across the state. Each base plays a crucial role in supporting **national defense, training, and logistics operations**.

This guide provides an overview of each **military base in North Carolina**, its **mission**, and **historical significance**.

## **List of Military Bases in North Carolina**

- **Coast Guard Aviation Logistics Center**
- **Coast Guard Air Station Elizabeth City**
- **Coast Guard Sector North Carolina**
- **Fort Liberty (formerly Fort Bragg)**
- **Marine Corps Air Station Cherry Point**
- **Marine Corps Air Station New River**
- **Camp Lejeune**
- **Military Ocean Terminal Sunny Point**
- **Pope Army Airfield**
- **Seymour Johnson Air Force Base**

## **1. Coast Guard Aviation Logistics Center**

📍 **Location:** Elizabeth City, NC
🎯 **Mission:** Aviation logistics support for the Coast Guard and other federal agencies

The **Coast Guard Aviation Logistics Center** provides **maintenance, equipment, and training** for Coast Guard personnel operating aircraft. The center also oversees **aircraft carrier qualification training** for Coast Guard pilots.

[Visit the Coast Guard Aviation Logistics Center](https://www.uscg.mil/)

## **2. Coast Guard Air Station Elizabeth City**

📍 **Location:** Elizabeth City, NC
🎯 **Mission:** Maritime search and rescue, drug interdiction, and humanitarian aid

Home to the **Helicopter Interdiction Tactical Squadron (HITRON)**, this base plays a vital role in **drug enforcement, rescue missions, and overseas humanitarian operations**. The station supports **Coast Guard, Navy, and Air Force** missions.

[Visit Coast Guard Air Station Elizabeth City](https://www.uscg.mil/)

## **3. Coast Guard Sector North Carolina**

📍 **Location:** Morehead City, NC
🎯 **Mission:** Maritime safety, search and rescue, and navigation aid

One of the largest Coast Guard installations in the U.S., **Sector North Carolina** houses over **1,000 personnel** and manages **emergency response** for maritime disasters along the East Coast.

[Learn more about Coast Guard Sector North Carolina](https://www.uscg.mil/)

## **4. Fort Liberty (Formerly Fort Bragg)**

📍 **Location:** Fayetteville, NC
🎯 **Mission:** Home of the **82nd Airborne Division** and **Special Operations Command**

Fort Liberty is the **largest military base in North Carolina**, covering over **100,000 acres**. It serves as the **primary training and deployment hub** for airborne and special operations forces.

### **Key Facilities:**

- **Camp Mackall** – Largest military training area in the U.S.
- **Combined Arms Live Fire Complex (CALF)** – Weapons training range for tanks and fighter aircraft
- **Home to the U.S. Army Training and Doctrine Command (TRADOC)**

Visit the Fort Liberty Official Website

## **5. Marine Corps Air Station Cherry Point**

📍 **Location:** Havelock, NC
🎯 **Mission:** Training and operations for Marine aviation units

Home to the **2nd Marine Aircraft Wing**, this base provides **aviation support, pilot training, and operational command** for the Marine Corps. It employs over **5,000 personnel** and has a **$600 million annual budget**.

Visit Marine Corps Air Station Cherry Point

## **6. Marine Corps Air Station New River**

📍 **Location:** Jacksonville, NC
🎯 **Mission:** Helicopter and tilt-rotor aircraft training

Established in 1942, **MCAS New River** is responsible for **training Marine Corps pilots** to operate aircraft such as the **CH-53 Super Stallion** and **MV-22 Osprey**.

Visit Marine Corps Air Station New River

## **7. Marine Corps Base Camp Lejeune**

📍 **Location:** Jacksonville, NC
🎯 **Mission:** Home of the **2nd Marine Division** and **I Marine Expeditionary Force (I MEF)**

Camp Lejeune is **the largest Marine Corps base on the East Coast**, covering over **100 square miles**. It serves as a key **training hub** for **U.S. and NATO forces**, conducting joint military exercises.

### **Key Units:**

- **2nd Marine Division**
- **4th Marine Logistics Group**
- **2nd Marine Aircraft Wing**

Visit Marine Corps Base Camp Lejeune

## **8. Military Ocean Terminal Sunny Point**

📍 **Location:** Southport, NC
🎯 **Mission:** Military shipping and hazardous materials disposal

One of the **largest military shipping facilities in the world**, this terminal handles **logistics operations for ammunition, explosives, and hazardous materials**.

[Learn more about Military Ocean Terminal Sunny Point](https://www.sddc.army.mil/)

## **9. Pope Army Airfield**

📍 **Location:** Fayetteville, NC (Part of Fort Liberty)
🎯 **Mission:** Supports **airborne and special operations forces**

Home to the **43rd Air Mobility Operations Group**, Pope Army Airfield plays a key role in **airborne troop deployments, combat airlift, and logistics support**.

[Visit Pope Army Airfield](https://www.pope.af.mil/)

## **10. Seymour Johnson Air Force Base**

📍 **Location:** Goldsboro, NC
🎯 **Mission:** Home of the **4th Fighter Wing** and Joint Base McGuire-Dix-Lakehurst operations

Established in **1942**, this base is named after **Lt. Seymour A. Johnson**, a test pilot who died in 1941. It serves as a **fighter pilot training hub**, specializing in **F-15E Strike Eagle operations**.

[Visit Seymour Johnson Air Force Base](https://www.seymourjohnson.af.mil/)
