Welcome to the Space Coast. If you have orders for a PCS to Patrick Space Force Base, you are moving to one of the most distinctive installations in the U.S. military. PCS stands for Permanent Change of Station, the military term for a formal reassignment from one duty station to another. This 2026 guide covers what you need to know before you arrive: the base mission, where military families live, how housing pay works, schools, healthcare, and daily life on Florida's Atlantic coast.

## What and Where Is Patrick Space Force Base?

Patrick Space Force Base sits on a barrier island on Florida's Atlantic coast, adjacent to Cocoa Beach in Brevard County, roughly 60 miles east of Orlando. Patrick SFB is home to [Space Launch Delta 45](https://www.patrick.spaceforce.mil/), the U.S. Space Force unit responsible for the Eastern Range.

According to the [official Space Launch Delta 45 About Us page](https://www.patrick.spaceforce.mil/About-Us/), the Eastern Range is a 15-million-square-mile corridor of Atlantic airspace and ocean used for rocket launches from Cape Canaveral Space Force Station, just north of the base. SLD 45's mission is to ensure the safe and successful launch of both U.S. government and commercial payloads into orbit, coordinating range safety, tracking, and telemetry and preventing conflicts with air and sea traffic. In April 2026, SLD 45 supported NASA's Artemis II mission, the first crewed lunar-orbit flight of the program.

Patrick SFB is also home to the [920th Rescue Wing](https://www.920rqw.afrc.af.mil/), Air Force Reserve Command's premier combat search and rescue, or CSAR, unit. The 920th operates HC-130J Combat King II aircraft and HH-60W Jolly Green helicopters and consists of more than 2,500 combat rescue airmen trained to locate and recover isolated personnel. It also regularly supports civilian search and rescue off Florida's coast.

## Where to Live When PCSing to Patrick SFB

Most families moving to Patrick SFB live in on-post housing or settle in one of the surrounding Space Coast communities. Your choice usually comes down to commute, schools, budget, and how close you want to be to the beach. The Indian River Lagoon and barrier island geography shape road access, so some geographically short trips still run 20 or more minutes.

### Satellite Beach

Satellite Beach sits directly on the barrier island just south of the base, about 10 minutes to the main gate. It is quiet, family-friendly, and as close to beach-side as it gets without living on post.

### Cocoa Beach

Cocoa Beach is the most recognized community near the base, sitting just north of Patrick SFB at roughly 10 minutes from the gate. It combines a year-round beach lifestyle with proximity to the installation, with options ranging from beachside condos to single-family homes.

### Indian Harbour Beach

Indian Harbour Beach lies just south of Satellite Beach, also about 10 minutes from the gate. It has a calmer, residential character with beachfront and lagoon-side options, and a smaller tourism footprint than Cocoa Beach.

### Melbourne

Melbourne is the largest city in Brevard County, about 20 minutes south of the base. It offers the widest range of housing, services, and retail on the Space Coast. It is a strong choice for families who want city amenities and can handle a slightly longer commute.

### Cocoa

The city of Cocoa sits on the mainland about 20 minutes northwest of the base, across the Indian River Lagoon. It is older and more affordable than the barrier island communities, with convenient access to US-1 and I-95.

### Rockledge

Rockledge borders Cocoa on the mainland, also roughly 20 minutes from the gate. Established neighborhoods, a growing restaurant scene, and access to the Viera corridor to the south make it a practical middle-ground option.

### Merritt Island

Merritt Island lies between the barrier island and the mainland, about 25 minutes from Patrick SFB. It offers quiet lagoon-side living, older home prices, and proximity to Cape Canaveral and Kennedy Space Center.

### Viera

Viera is a planned community southwest of Melbourne, about 25 minutes from the base. It has newer construction, well-regarded schools, and easy access to Interstate 95 — a good fit for families who want a suburban feel and newer amenities.

## Patrick SFB BAH and the Cost of Living

If you live off post, the military helps cover your housing costs through BAH, which stands for Basic Allowance for Housing. BAH is a monthly, tax-free payment that depends on your rank, dependent status, and your duty station's Military Housing Area, or MHA.

Because rates change each year and vary by rank and dependent status, we do not publish a rate table here. Use the official DoD tool to find your figure: [travel.dod.mil BAH Rate Lookup](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/). Always confirm your rate before signing a lease or making an offer.

For a sense of buying costs, the typical home value in Melbourne was about $376,047 in mid-2026, according to [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/19307/melbourne-fl/). That is a typical value, not a median sale price. Values vary between the barrier island communities and the mainland, so Cocoa Beach and Satellite Beach will look different from Rockledge or Cocoa. As of the week of June 18, 2026, [Freddie Mac](https://www.freddiemac.com/pmms) reported the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate at 6.47 percent. Whether buying or renting makes more sense depends on your expected tour length.

## Schools Near Patrick SFB

All public schools in Brevard County fall under [Brevard Public Schools](https://www.brevardschools.org/), a single countywide district. The district supports military families through the Military Interstate Compact, academic planning resources, mental health services, and Military Family Resource pages at individual schools. Cocoa Beach Jr/Sr High School carries the Purple Star designation for military family support.

The school your child attends depends on your home address. Attendance boundaries can shift, so confirm the assigned school for any specific property before you commit.

## Healthcare: The 45th Medical Group

On-post medical care at Patrick SFB is provided by the [45th Medical Group](https://patrick.tricare.mil/), located at 1381 S. Patrick Drive on the base. The 45th MDG provides primary care and health services for eligible patients — Active Duty, dependents, and retirees. The [Clark Health Clinic](https://patrick.tricare.mil/Clinics/Clark-Health-Clinic) is a satellite location. Standard clinic hours are Monday through Thursday 0730 to 1630 and Friday 0930 to 1630.

Your military health coverage runs through TRICARE, the health care program for service members and their families. Set up your TRICARE plan early in your PCS. Update DEERS, transfer your plan, and request any referrals through the [45th MDG appointments and referrals page](https://patrick.tricare.mil/Getting-Care/Appointments-Referrals) before you arrive so there is no gap in care.

## Climate and Things to Do

The Space Coast has a subtropical climate with hot, humid summers, mild winters, and a rainy season that runs roughly June through September. The [National Weather Service in Melbourne](https://www.weather.gov/mlb/) has climate normals and records for the region. Winters rarely drop below 40 degrees. Hurricane season runs June through November — learn your Brevard County evacuation zone when you arrive.

The beach lifestyle is real here. The Cocoa Beach Pier, built in 1962 and stretching 800 feet into the Atlantic, has dining and live music and is minutes from the gate. Surfing is part of local culture, and the break at Cocoa Beach is active year-round.

[Kennedy Space Center Visitor Complex](https://www.kennedyspacecenter.com/), about 20 miles north, is one of Florida's most visited attractions, with full-scale rockets, the space shuttle Atlantis, and launch viewing on range days. Living next to the Eastern Range means you may be able to watch launches from your yard or the beach.

[Canaveral National Seashore](https://www.nps.gov/cana/index.htm) protects 24 miles of undeveloped Atlantic barrier island north of Cape Canaveral. The park has beaches, coastal dunes, ancient Timucua shell mounds, and Mosquito Lagoon, a biologically rich estuary, all a short drive from the base.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What MHA or area is Patrick SFB in for BAH?

Patrick Space Force Base falls within the Brevard County Military Housing Area for BAH purposes. Your rate depends on your rank and whether you have dependents. Look up your exact figure with the official [DoD BAH calculator](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/) using your rank and ZIP code.

### Where do military families live near Patrick SFB?

Common choices include Satellite Beach, Cocoa Beach, and Indian Harbour Beach on the barrier island; Melbourne and Rockledge on the mainland for more inventory; Merritt Island for a quieter lagoon setting; and Viera for newer construction and suburban amenities. On-post housing is also an option — ask your sponsor and the housing office about availability and wait times.

### Which school district covers Patrick SFB?

All of Brevard County is served by Brevard Public Schools. The specific school your child attends depends on your home address. Confirm the assigned school for any specific property before you commit.

### Where do I get healthcare at Patrick SFB?

On-post care is provided by the [45th Medical Group](https://patrick.tricare.mil/) at 1381 S. Patrick Drive. Set up your TRICARE plan early, update DEERS, and arrange any referrals before you arrive so your care continues without a gap.

### What is the weather like on the Space Coast?

The Space Coast has a subtropical climate with hot, humid summers, mild winters, and a June-through-September rainy season. Winters rarely drop below 40 degrees. Hurricane season runs June through November. The [National Weather Service in Melbourne](https://www.weather.gov/mlb/) has climate normals and records.

### Can I see rocket launches from the area?

Yes. The Eastern Range, managed by Space Launch Delta 45, supports launches from Cape Canaveral Space Force Station, about 15 miles north of the base. Many launches are visible from the beach and from communities across Brevard County.
