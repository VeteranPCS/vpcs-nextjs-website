Arkansas is home to **over 300,000 veterans**, around **5,000 active-duty service members**, and **over 13,000 National Guard and reserve members**. The state's military bases contribute significantly to the **local economy**, supporting **over 20,000 jobs** and generating more than **$2.5 billion annually**.

Arkansas is home to **six military installations**, each playing a critical role in **training, defense, and strategic military operations**.

## **List of Military Bases in Arkansas**

- **Camp Robinson**
- **Ebbing Air National Guard Base**
- **Fort Chaffee**
- **Little Rock Air Force Base**
- **Pine Bluff Arsenal**

## **1. Camp Robinson**

📍 **Location:** North Little Rock, AR
🎯 **Mission:** National Guard training and infantry operations

**Camp Joseph T. Robinson** serves as a **major training center for the U.S. Army and National Guard**. The base provides training in:

- **Infantry tactics**
- **Aviation operations**
- **Logistics support**

### **Key Units Stationed at Camp Robinson:**

- **39th Infantry Brigade Combat Team**
- **77th Combat Aviation Brigade**
- **87th Troop Command**

[Visit Camp Robinson](https://www.arguard.org/)

## **2. Ebbing Air National Guard Base**

📍 **Location:** Fort Smith, AR
🎯 **Mission:** Air National Guard training and recruitment

Located in **Fort Smith**, **Ebbing Air National Guard Base** is home to the **188th Wing of the Arkansas Air National Guard**. It serves as the **recruiting and processing center** for new Air National Guard members, providing:

- **Recruitment services**
- **Processing of new recruits**
- **Training and career guidance**

Visit Ebbing Air National Guard Base

## **3. Fort Chaffee**

📍 **Location:** Fort Smith, AR
🎯 **Mission:** Military training and combat readiness

**Fort Chaffee** is a **large-scale training facility**, covering over **64,000 acres**. It is used by:

- **Active-duty Army units**
- **Army National Guard**
- **Reserve forces**

### **Key Training Facilities at Fort Chaffee:**

- **Airfield operations**
- **Live-fire training areas**
- **Urban warfare training zones**
- **CBRN (Chemical, Biological, Radiological, and Nuclear) training facilities**

Established in **1941**, Fort Chaffee has been used for **training soldiers in World War II, the Korean War, and the Vietnam War**.

[Visit Fort Chaffee](https://www.ftchaffee.com/)

## **4. Little Rock Air Force Base**

📍 **Location:** Jacksonville, AR
🎯 **Mission:** Air mobility and transport operations

**Little Rock Air Force Base** is the **largest military installation in Arkansas** and serves as a **key strategic hub for U.S. air mobility operations**.

### **Aircraft Based at Little Rock AFB:**

- **C-130 Hercules** – Tactical airlift aircraft
- **C-17 Globemaster III** – Heavy airlift aircraft
- **KC-135 Stratotanker** – Aerial refueling tanker

### **Key Units at Little Rock AFB:**

- **19th Airlift Wing** – Provides **rapid global mobility and airlift capabilities**
- **314th Airlift Wing** – **Aircrew training for C-130 pilots**
- **189th Airlift Wing** (Arkansas Air National Guard) – **Training and combat support**

Little Rock AFB has been an **essential part of U.S. Air Force training and deployment operations since 1942**.

[Visit Little Rock Air Force Base](https://www.littlerock.af.mil/)

## **5. Pine Bluff Arsenal**

📍 **Location:** Pine Bluff, AR
🎯 **Mission:** Munitions production and military equipment manufacturing

Covering over **13,000 acres**, **Pine Bluff Arsenal** is a **major defense production site** specializing in:

- **Munitions manufacturing**
- **Chemical defense systems**
- **Smoke generators and riot control agents**
- **Pyrotechnic devices**

Established in **1941**, Pine Bluff Arsenal has produced **military equipment for every major U.S. conflict** since World War II.
