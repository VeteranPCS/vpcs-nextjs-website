A **Permanent Change of Station (PCS) move** is one of the most significant transitions for active duty military personnel and their families. Whether you are relocating within the United States or moving overseas, **planning ahead** is essential for a **smooth and efficient move**.

This guide provides a **comprehensive PCS checklist and timeline**, ensuring you stay organized and reduce the stress that comes with relocation. From **pre-move preparations** to **settling into your new duty station**, follow this structured approach to make your move as seamless as possible.

## **Pre-Move Checklist (8-12 Weeks Before Move)**

### **1. Review Your PCS Orders**

- Confirm your **report date, destination, and entitlements**.
- Understand **allowances, reimbursements, and moving options**.
- Contact your **unit personnel office** for guidance on next steps.

### **2. Create a Moving Binder**

- Organize all important documents in one place, including:
  - PCS orders
  - Medical records
  - School records
  - Housing documents
  - Insurance policies
- Make digital copies for backup.

### **3. Plan Your Budget**

- Outline **expected expenses**, including:
  - Temporary lodging
  - Travel costs
  - Shipping expenses for pets or vehicles
- Understand **allowances such as Dislocation Allowance (DLA) and Temporary Lodging Expense (TLE)**.

### **4. Contact Your Transportation Office**

- Schedule a consultation with the **Personal Property Office (PPO)** or **Transportation Office**.
- Use [**Move.mil**](https://www.move.mil/) to explore your **household goods shipment options**.
- Decide if you will use a **government-mandated moving service** or conduct a **Personally Procured Move (PPM)**.

### **5. Secure Housing at Your New Duty Station**

- If moving **on-base**, apply through the **Military Housing Office**.
- If moving **off-base**, research rental or homebuying options.
- Connect with [**VeteranPCS real estate agents**](https://veteranpcs.com) for assistance with buying or renting.

### **6. Research Your New Location**

- Use **MilitaryINSTALLATIONS** to explore local services.
- Look into **childcare, schools, and healthcare facilities**.
- Consider **commuting distances** and **base amenities**.

### **7. Notify Key Service Providers**

- Contact **utility companies, internet providers, and insurance companies**.
- Schedule **final bill payments** and request **service transfers**.
- Arrange mail forwarding through **USPS**.

## **1-2 Weeks Before Move**

### **1. Confirm Travel Arrangements**

- Book **flights, hotels, or rental vehicles** if necessary.
- Arrange **pet transportation** if moving with animals.
- Have a **backup plan** in case of travel delays.

### **2. Pack a PCS Essentials Kit**

Prepare a bag with **important items** to keep with you during travel:

- Orders, IDs, and travel documents
- Medications and medical records
- A few days’ worth of clothing
- Snacks and personal hygiene items
- Electronics and chargers

### **3. Prepare Household Items for Movers**

- Declutter and **donate or sell** unwanted items.
- Separate **high-value or sentimental belongings** to carry with you.
- Label **all boxes** with detailed inventory.

### **4. Schedule Final Appointments**

- Complete **medical checkups** and obtain necessary prescriptions.
- Schedule **vehicle maintenance** for long-distance travel.
- Ensure **children’s school records** are transferred.

### **5. Conduct a Home Walkthrough**

- Take **photos of your home** before movers arrive.
- Clean and document **any existing damage** in rental properties.
- Review **your lease terms** to ensure full deposit refunds.

## **Moving Day Checklist**

### **1. Coordinate with Movers**

- Supervise the **packing and loading process**.
- Verify that **all boxes are labeled correctly**.
- Keep **essential documents, valuables, and your PCS kit** with you.

### **2. Perform a Final Home Inspection**

- Walk through your home one last time.
- Secure **windows, doors, and utilities**.
- Leave **forwarding address** for any remaining mail.

### **3. Prepare for Travel**

- Confirm your **route and accommodations** if driving.
- Keep **snacks and essentials** handy for children or pets.
- Stay **in contact with your new unit** for arrival instructions.

## **Post-Move Checklist (First Week After Arrival)**

### **1. Check In at Your New Duty Station**

- Report to **your unit or gaining command** as per your orders.
- Schedule a meeting with the **base housing office** if needed.

### **2. Inspect Delivered Household Goods**

- Cross-check delivered items with **your inventory list**.
- Document **any missing or damaged belongings** and file claims immediately.

### **3. Register for Local Services**

- Set up **utilities, internet, and phone service**.
- Register **children for school** and update **emergency contacts**.
- Locate **medical and dental providers** covered under TRICARE.

## **Within the First Month**

### **1. Update Personal Information**

- Notify **banks, credit card companies, and insurance providers** of your address change.
- Update your **driver’s license and vehicle registration** (if required).
- Change your address with **the USPS and any subscription services**.

### **2. Get Involved in Your New Community**

- Attend **newcomer briefings** offered on base.
- Join **spouse or family support groups** to connect with others.
- Explore local **parks, gyms, and family-friendly activities**.

## **Final Thoughts: Stay Organized for a Smooth PCS Move**

Relocating under military orders can be **challenging**, but **preparing in advance** makes all the difference. By following this structured **PCS checklist and timeline**, you can stay organized and handle your move with confidence.

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

### **1. When should I start preparing for my PCS move?**

It is best to start planning **8-12 weeks before your move date**. This allows enough time to **secure housing, schedule movers, and handle logistics** without unnecessary stress.

### **2. What is the best way to move my household goods?**

You can either:

- Use the **government-arranged moving service** through the **Transportation Office**.
- Choose a **Personally Procured Move (PPM)**, where you handle the move and get reimbursed for expenses.

### **3. How do I track my household goods during the move?**

Use [**Move.mil**](https://www.move.mil/) to track your shipment and get updates on delivery timelines.

### **4. What expenses does the military cover during a PCS?**

The military provides **allowances** to offset moving costs, including:

- **Dislocation Allowance (DLA)**
- **Temporary Lodging Expense (TLE)**
- **Per Diem for travel days**

### **5. How can I find housing at my new duty station?**

- Check with the **Military Housing Office** for on-base housing availability.
- If looking for off-base housing, **VeteranPCS connects military families with veteran real estate agents** who understand PCS moves.
