If you just received orders to Fort Eisenhower (formerly Fort Gordon), welcome to the Augusta area. PCS moves can feel overwhelming, especially when you are relocating to a new state and trying to figure out where to live, what the area is like, and how the homebuying process works.

Ineisha Wiley, a local REALTOR® who works with military families in the Augusta market, puts it simply: there are three things every PCSing family should know before they arrive. You do not have to live right outside the gate, your VA Loan benefit is more powerful than you think, and you can actually buy a home before you even set foot in Georgia. This guide takes those three insights and goes deeper, giving you everything you need to plan a confident move to Fort Eisenhower.

## What Is Fort Eisenhower and Why Are You Going There?

Fort Eisenhower is one of the largest Army installations in the world. Renamed from Fort Gordon in 2023, the post is home to the [U.S. Army Cyber Center of Excellence](https://cybercoe.army.mil/), the U.S. Army Cyber Command (ARCYBER), and elements of the National Security Agency's Georgia Cryptologic Center. It is the Army's premier hub for cyber warfare, signals intelligence, electronic warfare, and information operations.

More than 16,000 military service members and approximately 13,500 civilian personnel are assigned to the installation, according to [Fort Eisenhower's official resources](https://home.army.mil/eisenhower). In addition to its cyber mission, the post houses components of the Army Intelligence and Security Command, operations units from the Navy, Air Force, and Marines, and multiple reserve and National Guard units.

For families, that means this is a growing, mission-critical installation with an expanding population. The surrounding area has responded with rapid housing development, improved infrastructure, and a community that is accustomed to welcoming PCS families year-round.

## You Do Not Have to Live Right Outside the Gate

As Ineisha Wiley points out, many military families assume they need to live as close to the installation as possible. In reality, the Augusta area has several excellent communities within a short commute, and each one offers a different lifestyle depending on your budget, family needs, and priorities.

### Grovetown: New Construction and Short Commutes

Grovetown sits directly adjacent to Fort Eisenhower in Columbia County and is one of the fastest-growing communities in the region. The commute to post is typically under 10 minutes, making it a favorite among service members who want to minimize drive time. Grovetown is known for an abundance of new construction homes and neighborhoods built with military families in mind. Entry-level pricing starts in the low $200s for three-bedroom homes, which makes Grovetown one of the most BAH-friendly options in the area.

### Evans: Top-Rated Schools and Established Neighborhoods

Evans offers a more established community feel with tree-lined streets, strong neighborhood identities, and some of the highest-rated schools in the state. It sits a bit farther from post than Grovetown, but many families gladly make the extra drive for the quality of life. Price points in Evans tend to run higher, with homes often ranging from the mid-$200s to the mid-$400s, but the schools and amenities make it a top choice for families with school-age children.

### North Augusta: South Carolina Tax Benefits

Located just across the Savannah River in South Carolina, North Augusta offers a compelling option for families who want to take advantage of different state property tax rates and housing options. South Carolina's tax structure can be favorable compared to Georgia, depending on your situation, so it is worth running the numbers. North Augusta also blends small-town charm with modern development and gives you easy access to both downtown Augusta and the interstate system.

### Other Options: Martinez, Harlem, and Hephzibah

Martinez offers a nice balance of affordability and access, sitting between Evans and Augusta proper. Harlem is a quieter, more rural option that still puts you within a reasonable commute. For those on a tighter budget, Hephzibah offers affordability and direct access to Fort Eisenhower through Gate 5, just six miles from the installation.

## Understanding BAH and the Cost of Living

One of the biggest advantages of a PCS to the Augusta area is that the cost of living runs about 10% lower than the national average, according to [Salary.com cost of living data](https://www.salary.com/research/cost-of-living/augusta-ga). That means your Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH) tends to stretch further here than at many other installations.

For 2026, Fort Eisenhower BAH rates saw approximately a 6% increase over the previous year. According to the [Defense Travel Management Office](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/), monthly BAH rates with dependents at Fort Eisenhower are approximately $1,890 for an E-5, $2,157 for an E-6, and $2,190 for an E-7. These rates align well with the Columbia County housing market, where the median home sales price sits around $259,000.

For most E-5 and above service members with dependents, Columbia County's price points fit comfortably within BAH. Junior enlisted families may want to focus their search on Grovetown and Harlem for the most comfortable budget fit. In many cases, a monthly mortgage payment on a home purchased with a VA Loan in this area will come in at or below what you would pay in rent, which brings us to the next critical point.

## Your VA Loan Is More Powerful Than You Think

As Ineisha Wiley emphasizes, many service members do not realize just how powerful their VA home loan benefit can be. The [VA home loan program](https://www.va.gov/housing-assistance/home-loans/) was designed specifically for military families, and it comes with advantages that conventional loans simply cannot match.

### Zero Down Payment

The most significant benefit is the ability to purchase a home with no down payment. According to the [Consumer Financial Protection Bureau](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/), conventional loans typically require anywhere from 3% to 20% down. On a $260,000 home near Fort Eisenhower, that would mean $7,800 to $52,000 out of pocket. With a VA Loan, that number is zero.

### No Private Mortgage Insurance

Conventional borrowers who put down less than 20% are required to pay private mortgage insurance (PMI), which can add $100 to $300 or more to a monthly payment. VA Loans eliminate this requirement entirely, saving military families thousands of dollars over the life of the loan.

### Competitive Interest Rates

VA Loans consistently offer some of the lowest interest rates available in the mortgage market. According to [VA.gov](https://www.va.gov/housing-assistance/home-loans/loan-types/purchase-loan/), lenders can offer favorable rates because the VA guarantees a portion of the loan, which reduces risk for the lender and passes savings on to the borrower.

### Easier to Buy Than Rent

With the rapid housing growth happening around Fort Eisenhower, rental rates have climbed. A typical three-bedroom rental in Columbia County ranges from $1,400 to $2,100 per month. When you factor in zero down payment, no PMI, and competitive interest rates, many PCS families find that a monthly mortgage payment is equal to or lower than what they would pay in rent, with the added benefit of building equity.

## You Can Buy a Home Before You Even Arrive

One of the biggest misconceptions about PCSing is that you have to wait until you arrive at your new duty station to start the home search. As Ineisha Wiley notes, military buyers purchase homes completely remotely every day using virtual tours, video walkthroughs, digital paperwork, and remote closings.

### How Remote Buying Works

Once you have orders in hand, you can connect with a local real estate agent and a VA Loan lender to start the process immediately. Here is what a typical remote purchase timeline looks like.

**60 to 90 Days Before Your Report Date**: Get pre-approved with a VA Loan lender and begin reviewing neighborhoods and listings online with your agent.

**45 to 60 Days Out**: Your agent schedules live video walkthroughs of homes that match your criteria. Many agents will walk through the home on a video call, opening cabinets, checking closets, and showing you the neighborhood in real time.

**30 to 45 Days Out**: Once you find the right home, your agent submits the offer and handles negotiations. Inspections can be coordinated with you reviewing reports digitally.

**15 to 30 Days Out**: Digital paperwork and e-signatures handle most of the closing process. Many title companies in the Augusta area offer fully remote closings, meaning you can sign your documents electronically and have keys waiting for you when you arrive.

### Why This Matters

Buying remotely means you can have a home ready on your report date instead of scrambling for temporary lodging, rushing into a rental, or making a pressured housing decision during your first week at a new installation. It also gives you more time to make a thoughtful decision about the right neighborhood for your family.

## Schools: Why Columbia County Is the Top Pick for Military Families

Schools are often the deciding factor in where military families choose to live, and the Fort Eisenhower area gives you a clear choice between two main districts.

The [Columbia County School District](https://www.ccboe.net/) consistently ranks among the best in Georgia and is the primary reason many military families choose to live in Evans or Grovetown rather than closer to post in Richmond County. According to [Niche.com](https://www.niche.com/k12/d/columbia-county-schools-ga/), Columbia County schools earn top marks for academics, teachers, and college preparation. In 2025, five schools in the area received the Military Flagship School designation for their commitment to supporting military-connected students, including Cedar Ridge Elementary, Greenbriar Middle, Grovetown Elementary, and Riverside Middle. That designation means these schools have demonstrated specific programs and policies designed to ease transitions for children who move frequently, including welcoming committees, transfer-friendly enrollment processes, and staff trained to support military-connected students.

The [Richmond County School System](https://www.rcboe.org/) serves the Augusta side of the area, including families who live on post. Children living on Fort Eisenhower attend Richmond County schools, specifically Freedom Park Elementary and The Academy of Richmond County. Richmond County offers proximity to base and more affordable housing, but Columbia County's academic reputation is the primary reason families choose to commute a few extra minutes each day.

If you are PCSing mid-year, Columbia County has a dedicated [Military Families page](https://www.ccboe.net/apps/pages/index.jsp?uREC_ID=1197233&type=d&pREC_ID=1439661) on their website with enrollment resources and support specifically for PCS families. They are experienced with mid-year enrollments and can help your children transition into the right classes quickly, which is especially important for high school students who need credits to transfer cleanly.

## Healthcare on Post

Fort Eisenhower is home to the Dwight D. Eisenhower Army Medical Center (EAMC), a full-service military hospital that provides comprehensive care to the military community. This is a significant advantage compared to installations that only have clinics and require families to seek off-post care for most services. EAMC offers primary care, specialty clinics, dental care, behavioral health services, and emergency services all on the installation. For families with young children, the hospital also has pediatric services and a birthing center, which means you will not need to navigate the civilian healthcare system for most routine and urgent needs. The Augusta area also has a strong civilian medical community, including Augusta University Medical Center, which provides additional specialty care options if you need them.

## Recreation and Quality of Life

The Augusta area offers more than you might expect when it comes to recreation and quality of life for military families.

### On Post

Fort Eisenhower's MWR program, according to [Fort Eisenhower MWR](https://gordon.armymwr.com/), includes the Eisenhower Lakes Golf Club (designed by Robert Trent Jones Sr.), Hilltop Riding Stables, a disc golf course, two bicycle road courses, Leitner Lake Recreation Complex with 35 full-hookup campsites, Five Star Lanes bowling center, and the Fort Eisenhower Dinner Theatre offering year-round shows with buffet meals. The U.S. Army Signal Corps Museum is also on post and free for visitors.

### Off Post

Downtown Augusta offers the Riverwalk along the Savannah River, the Morris Museum of Art, and a growing dining and entertainment scene. Augusta is, of course, also the home of the Masters Tournament at Augusta National Golf Club. Phinizy Swamp Nature Park provides trails and birdwatching opportunities, and Lake Thurmond (about 30 miles away) is a popular destination for boating, fishing, camping, and hiking. The area also offers access to the Woodrow Wilson Boyhood Home, a National Historic Landmark just 20 minutes from post.

### Weather

One thing to prepare for is the heat. The Augusta area is hot and humid from late spring through early fall. Families who enjoy outdoor activities will want to plan accordingly, and living in a neighborhood with a pool or near one of the area lakes makes the summer months much more enjoyable.

## On-Post Housing: What to Expect

On-post housing at Fort Eisenhower is managed by Balfour Beatty Communities and includes six neighborhoods with two- to five-bedroom single-family homes and townhomes. Neighborhood eligibility is determined by rank. The community is pet-friendly and walkable, with amenities including playgrounds, basketball and tennis courts, and a fitness center.

However, with only about 1,000 homes on post and the installation's growing population driven by the expanding cyber mission, waitlists are common, especially during peak PCS season from May through August. Most families end up living off post, which is why understanding the surrounding communities is so important.

If your report date is still a few months out, here is a simple action plan to make the most of your time.

**As Soon as You Have Orders**: Contact a VA Loan lender to get pre-approved. This tells you exactly what you can afford and puts you in a strong position to make offers quickly. Reach out to a real estate agent in the Augusta area who understands military timelines and the local market.

**60 Days Out**: Start narrowing down neighborhoods based on your priorities, whether that is school quality, commute time, budget, or proximity to amenities. Begin virtual tours with your agent.

**30 Days Out**: If you have found the right home, your agent and lender can coordinate inspections, appraisals, and closing logistics while you handle the rest of your PCS checklist.

**Move-In Week**: If everything goes smoothly, you can pick up keys and move into your own home rather than checking into temporary lodging or signing a lease under pressure.
