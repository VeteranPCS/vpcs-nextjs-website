If you have orders for a PCS to Beale Air Force Base, welcome to the Sacramento Valley. PCS stands for Permanent Change of Station, the military term for an official move from one duty station to another. Beale AFB sits in Yuba County, California, between the cities of Marysville and Yuba City, about 40 miles north of Sacramento. This 2026 guide covers the base mission, where to live, how housing pay works, schools, healthcare, the local climate, and things to do once you are settled.

## What and Where Is Beale AFB?

Beale Air Force Base is located in Yuba County in California's northern Sacramento Valley, roughly nine miles east of Marysville. It is the home of the [9th Reconnaissance Wing](https://www.beale.af.mil/Information/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/279932/9th-reconnaissance-wing/), the Air Force's primary high-altitude intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance wing.

The 9th RW operates the U-2 Dragon Lady, a single-engine reconnaissance aircraft that has gathered imagery and signals intelligence since the 1950s, and the RQ-4 Global Hawk, an unmanned aerial vehicle built for long high-altitude missions. The wing also operates the T-38 Talon for pilot proficiency training. According to the [9th RW fact sheet on beale.af.mil](https://www.beale.af.mil/Information/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/279932/9th-reconnaissance-wing/), the wing is composed of more than 3,000 personnel in four groups and conducts reconnaissance missions around the world 24 hours a day, seven days a week. Beale AFB also hosts a missile-warning radar mission that contributes to national defense.

## Where to Live When PCSing to Beale AFB

Most families who move to Beale AFB either live in on-post housing or settle in one of the surrounding communities. Your choice usually comes down to commute, schools, budget, and the kind of setting you want. Ask your sponsor and the housing office about on-post availability and current wait times before you commit to a neighborhood off base.

### Marysville

Marysville is the county seat of Yuba County and one of the closest communities to the base, with a drive of roughly 15 minutes. It is a small, historic city on the Yuba River with affordable housing and a tight-knit feel. Many service members stationed at Beale find Marysville a practical choice for keeping commutes short and costs manageable.

### Wheatland

Wheatland is a small town in Yuba County, also about 15 minutes from the main gate. It has a quiet, agricultural character and sits closer to the foothills. Families who want a small-town setting with a short commute often look here.

### Yuba City

Yuba City, just across the Feather River from Marysville, is the larger commercial hub of the area and about 20 minutes from the base. It offers more shopping, dining, and medical options than Marysville while still keeping commutes reasonable. It is the most service-rich community in the immediate area and a common choice for families who want more amenities close by.

### Lincoln

Lincoln is in Placer County, roughly 30 minutes south of the base. It has grown quickly over the past two decades with new master-planned communities, strong schools, and a suburban feel. Families willing to drive a little farther for more amenities and newer construction often look in Lincoln.

### Roseville

Roseville is a large suburban city in Placer County, about 45 minutes from Beale. It has a wide range of housing, restaurants, shopping, and services, and it connects easily to the Sacramento metro. Expect a longer daily commute in exchange for city-level amenities and a larger housing market.

### Sacramento

The state capital is roughly 50 minutes south. It offers the most in terms of employment, entertainment, and urban life, but the commute is the longest on this list. It tends to work best for families with flexible schedules or a second income in the city.

## Beale AFB BAH and the Cost of Living

If you live off base, the military helps cover your housing through BAH, which stands for Basic Allowance for Housing. BAH is a monthly, tax-free payment. The amount depends on three things: your pay grade, whether you have dependents, and your duty station's Military Housing Area, or MHA. An MHA is the geographic zone the Department of Defense uses to set local housing pay. Beale AFB falls within the Yuba City, California Military Housing Area.

Because rates change each year and vary by rank and dependent status, we do not publish a rate table here. To find your exact figure, use the official DoD BAH calculator at [travel.dod.mil](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/). Enter your pay grade and ZIP code to see the current rate. Always confirm your rate before signing a lease or making an offer.

For a sense of buying costs, the typical home value in Yuba City was about $443,000 in early 2026, according to [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/34940/yuba-city-ca/). That is a typical value, not a median sale price. As of the week of June 18, 2026, [Freddie Mac](https://www.freddiemac.com/pmms) reported the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate at 6.47 percent. Whether buying or renting makes more sense depends on how long you expect to be stationed here, so run the numbers for your own situation.

## Schools Near Beale AFB

The schools your children attend depend on where you live. Several districts serve the Beale AFB area.

Families living on base at Beale are primarily served by the [Wheatland Elementary School District](https://nces.ed.gov/ccd/districtsearch/district_detail.asp?ID2=0642330) and [Wheatland Union High School District](https://nces.ed.gov/ccd/districtsearch/district_detail.asp?ID2=0642350), according to the National Center for Education Statistics. Lone Tree Elementary and Wheatland Charter Academy, both within the Wheatland Elementary School District, are located directly at or near Beale AFB.

Families who live in Marysville or the broader Yuba County area may fall under the [Marysville Joint Unified School District](https://www.mjusd.com/), which serves approximately 10,000 students at 23 schools across Yuba County. Families settling in the Plumas Lake area, between the base and Yuba City, may be served by the [Plumas Lake Elementary School District](https://nces.ed.gov/ccd/districtsearch/district_detail.asp?ID2=0631180), a smaller district covering kindergarten through eighth grade. Families who move to Lincoln or Roseville will fall under the Lincoln Unified or Roseville City School District, respectively.

School attendance boundaries can shift, so confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit.

## Healthcare: The 9th Medical Group

On-base medical care at Beale AFB is provided by the [9th Medical Group](https://beale.tricare.mil/), which operates the Beale AFB clinic and offers primary care, family health, flight medicine, specialty care, and other health services for eligible patients. You can find clinic details, appointments, and referral information on [beale.tricare.mil](https://beale.tricare.mil/Getting-Care/Appointments-Referrals).

Your military health coverage runs through TRICARE, the health care program for service members and their families. Set up your TRICARE plan early in your PCS, update DEERS, and coordinate any referrals before you arrive so there is no gap in care.

## Climate and Things to Do

Beale AFB sits in the Sacramento Valley, one of the hottest and driest valley floors in California during summer. According to the [National Weather Service Sacramento office](https://www.weather.gov/sto/), the region experiences hot, dry summers — with highs regularly above 95°F in July and August — and mild, wet winters. Rain falls mostly from November through March. Spring and fall are pleasant, with warm days and cool nights.

The surroundings offer a lot for outdoor-minded families. The Sutter Buttes rise just west of Yuba City — often called the smallest mountain range in the world — and the Middle Mountain Foundation offers guided hikes there by reservation. [Lake Oroville](https://www.parks.ca.gov/?page_id=462), about 30 miles northeast, has boating, fishing, and camping in the Sierra Nevada foothills. The gold country along Highway 49 — Nevada City, Grass Valley, and nearby river towns — begins less than an hour east. Collins Lake and Camp Far West are within 30 minutes for a quicker outdoor fix. Sacramento, 50 minutes south, covers museums, sports, and urban dining.
