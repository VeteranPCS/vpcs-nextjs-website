When you get orders to Florida, the first question after "where will we live" is almost always "where will the kids go to school." That order is backwards. In most Florida metros, picking the school comes first and the house follows, because school zone lines decide which neighborhoods are even on the table.

This guide covers Florida's A-to-F school grading, the Interstate Compact rights your family already has, and the best-rated districts near MacDill, Eglin and Hurlburt Field, NAS Jacksonville, NAS Pensacola, and Patrick SFB. PCS is short for Permanent Change of Station — the military term for moving from one duty station to another.

## How Florida Grades Its Schools

Florida gives every public school and district a single letter grade from A to F each year. The grade comes from the [Florida Department of Education](https://www.fldoe.org/accountability/accountability-reporting/school-grades/) and reflects test scores in reading, math, science, and social studies, plus graduation rates and growth for lower-performing students.

For 2024-25, [28 Florida districts earned an A](https://floridaphoenix.com/2025/07/07/report-card-floridas-highest-and-lowest-rated-school-districts/), 31 earned a B, and 8 earned a C. No districts earned a D or F. Statewide, 44 percent of individual schools earned an A.

A few things to know before you stake a PCS decision on a letter grade:

- **District grade is an average.** Inside a B-rated district, individual schools can range from A to D. Zoning matters more than the district letter.
- **GreatSchools and Niche use different math.** [GreatSchools](https://www.greatschools.org/) leans on test scores. Niche weighs parent reviews and college outcomes. Compare both alongside the state grade.

## The Interstate Compact: Your Built-In Safety Net

The single most important thing PCS parents should know is that Florida participates in the [Interstate Compact on Educational Opportunity for Military Children](https://mic3.net/state/florida/), often shortened to MIC3. All 50 states and DC are members. The Compact is a binding agreement that protects military-connected K-12 students during a PCS so a move does not derail their education.

<Callout type="info" title="What the Interstate Compact does for your kids">

- **Enrollment.** The receiving Florida school must enroll your child using hand-carried unofficial records while official transcripts are in transit. Your child cannot be held out of class waiting on paperwork.
- **Course placement.** Florida schools must honor the courses, grade level, and program your child was in at the sending school, including AP, honors, and CTE tracks.
- **Special education.** Florida must provide [comparable IEP services](https://www.fldoe.org/schools/k-12-public-schools/sss/military-families/) right away while it does its own evaluation. Your child does not lose services during the transition.
- **Gifted and 504 plans.** Same rule. Programs transfer in immediately under comparable terms.
- **Graduation.** Senior-year transfers can use exit exams, course substitutions, or sending-state requirements to graduate on time, even if Florida's requirements differ.
- **Extracurriculars.** Tryout deadlines that have already passed cannot be used to block your child from sports, band, or other activities.

</Callout>

Every Florida district that serves military families has a designated Compact point of contact, and on most installations the [School Liaison Officer](https://www.militaryonesource.mil/education-employment/for-children-youth/) (SLO) is your fastest path to the right person. Call the SLO at your gaining base before you move, not after.

## DoDEA in Florida

The Department of Defense Education Activity (DoDEA) runs schools mostly overseas and on a handful of stateside Army and Marine Corps installations. Florida does **not** have DoDEA-operated schools on its Air Force, Navy, or Space Force bases. Service members' children attend the local public district, a public charter, a private school, or one of Florida's school choice options — and most of the districts below outperform the typical stateside DoDEA school anyway.

## Base-by-Base: Where Military Families Actually Send Their Kids

### Eglin AFB and Hurlburt Field (Niceville and Fort Walton Beach)

**District:** Okaloosa County School District. **District grade:** A. **Military note:** Okaloosa is a [Purple Star District of Distinction](https://midbaynews.com/elections/schools) and serves the highest percentage of military students in Florida.

Okaloosa earned 793 points in the 2024-25 cycle, with [92 percent of schools rated A or B](https://midbaynews.com/elections/schools) and an 88 percent graduation rate.

Standout A-rated schools:

- **Niceville High School** — A-rated, 96 percent graduation rate, strong AP program.
- **Collegiate High School at Northwest Florida State College** (Niceville) — dual-enrollment magnet, A+ rated, ranked roughly 50th in Florida.
- **Okaloosa STEMM Center** (Niceville) — application magnet, A-rated.
- **Edge Elementary** (Niceville) — A-rated, heavy military-family enrollment.
- **Bluewater Bay Elementary** — A-rated, popular with Eglin families.
- **Eglin Elementary** (on-base) — A-rated, 82 percent math proficiency.

Niceville is the school-quality pick. For a deeper look at the area, see our [Eglin AFB neighborhood guide](/blog/best-places-to-live-near-eglin-air-force-base-veteranpcs).

### NAS Pensacola (Pensacola and Gulf Breeze)

**Primary district:** Escambia County (Pensacola). **Preferred district:** Santa Rosa County (Gulf Breeze, Pace, Milton). **Santa Rosa grade:** A.

A huge share of military families live across the Pensacola Bay Bridge in Gulf Breeze and accept a 20 to 25 minute commute to get into Santa Rosa County schools. [Fifteen of 29 Santa Rosa schools earned an A in 2024-25](https://www.wkrg.com/northwest-florida/santa-rosa-county/santa-rosa-county-schools-earn-a-district-rating-and-15-campuses-receive-top-marks/), with reading and math proficiency running about 10 points above the state average.

Standout A-rated schools:

- **Gulf Breeze High School** — A-rated, U.S. News-ranked statewide.
- **Gulf Breeze Middle School** — A-rated.
- **Gulf Breeze Elementary School** — top-ranked in the county.
- **Pensacola Beach Elementary** (charter) — A-rated, small beach community.

Inside Escambia County, ratings vary by neighborhood, but families who want a default-good zoned school usually choose Santa Rosa.

### NAS Jacksonville and NS Mayport

**Districts:** Duval County (Jacksonville), Clay County (Orange Park, Fleming Island), St. Johns County (south of Jacksonville). Clay is an A+ rated district; St. Johns is consistently the [highest-scoring district in Florida](https://floridaphoenix.com/2025/07/07/report-card-floridas-highest-and-lowest-rated-school-districts/). Duval runs the full A-to-D range, so families who want zoned-school certainty mostly choose Clay or St. Johns and drive in.

Clay County standouts (great fit for NAS Jax):

- **Fleming Island High School** — A-rated, [97 percent graduation rate](https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/florida/districts/clay-county-district-schools/fleming-island-high-school-4806), 2025-26 School of Excellence.
- **Fleming Island Elementary** — 93 percent math proficiency on the 2025 FAST assessment.
- **Lakeside Junior High** — strong academics and athletics.

Duval magnet options (application required):

- **Stanton College Preparatory** — top-ranked Florida magnet, IB program.
- **Paxon School for Advanced Studies** — IB program, highly competitive.

For Mayport, Atlantic Beach and Neptune Beach communities feed into Duval schools with smaller class sizes than the urban core.

### MacDill AFB (Tampa)

**Districts:** Hillsborough County (primary), Pinellas County (across the bay). **Hillsborough grade:** A. MacDill sits at the south tip of the Tampa peninsula, which means South Tampa zoning matters more than the district letter.

South Tampa A-rated schools closest to base:

- **Plant High School** — [ranked 36th in Florida](https://www.usnews.com/education/best-high-schools/florida/districts/hillsborough-county-public-schools/plant-high-school-5065), 79 percent AP participation.
- **Mitchell Elementary** — walking distance for many MacDill families.
- **Coleman Middle School** — feeds Plant.

If South Tampa is out of reach: **Newsome High School** (Lithia/FishHawk) and **Steinbrenner High School** (Lutz) are both A-rated. Across the bay in Pinellas, **Palm Harbor University High** and **East Lake High School** are A-rated.

<Callout type="success" title="Best zoned-school value near MacDill">

Brandon and Riverview east of Tampa give military families more house for the same Tampa BAH, with several newer A and B-rated schools. The tradeoff is a 30 to 45 minute commute to the MacDill gate depending on I-75 traffic.

</Callout>

### Patrick SFB (Satellite Beach and Cocoa Beach)

**District:** Brevard Public Schools. **District grade:** A (second consecutive year).

[Brevard earned its second straight A district rating in 2024-25](https://thespacecoastrocket.com/brevard-county-schools-a-rating-2025-grades/), with no schools rated D or F across 110 campuses. Patrick SFB families have one of the better default-school situations in Florida.

Standout A-rated schools:

- **Satellite High School** (Satellite Beach) — A-rated, IB program, walkable beach community.
- **DeLaura Middle School** (Satellite Beach) — A-rated feeder for Satellite High.
- **Holland Elementary** — A-rated, in the heart of the Satellite Beach zone.
- **West Shore Junior/Senior High** (Melbourne) — application magnet, consistently among Florida's top-ranked schools.

Cocoa Beach is the other heavy-military zone, with solid B-rated schools and a short commute to Patrick.

## Special Education, IEPs, and Gifted Programs

An IEP is the legally binding plan a school writes to deliver special education services. A 504 plan provides accommodations without specialized instruction. The Compact protects continuity, but you still have to manage the handoff.

<Callout type="info" title="IEP and gifted-program transfer checklist">

- **Hand-carry copies of everything before you move.** Current IEP, most recent evaluation, progress reports, gifted paperwork, and any private evaluations.
- **Tell the gaining school you are a military family covered by the Compact.** Ask for the district's military point of contact by name.
- **Florida must provide comparable services immediately.** "Comparable" means similar in scope and intensity, not identical. If the offer is materially weaker, get it in writing and escalate to the district MIC3 contact.
- **Florida will do its own evaluation.** Services continue while it runs. You have a right to participate in the new IEP meeting.
- **Gifted identification transfers under the Compact.** Criteria vary by state, so the Compact gives you the right to a re-evaluation, not a guaranteed outcome.

</Callout>

## School Choice: Charters, Virtual, and Scholarships

Florida has one of the most open school-choice systems in the country:

- **Open enrollment.** Most districts let you apply to any school with available seats. Transportation is usually your responsibility.
- **Public charters.** Every metro listed above has charter options. Check the state letter grade, not the marketing.
- **Florida Virtual School (FLVS).** [FLVS](https://www.flvs.net/) is a free, state-run online school. Families use it for full-time enrollment, single-course supplements, or continuity during a mid-year PCS.
- **Family Empowerment Scholarship.** Florida's universal voucher program, administered by [Step Up For Students](https://www.stepupforstudents.org/), can help pay private-school tuition. Military families relocating into Florida are eligible to apply.

## Your Pre-PCS School Checklist

In the 60 days before a move:

- **Pull the current Florida school grade** for every school you are considering, plus the three-year trend.
- **Call the School Liaison Officer** at your gaining base. They handle Compact issues every day.
- **Hand-carry copies** of report cards, transcripts, IEPs, 504 plans, gifted paperwork, and immunization records. Florida's immunization requirements may differ from your current state.
- **Verify the school zone in writing** for any home before you go under contract.
- **Look into Florida Virtual School** as a bridge if your move lands mid-semester.

The families who land smoothly in Florida pick the school first, verify the zone second, and write the offer third. Do it in that order and the rest of the move gets a lot quieter.

*This guide reflects data as of May 2026. Florida school grades and district boundaries change. Verify current grades at [fldoe.org](https://www.fldoe.org/accountability/accountability-reporting/school-grades/), and contact your gaining installation's School Liaison Officer before making enrollment decisions.*
