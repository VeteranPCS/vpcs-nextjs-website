If you have orders for a PCS to Shaw Air Force Base, welcome to the South Carolina Midlands. PCS stands for Permanent Change of Station, the military term for moving from one duty station to another. Shaw AFB sits inside the city limits of Sumter and is home to one of the largest F-16 wings in the Air Force. This 2026 guide covers where to live, how housing pay works, schools, healthcare, and what daily life looks like in Sumter and the surrounding area.

## What and Where Is Shaw Air Force Base?

Shaw Air Force Base is a U.S. Air Force installation located on more than 3,569 acres within the city limits of Sumter, South Carolina, in the heart of the state's Midlands region. According to the [official Shaw AFB fact sheet](https://www.shaw.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/663885/shaw-air-force-base/), Shaw is home to the 20th Fighter Wing (20th FW) and also hosts Headquarters Ninth Air Force, U.S. Air Forces Central, Third Army, and U.S. Army Central (ARCENT).

The [20th Fighter Wing fact sheet](https://www.shaw.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/212636/20th-fighter-wing-fact-sheet/) describes the wing's mission as providing combat-ready airpower and Airmen to meet any challenge, anytime, anywhere. The 20th FW flies the F-16C/D Fighting Falcon and is the host unit at Shaw, responsible for facilities, personnel, and material across the installation. Shaw is home to more than 8,200 active-duty military members, approximately 1,200 civilian employees, and roughly 12,000 family members.

The base was designated Shaw Field in 1941 in honor of 1st Lt. Ervin David Shaw, a Sumter County native killed on a reconnaissance mission over France in World War I. The 20th FW has also supported recent U.S. Central Command operations, including Operation Midnight Hammer.

## Where to Live When PCSing to Shaw AFB

Most families choose between on-post housing and one of the communities surrounding Sumter. Your decision usually comes down to commute time, schools, and budget. Check with your sponsor and the housing office about on-post availability and wait times early in your PCS process.

### Sumter

Sumter is the host city for Shaw AFB, with a commute of about 10 minutes to the main gate. The city has a mix of housing types, from established neighborhoods to newer construction, and daily errands are easy. For families who want the shortest off-post drive and the strongest connection to the military community, Sumter is the natural first choice.

### Dalzell

Dalzell sits just a few miles from the base — also about 10 minutes — and is quieter than Sumter proper. Housing here tends to be among the most affordable close to the gate, with a neighborhood feel that many families prefer.

### Lugoff

Lugoff is in Kershaw County, roughly 30 minutes northwest of Shaw. It offers newer subdivisions and a more rural setting for families who want more land without commuting all the way to Columbia.

### Camden

Camden is the Kershaw County seat, about 35 minutes from the gate. It is an older South Carolina town with a historic downtown, good schools, and a community feel distinct from typical suburban options. It also sits midway between Shaw and Columbia, which suits families with commutes in both directions.

### Columbia

Columbia is the state capital, about 45 minutes from Shaw, and offers a full metro — the University of South Carolina, a major medical center, and a wide range of dining and retail. Families willing to accept a longer drive often prioritize the job market for spouses or city amenities. ## Shaw AFB BAH and Cost of Living

If you live off post, the military helps cover your housing through BAH, which stands for Basic Allowance for Housing. BAH is a monthly, tax-free payment. The amount depends on three things: your rank, whether you have dependents, and your duty station's Military Housing Area, or MHA. An MHA is the geographic zone the Department of Defense uses to set local housing pay.

Because rates change each year and vary by rank and dependent status, we do not publish a rate table here. To find your exact figure, use the official DoD calculator at [travel.dod.mil BAH Rate Lookup](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/). Enter your rank and ZIP code to see the current rate. Always confirm yours before signing a lease or making an offer.

For a sense of buying costs, the typical home value in Sumter was about $175,127 in June 2026, according to [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/41128/sumter-sc/). That is a typical value, not a median sale price. Sumter is one of the more affordable housing markets in South Carolina, which gives VA-loan buyers meaningful purchasing power. As of the week of June 18, 2026, [Freddie Mac](https://www.freddiemac.com/pmms) reported the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate at 6.47 percent. Whether buying or renting makes more sense depends on how long you expect to be stationed here, so run the numbers for your own situation.

## Schools Near Shaw AFB

The schools your children attend depend on where you live.

[Sumter School District](https://www.sumterschools.net/military) serves Sumter County and has earned Purple Star School District recognition for its commitment to military families. It maintains a dedicated military family liaison and a school liaison program manager from Shaw AFB, making mid-year enrollment and deployment transitions smoother.

Families in Lugoff or Camden fall under Kershaw County schools. Those who live in Columbia fall under Richland County School District One or Two, depending on the specific address. Confirm the assigned school for any home before you commit — attendance zones can shift.

## Healthcare: 20th Medical Group

On-post care at Shaw AFB is provided by the [20th Medical Group](https://shaw.tricare.mil/), which offers primary care, public health, operational medicine, and family advocacy for eligible patients.

Your coverage runs through TRICARE, the health care program for service members and their families. Set up your plan early, update DEERS, and request any referrals through the [20th MDG appointments and referrals page](https://shaw.tricare.mil/Getting-Care/Appointments-Referrals) before you arrive so there is no gap in care.

## Climate and Things to Do

Sumter sits in a humid subtropical climate, with hot and humid summers and mild winters that occasionally bring ice. The [National Weather Service Columbia office](https://www.weather.gov/cae/) covers Sumter County — check it for monthly normals before your move. If you are coming from a drier or cooler region, expect July and August humidity to be an adjustment.

The area has more to offer than newcomers often expect. [Swan Lake Iris Gardens](https://www.sumtersc.gov/tourism/things-do/parks-trails/swan-lake-iris-gardens) in Sumter is the only public park in the United States to feature all eight swan species. The 150-acre grounds include more than 120 varieties of Japanese iris, a cypress boardwalk that extends roughly 1,000 feet into the swamp, and free admission year-round.

[Poinsett State Park](https://southcarolinaparks.com/poinsett) in Sumter County sits where the Sandhills meet the coastal plain. It has trails that link to the Palmetto Trail, a 10-acre lake for fishing, and tent and RV camping. Lake Marion, one of the largest lakes in South Carolina, is within easy driving range and is known for bass fishing and boating.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What MHA is Shaw AFB in for BAH?

Shaw AFB falls within its own Military Housing Area for BAH purposes. Your rate depends on your rank and whether you have dependents. Look up your exact figure with the [DoD BAH calculator](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/) using your rank and ZIP code.

### Where do military families live near Shaw AFB?

Common choices include Sumter and Dalzell for short commutes, Lugoff and Camden for a more rural or historic-town feel, and Columbia for full metro amenities with a longer drive. On-post housing is also an option; ask your sponsor about availability and wait times.

### What aircraft does the 20th Fighter Wing fly?

The 20th Fighter Wing flies the F-16C/D Fighting Falcon. According to the official wing fact sheet, the 20th FW is the host unit at Shaw and provides combat-ready airpower worldwide.

### Which school district serves Shaw AFB families?

Sumter County families fall under Sumter School District (Purple Star recognized). Kershaw County families fall under the Kershaw County schools. Columbia-area families fall under Richland County School District One or Two depending on address. Confirm the assigned school before committing to any address.

### Where do I get healthcare at Shaw AFB?

On-post care is provided by the 20th Medical Group at [shaw.tricare.mil](https://shaw.tricare.mil/). Set up your TRICARE plan early, update DEERS, and request referrals before you arrive so your coverage continues without a gap.

### How far is Sumter from Columbia?

Sumter is about 45 miles east of Columbia — roughly a 45-minute drive — and Columbia provides the nearest major airport, hospitals, and full metro retail and dining.
