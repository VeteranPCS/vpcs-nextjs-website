There is a common belief that a realtor's main job is to show up and unlock the front door. With so many listings online, you might wonder whether you really need an agent at all. After all, you can find the homes yourself, right?

Here is the truth. Opening doors is the most visible part of the job, but it is also the smallest. The real work happens behind the scenes, where a good agent guides you through a complicated process, negotiates on your behalf, reads the market, and keeps the paperwork straight. For military families moving on a tight timeline, that support can be the difference between a smooth closing and a stressful scramble.

## A realtor does far more than open doors

Buying a home is not one task. It is dozens of smaller tasks that all have to line up. Your agent helps you set a smart offer price, spots problems before they become expensive, and manages the back-and-forth between you, the seller, the lender, and the title company. They track deadlines, flag red flags, and keep the deal moving.

Setting up showings is just the part you see. Think of it like a deployment. The mission brief is short and visible, but the planning, logistics, and coordination behind it carry the real weight. A skilled agent carries that weight so you can focus on your move and your family.

## Local knowledge you cannot get from a listing photo

A listing can tell you the square footage and the asking price. It cannot tell you whether that price is fair, whether the neighborhood is on the way up, or whether a quiet street floods every spring. Your agent can.

Good realtors know local market trends, recent sale prices, and the small details that separate one block from another. They understand local rules and can read the history behind a property. That knowledge helps you price an offer correctly and avoid homes with hidden issues. ## Deals close more often when both sides have representation

Here is something many buyers do not realize. A deal is far more likely to actually close when both the buyer and the seller have professional representation. That is not a coincidence.

When each side has an agent, problems get handled early instead of blowing up at the last minute. Your agent negotiates on your behalf, addresses concerns before they stall the deal, and steers the contract through its legal requirements. A home purchase has a lot of moving parts, and an experienced guide keeps them from falling apart.

## Why this matters more after the NAR settlement

The real estate industry is changing. After a major legal settlement involving the National Association of Realtors (NAR), the way agents are paid and how those costs are explained have become more transparent. Buyers now have clearer conversations about commissions up front.

That transparency is a good thing, but it also means you should understand exactly what you are getting for your money. The answer, in most cases, is a lot. A trusted agent who earns their fee through negotiation, market insight, and protection from costly mistakes is more valuable than ever, not less.

## What representation actually protects you from

When people skip an agent, they often picture the money they will save. What they miss is the risk they take on. A few real examples of where representation pays off:

- For Sale By Owner homes, often called FSBOs, tend to sit on the market longer and sell for less than agent-listed homes. Going it alone as a buyer can leave you without a guide through the same complicated steps.
- Contract knowledge matters more than most people think. Who holds the earnest money deposit? What happens if a dispute comes up? Your agent knows the answers before you need them.
- Strong representation can keep a disagreement from turning into mediation or a legal fight, which saves you time, stress, and money.
- Contracts come with deadlines and fine print, from homeowners association (HOA) assessments to surprise repairs. An agent makes sure nothing slips past you.
- During the home evaluation, an agent helps you catch unpermitted work or poorly done do-it-yourself projects and then negotiates repairs so you are not stuck with the bill later.

## Why a military-experienced agent is worth even more

For a military homebuyer, the right agent does all of the above and then adds a layer of expertise that civilian buyers never need. A military-experienced, VA-savvy agent understands the moving parts unique to your life.

They know how your Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH), the monthly housing stipend the military provides, fits into your budget. They understand the VA loan, the home loan backed by the Department of Veterans Affairs, and how to keep that financing on track. And they respect the tight timelines of a Permanent Change of Station (PCS) move, where you often have weeks, not months, to find a home.
