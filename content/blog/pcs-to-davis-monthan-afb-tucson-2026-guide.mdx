If you have orders for a PCS to Davis-Monthan Air Force Base, welcome to Tucson, Arizona. PCS stands for Permanent Change of Station — the military term for relocating from one duty station to another. Davis-Monthan AFB sits inside the Tucson city limits and is home to an active combat wing, a rescue group, and the world's largest aircraft storage facility. This 2026 guide covers where to live, how housing pay works, schools, healthcare, and daily life in the Sonoran Desert.

## What and Where Is Davis-Monthan AFB?

Davis-Monthan Air Force Base is located in Tucson, Pima County, Arizona, and is part of Air Combat Command (ACC). It is home to the [355th Wing](https://www.dm.af.mil/About-DM/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/666235/davis-monthan-355th-wing-fact-sheet/), which carries a two-word mission: **RESCUE & ATTACK!** According to the official base fact sheet, the wing trains and deploys A-10C Thunderbolt II pilots and operates EC-130H Compass Calls, HC-130J Combat Kings, HH-60W Jolly Green IIs, and F-16 Fighting Falcons. The [563rd Rescue Group](https://www.dm.af.mil/About-DM/Units/563rd-Rescue-Group/) — which provides combat search and rescue (CSAR) capability — was realigned under the 355th Wing in 2019. The wing's vision, per dm.af.mil, is to remain a "highly lethal and agile team, always ready to deploy, defend the base, and generate combat power."

A note on the A-10: the Air Force has been working toward phasing out the A-10C fleet as part of broader modernization. The dm.af.mil fact sheet continues to list A-10C training and deployment as the 355th Wing's primary role. If your assignment involves the A-10 program, confirm current fleet status with your gaining unit.

Davis-Monthan is also home to the [309th Aerospace Maintenance and Regeneration Group (AMARG)](https://www.dm.af.mil/About-DM/Units/Mission-Partners/309-AMARG/), known as "The Boneyard" — a 2,600-acre facility and the largest aircraft storage and regeneration facility in the world, maintaining more than 3,400 aircraft for all U.S. military branches.

## Where to Live When PCSing to Davis-Monthan AFB

Because DMAFB sits inside Tucson, you have a wide range of off-base options. Ask your gaining unit about on-base availability and wait times before committing.

### Tucson (Central and East Side)

Neighborhoods on the east and southeast side of Tucson sit closest to the base, often within 15 minutes. You are in the city, with restaurants, shopping, and entertainment nearby. Neighborhoods vary block by block, so touring in person or talking to a local agent helps.

### Vail

About 20 minutes southeast, Vail is known for newer construction and the well-regarded Vail Unified School District. It draws many military families with school-age children who want a manageable commute and strong schools.

### Sahuarita

About 25 minutes south, Sahuarita offers newer master-planned neighborhoods and lower housing costs than central Tucson. Families who want more space for the money often look here first.

### Oro Valley

At the northern edge of the metro, about 30 minutes out, Oro Valley consistently ranks among the most desirable communities in southern Arizona: strong schools, low crime, and a quieter suburban feel. The longer drive is the main trade-off.

### Marana

Also about 30 minutes out, Marana sits northwest of Tucson along I-10 and has grown considerably in recent years. It offers newer housing and a manageable commute for families who want to be outside the city core.

## Davis-Monthan AFB BAH and Cost of Living

If you live off base, the military helps cover your housing through BAH, which stands for Basic Allowance for Housing. BAH is a monthly, tax-free payment. The amount depends on your rank, whether you have dependents, and your duty station's Military Housing Area, or MHA. Davis-Monthan AFB falls within the Tucson, Arizona MHA.

Because rates change each year and vary by rank and dependent status, we do not publish a rate table here. Find your exact figure with the official DoD calculator: [travel.dod.mil BAH Rate Lookup](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/). Always confirm before signing a lease or making an offer.

For a sense of buying costs, the typical home value in Tucson was $326,242 as of May 31, 2026, according to [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/7481/tucson-az/) — a typical value, not a median sale price. As of the week of June 18, 2026, [Freddie Mac](https://www.freddiemac.com/pmms) reported the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate at 6.47 percent. Tucson's typical values are lower than many other Sun Belt metros, which can work in your favor with a VA loan — the home loan backed by the Department of Veterans Affairs. ## Schools Near Davis-Monthan AFB

The schools your children attend depend on where you live off base.

[Tucson Unified School District (TUSD)](https://www.tusd1.org/) is southern Arizona's largest district, serving roughly 47,000 students in 89 schools across much of central and east Tucson. [Vail Unified School District](https://www.vailschooldistrict.org/) serves the Vail community and is consistently well regarded. [Amphitheater Public Schools](https://www.amphi.com/) covers north Tucson and Oro Valley with about 11,500 students in 22 schools. [Marana Unified School District](https://www.maranausd.org/) serves the northwest corridor and reached its centennial in 2026.

Boundaries can shift, so confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit.

## Healthcare: 355th Medical Group

On-base medical care is centered at the [355th Medical Group](https://davismonthan.tricare.mil/), which provides health services for active-duty airmen, their families, and eligible retirees. The facility is at 4175 South Alamo Avenue, open Monday through Friday, 7:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m., closed for training Wednesday mornings from 7 to 9:15 a.m.

Coverage runs through TRICARE, the health care program for service members and families. Set up your plan early — update DEERS, transfer coverage, and request referrals before you arrive. The MHS Nurse Advice Line is available 24/7 at 1-800-TRICARE (874-2273), option 1.

## Climate and Things to Do in Tucson

Tucson sits at roughly 2,400 feet in the Sonoran Desert. Summers are hot — highs regularly exceed 100°F from June into July — and then monsoon season arrives. According to the [National Weather Service Tucson](https://www.weather.gov/twc/monsoon), the official monsoon season runs June 15 through September 30. Afternoon thunderstorms, dust storms called haboobs, and flash flooding are all part of the pattern. Dry washes fill quickly; take flash flood warnings seriously. The rains also cool temperatures and turn the desert green. Outside of summer, the weather is sunny, mild, and comfortable.

[Saguaro National Park](https://www.nps.gov/sagu/) wraps around both the east and west sides of the city, protecting more than 165 miles of trails through stands of giant saguaro cactus, some over 200 years old. Mount Lemmon in the Santa Catalinas rises to nearly 9,200 feet, offering year-round hiking and occasional winter snow less than an hour from downtown. [Sabino Canyon Recreation Area](https://www.fs.usda.gov/coronado/) features shaded canyon hikes and the popular Seven Falls destination. The [Pima Air and Space Museum](https://www.pimaair.org/), right outside the base fence, displays more than 275 aircraft and offers bus tours of the AMARG Boneyard — a natural first stop for any aviation family.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What MHA or area is Davis-Monthan AFB in for BAH?

Davis-Monthan AFB falls within the Tucson, Arizona Military Housing Area for BAH purposes. Your rate depends on your rank and dependent status. Use the official [DoD BAH calculator](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/) with your rank and ZIP code to find the current figure.

### Where do military families live near Davis-Monthan AFB?

East and central Tucson offer the shortest commute. Vail draws families for its school district and newer housing. Sahuarita offers more space at lower prices. Oro Valley and Marana are popular for a suburban setting, with a longer drive. On-base housing is also an option — ask your gaining unit about wait times.

### What is the A-10 fleet status at Davis-Monthan?

The dm.af.mil fact sheet lists A-10C training and deployment as the 355th Wing's primary role. The Air Force has been phasing out the A-10 fleet as part of modernization. If your assignment involves the A-10 program, confirm current status with your gaining unit before you arrive.

### Which school district serves Davis-Monthan AFB families?

It depends on your address. Tucson Unified covers central and east Tucson; Vail Unified serves the Vail area; Amphitheater covers north Tucson and Oro Valley; Marana Unified serves the northwest. Confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit.

### Where do I get healthcare at Davis-Monthan AFB?

The [355th Medical Group](https://davismonthan.tricare.mil/) provides on-base primary and specialty care. Set up your TRICARE plan, update DEERS, and arrange referrals before you arrive. The MHS Nurse Advice Line (1-800-874-2273, option 1) is available 24/7.

### What should I know about Tucson's monsoon season?

The official monsoon season runs June 15 through September 30, per [NWS Tucson](https://www.weather.gov/twc/monsoon). Expect afternoon thunderstorms, dust storms, and flash flooding from July through September. Take flash flood warnings in dry washes seriously. The rains also bring cooler evenings and green desert scenery that most residents look forward to.
