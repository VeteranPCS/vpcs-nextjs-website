Mississippi has a strong and steady military presence, much of it along the Gulf Coast. The state is home to **six major active installations**, supporting the **Air Force, Navy, and Army National Guard**, plus reserve and tenant units that train and operate alongside them. Whether you are **PCSing to Mississippi** (Permanent Change of Station, the military term for an official move to a new duty station), thinking about a move, or just curious about military history, this guide gives you a clear overview of the **key military bases** in the state.

## Overview of Military Installations in Mississippi

Mississippi military bases cover a wide range of missions. They include **technical training, weather reconnaissance into hurricanes, undergraduate pilot training, Seabee construction operations, ocean and weather science, and large-scale Army maneuver training**. The state trains and supports thousands of service members from every branch, and it keeps many military families moving in and out each year on PCS orders.

### Active Military Bases in Mississippi

- **Keesler Air Force Base**
- **Columbus Air Force Base**
- **Naval Construction Battalion Center Gulfport**
- **Naval Air Station Meridian**
- **Stennis Space Center (Naval Meteorology and Oceanography Command)**
- **Camp Shelby Joint Forces Training Center**

## Air Force Bases in Mississippi

### Keesler Air Force Base

Keesler AFB, in **Biloxi** on the Gulf Coast, is the **"Electronics Training Center of Excellence"** for the Air Force. Its host unit, the **81st Training Wing**, runs technical training for tens of thousands of students each year across many career fields, teaching not only Air Force members but also Army, Navy, Marine Corps, Coast Guard, and federal civilian personnel. Keesler is also home to the **403rd Wing**, the Air Force Reserve unit known as the **"Hurricane Hunters,"** which flies into tropical storms and hurricanes to gather weather data for the National Hurricane Center.

**Key Missions:**

- Technical training in electronics, cyber, and other career fields
- Weather reconnaissance flights into hurricanes
- Joint training for members of all the services

Learn more on the [Keesler Air Force Base website](https://www.keesler.af.mil/) and the [403rd Wing Hurricane Hunters page](https://www.403wg.afrc.af.mil/).

### Columbus Air Force Base

Columbus AFB, in **Columbus** in the northeast of the state, is home to the **14th Flying Training Wing**, part of Air Education and Training Command. Its mission is to **"Train World Class Pilots."** The wing runs the **52-week Specialized Undergraduate Pilot Training** program, which student pilots must complete before earning their wings. The flying mission is carried out by the **14th Operations Group** and its squadrons.

**Key Missions:**

- Specialized Undergraduate Pilot Training
- Developing mission-ready Air Force pilots
- Flight training operations and support

See the official [Columbus Air Force Base website](https://www.columbus.af.mil/) and the [Columbus AETC fact sheet](https://www.aetc.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/558875/columbus-air-force-base/).

## Navy Bases in Mississippi

### Naval Construction Battalion Center Gulfport

NCBC Gulfport, in **Gulfport** on the Gulf Coast, is **home to the Atlantic Fleet Seabees**, the Navy's construction force. The base exists to maintain facilities and provide support to **Naval Construction Force units** that deploy from or are homeported there, and it hosts dozens of tenant commands. Gulfport is the base for the **20th Seabee Readiness Group**, which helps man, train, and equip Seabee units, and it is also home to the **Naval Construction Training Center**, where new Seabees learn their trade.

**Key Missions:**

- Support and home port for Atlantic Fleet Seabee units
- Training for the Naval Construction Force
- Readiness and equipping of deploying Seabee battalions

Learn more on the [NCBC Gulfport installation page](https://cnrse.cnic.navy.mil/Installations/NCBC-Gulfport/) and the [Naval Construction Training Center Gulfport page](https://www.netc.navy.mil/NCTCGP/).

### Naval Air Station Meridian

NAS Meridian, northeast of **Meridian**, is a Navy jet training base. It is home to **Training Air Wing One** and its training squadrons, where student naval aviators complete **intermediate and advanced jet training** on their way to earning their wings. The station also hosts the **Naval Technical Training Center**, which runs schoolhouse training for sailors. The area's open airspace and good flying weather make it a strong place to learn to fly.

**Key Missions:**

- Intermediate and advanced naval jet pilot training
- Technical training for Navy sailors
- Support for student aviators and tenant commands

See the official [NAS Meridian installation page](https://cnrse.cnic.navy.mil/Installations/NAS-Meridian/) and the [Training Air Wing One page at NAS Meridian](https://www.cnatra.navy.mil/tw1/nas-meridian.asp).

### Stennis Space Center

Stennis Space Center, in **Hancock County** in south Mississippi, is a federal site that NASA operates and that hosts many Navy tenants. It is home to the **Naval Meteorology and Oceanography Command**, which oversees thousands of personnel who collect and use ocean and weather information to support fleet and joint commanders. The **Naval Oceanographic Office**, also based there, provides oceanographic products and services across the Department of Defense.

**Key Missions:**

- Naval meteorology and oceanography operations
- Ocean and weather data to support fleet and joint commanders
- Oceanographic products and services for the Department of Defense

Learn more on the [Naval Meteorology and Oceanography Command website](https://www.metoc.navy.mil/) and the [Naval Oceanographic Office page](https://www.cnmoc.usff.navy.mil/navoceano/).

## Army Bases in Mississippi

### Camp Shelby Joint Forces Training Center

Camp Shelby, near **Hattiesburg** in south Mississippi, is a major training site run by the Mississippi Army National Guard. Spread across more than **134,000 acres**, it can support battalion-level maneuver training and has served almost continuously as a training ground since **1917**. It is home to the **177th Armored Brigade (Training Support)**, whose soldiers mentor National Guard and Reserve units that come to Camp Shelby to train on systems such as the M1 Abrams tank and the Bradley Fighting Vehicle.

**Key Missions:**

- Large-scale Army maneuver and weapons training
- Training support for National Guard and Reserve units
- Mobilization and readiness operations

See the official [Camp Shelby Joint Forces Training Center page](https://ms.ng.mil/installations/shelby/Pages/default.aspx) and the [Camp Shelby training page](https://ms.ng.mil/installations/shelby/training/Pages/default.aspx).

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### 1. What is the largest military base in Mississippi?

**Keesler Air Force Base**, in Biloxi, is the largest and best-known military base in Mississippi. It is the Air Force's "Electronics Training Center of Excellence" and trains tens of thousands of students each year, and it is also home to the 403rd Wing "Hurricane Hunters."

### 2. How many military bases are in Mississippi?

Mississippi is home to **six major active installations**, supporting the Air Force, Navy, and Army National Guard, along with many reserve and tenant units.

### 3. What is the main Air Force base in Mississippi?

**Keesler Air Force Base** in Biloxi is the Air Force's main hub in Mississippi for technical training and weather reconnaissance. **Columbus Air Force Base** in the northeast of the state is the main pilot training base.

### 4. What is the main Navy presence in Mississippi?

**Naval Construction Battalion Center Gulfport** is the main Navy installation, home to the Atlantic Fleet Seabees. **Naval Air Station Meridian** trains Navy jet pilots, and **Stennis Space Center** hosts the Naval Meteorology and Oceanography Command.
