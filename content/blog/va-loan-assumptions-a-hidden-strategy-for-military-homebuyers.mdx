When PCS orders arrive and you're facing today's high mortgage rates, every dollar counts. While most military families focus on traditional VA Loan benefits, there's a lesser-known strategy that could save you thousands: assuming an existing VA loan from another service member or veteran.

Here's how VA loan assumptions work and why they might be your smartest move in today's market.

## What Is a VA Loan Assumption?

A VA loan assumption allows you to take over an existing VA mortgage from the current homeowner, inheriting their interest rate, loan balance, and payment terms. The [Department of Veterans Affairs](https://www.va.gov/housing-assistance/home-loans/assumable-loans/) states that VA loans are assumable by qualified buyers, including non-veterans.

This means if you're buying from a fellow service member who secured a VA loan at 2.75% in 2020, you could potentially assume that rate instead of getting a new loan at today's higher rates.

## Why Military Families Should Consider VA Loan Assumptions

**Lock in Lower Interest Rates**

The [Consumer Financial Protection Bureau](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/about-us/blog/servicemembers-have-debt-collection-rights/) reports that even a 1% difference in interest rates can save homebuyers hundreds of dollars monthly. With VA loans issued between 2020-2022 carrying rates around 2-3%, assuming one of these loans could slash your monthly payments compared to current rates.

**Skip Private Mortgage Insurance**

Unlike conventional loans, VA loans never require PMI, regardless of your down payment. When you assume a VA loan, you maintain this benefit even if you're not a veteran yourself.

**Reduced Closing Costs**

VA loan assumptions typically involve fewer fees than originating a new mortgage. The main cost is the 0.5% funding fee, significantly less than traditional closing costs.

**Faster Process for PCS Timelines**

Military families often face tight PCS timelines. VA loan assumptions can close faster than new loan applications since much of the underwriting legwork is already complete.

## How Military Homebuyers Qualify for VA Loan Assumptions

To assume a VA loan, you must meet the lender's qualification standards, which typically include:

- Credit score of 620 or higher (requirements vary by lender)
- Stable income with acceptable debt-to-income ratio
- Proof of employment or military orders
- Completion of a formal assumption application

[Military OneSource](https://www.militaryonesource.mil/financial-legal/personal-finance/) recommends getting pre-qualified before house hunting to streamline the assumption process.

## Understanding the Seller's VA Entitlement

When assuming a VA loan, it's important to understand how this affects the selling service member's benefits. Unless you're a veteran who can substitute your entitlement, the seller's VA loan benefit remains tied to the assumed loan.

For selling military families, this means their full VA entitlement won't be restored until the assumed loan is paid off. However, they may still have remaining entitlement for another VA loan, depending on their circumstances.

## The VA Loan Assumption Process for Military Families

**Step 1: Find Properties with Assumable VA Loans**

Work with a VeteranPCS agent who understands military moves and can identify homes with assumable VA loans near your new duty station.

**Step 2: Contact the Current Lender**

Your agent will help contact the seller's lender to confirm the loan is assumable and request assumption requirements.

**Step 3: Submit Your Assumption Package**

Provide financial documentation similar to a new loan application, including military orders, LES statements, and credit information.

**Step 4: Wait for Approval**

The assumption approval process typically takes 30-45 days, faster than most new loan applications.

**Step 5: Close and Take Over Payments**

Once approved, you'll close on the assumption and begin making payments on the existing loan terms.

## Real-World Benefits for Military Homebuyers

Consider this scenario: You're PCSing to Fort Liberty and find a home where the seller has a VA loan at 2.8% interest on a $300,000 balance. Current VA loan rates are 6.5%. By assuming the loan instead of getting new financing, you could save approximately $675 per month in payments.

Over a 30-year loan term, that's more than $240,000 in savings—money that could fund your children's education, boost retirement savings, or provide financial security during your military career.

## Important Considerations for Service Members

**Credit Requirements**

While VA loan assumptions may have more flexible credit requirements than conventional loans, lenders still evaluate your creditworthiness. The [Federal Trade Commission](https://www.consumer.ftc.gov/articles/free-credit-reports) recommends checking your credit report before applying.

**Equity Considerations**

You'll need to pay the seller any equity they have in the home. If they owe $250,000 but the home is worth $300,000, you'll need $50,000 cash for their equity plus any down payment required by the lender.

**Future VA Loan Use**

If you're a veteran assuming another veteran's loan, work with your lender to substitute your entitlement for theirs, preserving their ability to use VA benefits again.

## Making VA Loan Assumptions Work for Your PCS Move

The key to successful VA loan assumptions is starting early and working with professionals who understand military moves. Begin researching assumable loans in your new duty location as soon as you receive orders.

Consider these factors when evaluating assumable VA loans:

- Interest rate savings versus current market rates
- Remaining loan balance and monthly payments
- Home condition and location relative to your new base
- Seller's equity requirements
- Timeline compatibility with your PCS date

## Next Steps for Military Homebuyers

VA loan assumptions aren't right for every situation, but they can provide substantial savings for military families navigating today's challenging housing market. The combination of low interest rates, no PMI, and reduced closing costs makes this strategy particularly attractive during high-rate periods.

Start by discussing assumable VA loans with your real estate agent and lender. They can help identify opportunities and guide you through the process while keeping your PCS timeline on track.
