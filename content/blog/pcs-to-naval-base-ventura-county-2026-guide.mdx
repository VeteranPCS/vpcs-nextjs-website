If you have orders for a PCS to Naval Base Ventura County, welcome to the Southern California coast. PCS stands for Permanent Change of Station, the military term for a government-directed move between duty stations. Naval Base Ventura County — known as NBVC — sits between Oxnard and the Pacific Ocean, about 60 miles northwest of Los Angeles. This 2026 guide covers where to live, how housing pay works, schools, healthcare, and life along the Ventura County coast.

Moving to a new installation is a lot. Our goal is to make the NBVC part feel manageable.

## What and Where Is Naval Base Ventura County?

Naval Base Ventura County is a U.S. Navy installation that combines two main operating facilities — [Naval Air Station Point Mugu](https://cnrsw.cnic.navy.mil/Installations/NAVBASE-Ventura-County/About/) and Naval Construction Battalion Center Port Hueneme — along with a third facility, San Nicolas Island, located about 60 miles offshore in the Channel Islands. Together, the three sites cover roughly 19,470 acres, according to the [Commander, Navy Installations Command](https://cnrsw.cnic.navy.mil/Installations/NAVBASE-Ventura-County/About/).

NBVC is home to more than 80 tenant commands and departments that employ over 20,000 military and civilian personnel. According to [CNIC](https://cnrsw.cnic.navy.mil/Installations/NAVBASE-Ventura-County/About/), the installation describes its mission as supporting both Fleet and Fighter, with three warfare centers operating on the base: the Naval Air Warfare Center Weapons Division (NAWCWD) at Point Mugu, the Naval Surface Warfare Center Port Hueneme Division, and the Naval Facilities Engineering and Expeditionary Warfare Center (NAVFAC EXWC).

Point Mugu is the home of the West Coast E-2C Hawkeye squadrons, the airborne early warning aircraft that provide radar coverage for carrier strike groups. The [NAWCWD at Point Mugu](https://www.navair.navy.mil/PtMugu) manages the Point Mugu Sea Range and serves as the Navy's center of electronic warfare expertise. Port Hueneme is the home of the Pacific Seabees — Construction Battalions (CB) — who deploy worldwide to build infrastructure in support of naval and joint operations.

For a broader look at military installations across the state, see our guide to [what military bases are in California](/blog/what-military-bases-are-in-california).

## Where to Live When PCSing to NBVC

Most families who move to NBVC live either in on-post housing or in one of the surrounding communities. Your choice usually comes down to commute, schools, budget, and what kind of neighborhood feel you want.

*Approximate one-way drive times to NBVC. Times vary with traffic and which gate you use.*

### Port Hueneme

Port Hueneme is the closest city to the base, with a commute of roughly five minutes to the main gate. The city sits directly adjacent to the naval construction battalion center and has a tight-knit, working waterfront character. Many Seabee families and those attached to the Port Hueneme side choose it for the convenience and the short drive. Housing options range from established single-family homes to rentals close to the beach.

### Oxnard

Oxnard, about 10 minutes from the main gate, is the largest city in Ventura County by population. It offers the widest range of housing options, including newer subdivisions, older established neighborhoods, and rentals at a range of price points. According to [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/13150/oxnard-ca/), the typical home value in Oxnard was about $704,329 in June 2026 — that is a typical value, not a median sale price. Families who want more options and reasonable commute times often land here.

### Camarillo

Camarillo sits about 15 minutes from the base, inland along U.S. Highway 101. It tends to draw families who want a quieter suburban setting, highly rated schools, and a bit more space. [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/51239/camarillo-ca/) reported a typical Camarillo home value of about $847,145 in June 2026 — again, a typical value, not a median sale price. The [Pleasant Valley School District](https://www.pleasantvalleysd.org/) serves the K–8 grades in much of Camarillo, and Camarillo High School falls under Oxnard Union High School District.

### Ventura

The city of Ventura, about 20 minutes from the base, is a popular choice for families who want a beach-city feel with access to shops, restaurants, and parks. The waterfront and Ventura Harbor are nearby, and the drive to NBVC is straightforward. Housing costs run comparable to Camarillo, varying by distance from the beach.

### Newbury Park and Thousand Oaks

Newbury Park, roughly 25 minutes from the base, and Thousand Oaks, about 30 minutes away, sit inland in the Conejo Valley. Both offer highly rated schools, newer neighborhoods, and a suburban feel at the cost of a longer Highway 101 commute and higher home prices.

## NBVC BAH and the Cost of Living

If you live off base, the military helps cover housing costs through BAH, which stands for Basic Allowance for Housing. BAH is a monthly, tax-free payment tied to three things: your rank, whether you have dependents, and your duty station's Military Housing Area, or MHA. An MHA is the geographic zone the Department of Defense uses to set local housing pay. NBVC falls within the Oxnard-Ventura, California MHA.

Because rates change annually and vary by rank and dependent status, we do not publish a rate table here. To find your current figure, use the official DoD BAH calculator at [travel.dod.mil BAH Rate Lookup](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/). Enter your rank and the ZIP code for the area you plan to live in to see the current rate. Always confirm your rate before signing a lease or making an offer.

The Ventura County housing market is one of the more expensive in California. For home-buying context, [Freddie Mac](https://www.freddiemac.com/pmms) reported the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate at 6.47 percent as of the week of June 18, 2026. Whether buying makes more sense than renting for your family depends on how long you expect to be stationed here and your financial situation. ## Schools Near NBVC

The schools your children attend depend on where you live in Ventura County. A few districts are most relevant for NBVC families.

[Hueneme Elementary School District](https://www.hueneme.org/) serves grades K–8 in Port Hueneme and portions of Oxnard. For the 2026 school year, the district operates 11 public schools serving more than 7,000 students, according to data from the [National Center for Education Statistics](https://nces.ed.gov/ccd/districtsearch/district_detail.asp?Search=2&ID2=0617850&DistrictID=0617850&details=1). For high school, those students feed into [Oxnard Union High School District](https://www.ouhsd.k12.ca.us/), which serves high school students from Port Hueneme, Oxnard, Camarillo, and the surrounding unincorporated areas.

Families living in Camarillo are typically served by [Pleasant Valley School District](https://www.pleasantvalleysd.org/) for K–8 grades, with high school students attending Camarillo High School under the Oxnard Union High School District.

School boundary lines can change, so confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit to a home or rental.

## Healthcare: Naval Branch Health Clinics

Medical care at NBVC is provided through two facilities. The [Naval Branch Health Clinic Port Hueneme](https://camp-pendleton.tricare.mil/Clinics/Branch-Health-Clinic-Port-Hueneme) operates Monday through Friday from 7:30 a.m. to 4:00 p.m. and is located at 162 First Street, Building 1402, Port Hueneme. The [Naval Branch Health Clinic Point Mugu](https://www.tricare.mil/GettingCare/FindDoctor/MTF/Facilities/Naval-Branch-Health-Clinic-Point-Mugu) also serves eligible beneficiaries on the Point Mugu side of the installation. Both clinics fall under the administrative umbrella of Naval Hospital Camp Pendleton. Appointments are made through the MHS GENESIS Patient Portal.

Your coverage runs through TRICARE, the Defense Department's health care program for service members, their dependents, and eligible retirees. Make sure your information is current in DEERS — the Defense Enrollment Eligibility Reporting System — before you arrive. Choose or transfer your TRICARE plan early and arrange referrals before you need them so there is no gap in care.

## Climate and Things to Do

NBVC sits in one of the most temperate climates in the country. According to the [National Weather Service Los Angeles/Oxnard office](https://www.weather.gov/lox/), the coast has a mild Mediterranean climate: warm, dry summers, brief mild winters, and morning fog common in late spring and early summer. Average highs run from the mid-60s in winter to the upper 70s Fahrenheit in summer, with very little extreme heat thanks to the marine layer.

The outdoor life around NBVC is a genuine draw for military families. [Channel Islands National Park](https://www.nps.gov/chis/) protects five islands and their surrounding ocean environment just off the coast. The park's visitor center is at 1901 Spinnaker Drive in Ventura, and Island Packers provides boat transportation from Ventura Harbor and Channel Islands Harbor in Oxnard. The islands offer hiking, camping, kayaking, snorkeling, and wildlife viewing for species found nowhere else.

On the mainland, the Ventura and Oxnard beaches are accessible within minutes of the base. For inland adventure, [Los Padres National Forest](https://www.fs.usda.gov/lpnf) covers nearly two million acres of the Coast and Transverse Ranges, including the Ojai Ranger District in Ventura County. The forest has dozens of hiking trails, backpacking routes, and campgrounds, including access to the Matilija Wilderness and part of the Sespe Wilderness.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What MHA is Naval Base Ventura County in for BAH?

NBVC falls within the Oxnard-Ventura, California Military Housing Area for BAH purposes. Your rate depends on your rank and whether you have dependents. Use the official [DoD BAH Rate Lookup](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/) with your rank and local ZIP code to find your current figure.

### Where do military families live near NBVC?

The most common choices are Port Hueneme for the shortest commute, Oxnard for the widest range of housing options, Camarillo for a quieter suburban setting and strong schools, Ventura for a beach-city feel, and Newbury Park or Thousand Oaks for top-rated schools at a longer commute. On-base housing may also be available.

### Which school district will my children attend near NBVC?

It depends on your address. Hueneme Elementary School District serves K–8 in Port Hueneme and parts of Oxnard, with high school students in Oxnard Union High School District. Families in Camarillo are typically served by Pleasant Valley School District for K–8 and Oxnard Union High for high school. Confirm the specific school for any address before you commit.

### Where do I get healthcare at NBVC?

NBVC has two clinics: Naval Branch Health Clinic Port Hueneme and Naval Branch Health Clinic Point Mugu, both operating under Naval Hospital Camp Pendleton. Update your DEERS information early, select your TRICARE plan, and use the MHS GENESIS Patient Portal to schedule appointments.

### What is the weather like at NBVC?

The Oxnard coast has a mild Mediterranean climate with warm, dry summers and cool, brief winters. Average highs range from the mid-60s Fahrenheit in winter to the upper 70s in summer. Morning fog, called the marine layer, is common from May through July. It is one of the most comfortable climates in the United States year-round.
