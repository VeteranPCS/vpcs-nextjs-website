If you serve in the military or have served, Maine offers a few targeted property tax breaks for veterans and fully protects military retirement pay from its income tax. Maine is home to Portsmouth Naval Shipyard in Kittery, the Navy's oldest continuously operating shipyard, and the Maine Air National Guard's 101st Air Refueling Wing in Bangor, so it draws military families after a PCS, which is short for Permanent Change of Station, the official military move from one duty station to another. This guide explains the main rules in plain language and points you to the state's own sources. Tax law changes, so treat these figures as a starting point and confirm the current rules before you file.

## How Maine Helps Disabled Veterans With Property Taxes

Maine gives qualifying veterans a partial property tax exemption that lowers the taxable, or "just," value of their home. The U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, known as the VA, is the federal agency that rates service-connected disabilities, and a top rating is one way to qualify.

As [Maine Revenue Services explains](https://www.maine.gov/revenue/taxes/tax-relief-credits-programs/property-tax-relief-programs/property-tax-exemptions), a veteran who served during a recognized war period and is 62 or older, or who is receiving a 100 percent disability rating as a veteran, or who became 100 percent disabled while serving, is eligible for an exemption of $6,000 from the just value of the home. It is a fixed reduction in taxable value, not a full waiver, so the rest of the home's value is still taxed.

### Who Qualifies

You generally qualify if you are a Maine resident, the home is your permanent residence, and you meet one of the service tests: wartime service and age 62 or older, a 100 percent VA disability rating, or having become 100 percent disabled during service. Each partial exemption is adjusted by your town's certified assessment ratio, so the exact dollar amount on your bill can vary from one town to the next.

### A Larger Exemption for Specially Adapted Housing

Maine offers a bigger break for the most severely disabled. A veteran who received a federal grant for a specially adapted housing unit is eligible for an exemption of $50,000 from the just value of the home, as Maine Revenue Services describes on the same page. This is aimed at veterans whose disabilities call for a specially built or modified home.

### Surviving Spouses

The benefit can reach a surviving spouse. Maine extends its veteran property tax exemptions to certain unremarried surviving spouses, minor children, and dependent parents of a qualifying veteran. Because the conditions depend on your situation, confirm them with your town assessor before you rely on the benefit.

## How It Works With Your Local Tax Bill

Maine property tax is assessed and collected by your town or city, and you claim the veteran exemption through your local assessor. You file an application with proof of your service and disability, and it must reach the assessor's office no later than April 1 to apply for that tax year. After your first approval, you generally do not need to reapply each year unless your situation changes.

Maine also runs a separate, income-tax-based benefit. The state's [Property Tax Fairness Credit](https://www.maine.gov/revenue/taxes/tax-relief-credits-programs/income-tax-credits/property-tax-fairness-credit) can return part of the property tax or rent you paid, and the Legislature expanded it for veterans, so it is worth checking even when your exemption is small.

## Military Pay and Maine State Income Tax

Maine has a graduated state income tax, but it fully protects military retirement pay. As the [Maine Revenue Services individual income tax guidance](https://www.maine.gov/revenue/faq/individual-income-tax) confirms, benefits received under a United States military retirement plan, including survivor benefits, are fully exempt from Maine income tax. That makes Maine a friendly state for military retirees, since the pension you earned is not taxed by the state.

Active-duty pay is treated under the usual residency rules: a service member who keeps another home state and is stationed in Maine on orders is generally not taxed by Maine on military pay, while a Maine resident reports military pay like other income. VA disability compensation is not part of your federal income, so Maine does not tax it. Because rates and rules can change, confirm the current details with Maine Revenue Services before you file.

## A Note for Military Spouses: MSRRA

If you are a military spouse, the Military Spouse Residency Relief Act, known as MSRRA, may matter to you. MSRRA is a federal law that lets a military spouse keep a home state for tax and voting purposes even after moving on military orders. You do not automatically become a Maine resident just because your service member got orders here.

Under the related federal rules, a service member, the spouse, or both may choose the service member's home state, the spouse's home state, or the service member's permanent duty station for residency. Because that choice affects both states' taxes, confirm yours before you file. For the property tax exemption in this guide, what usually matters most is that the qualifying veteran owns and lives in the home as the primary residence.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do disabled veterans pay property taxes in Maine?

Often a reduced amount. A veteran who is rated 100 percent disabled by the VA, became 100 percent disabled while serving, or served during a recognized war period and is 62 or older, can exempt $6,000 from the just value of the home. It is a fixed reduction in taxable value, not a full waiver, so the rest of the home's value is still taxed. Confirm your eligibility with your town assessor.

### How much is the Maine veteran property tax exemption worth?

The standard veteran exemption removes $6,000 from the just value of the home. A veteran who received a federal grant for a specially adapted housing unit may instead receive a $50,000 exemption. Each amount is adjusted by your town's certified assessment ratio, so the dollar savings depend on your local tax rate.

### Does Maine tax military retirement pay?

No. Benefits received under a United States military retirement plan, including survivor benefits, are fully exempt from Maine income tax. VA disability compensation is not taxed either.

### When is the Maine veteran exemption application due?

You apply through your town or city assessor, and the application must reach the assessor's office no later than April 1 to apply for that tax year. After your first approval, you generally do not need to reapply each year unless your situation changes.

### Can a surviving spouse claim the Maine veteran exemption?

In many cases, yes. Maine extends its veteran property tax exemptions to certain unremarried surviving spouses, minor children, and dependent parents of a qualifying veteran. Because the rules depend on your situation, confirm the conditions with your town assessor.

Property tax rules and dollar amounts change from year to year, and your situation may have details a general guide cannot cover.
