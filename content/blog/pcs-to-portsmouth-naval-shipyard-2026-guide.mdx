If you have orders for a PCS to Portsmouth Naval Shipyard, you are headed to one of the most storied installations in the U.S. Navy. PCS stands for Permanent Change of Station, the military term for moving from one duty station to another. This 2026 guide covers what PNSY does and where it sits, where families live on both sides of the state line, how housing pay works, schools, healthcare, climate, and things to do once you land.

## What and Where Is Portsmouth Naval Shipyard?

Portsmouth Naval Shipyard, known as PNSY, is the U.S. Navy's oldest continuously operating naval shipyard, established in 1800. According to the [Portsmouth Naval Shipyard page on NAVSEA](https://www.navsea.navy.mil/Home/Shipyards/Portsmouth/), PNSY's mission is the safe overhaul, repair, and modernization of the Navy's nuclear-powered attack submarine fleet, including Los Angeles-class and Virginia-class submarines. The shipyard employs approximately 5,000 civilians and around 200 active-duty military personnel.

One geographic fact every incoming family should know: PNSY is physically on Seavey Island in Kittery, Maine, at the mouth of the Piscataqua River. It is named for Portsmouth, New Hampshire, directly across the river — and that city is where most off-base services and cultural life are centered. Families spread across both sides of the state line, some choosing Maine for proximity, others choosing New Hampshire for its schools and tax structure.

## Where to Live When PCSing to Portsmouth Naval Shipyard

Most families who move to PNSY either commute from the Maine side of the Piscataqua or from New Hampshire communities. Your choice usually comes down to commute length, the state's tax and school systems, housing cost, and the feel you want. Maine has no sales tax on most goods; New Hampshire has no income tax and no sales tax. Both are worth factoring into your budget before you sign anything.

### Kittery, ME

Kittery is the closest off-base community, just five minutes from the shipyard's main gate. As the town that physically surrounds the installation, it is the natural first choice for short commutes. Kittery has a compact downtown, a well-known outlet shopping district on Route 1, and easy access to southern Maine beaches. If minimizing your drive is the top priority, Kittery is hard to beat.

### Portsmouth, NH

Portsmouth is about 10 minutes away across the Memorial Bridge and is the cultural and commercial hub of the Seacoast. Historic downtown Portsmouth has a dense collection of restaurants, shops, and events year-round. Families who want walkable city life and a short commute often choose Portsmouth. Housing costs are higher here than almost anywhere else in the region.

### Newington, NH

Newington is a small, residential town about 10 minutes from the shipyard. It offers quieter neighborhoods with quick access to Route 16 and is a practical choice for families who want New Hampshire proximity without the downtown Portsmouth price.

### Eliot, ME

Eliot is a quiet Maine town roughly 15 minutes from the gate. It has a suburban-rural feel with more land and lower prices than Kittery, popular with families who want space without a long commute.

### York, ME

York is about 15 minutes south along the coast, covering York Village, York Beach, and Cape Neddick. It is one of the most scenic communities in southern Maine, with sandy beaches and rocky headlands. Housing costs reflect the scenery.

### Rye, NH

Rye is a coastal New Hampshire town about 15 minutes away with beach access on New Hampshire's short coastline. It is quieter and more residential than Portsmouth.

### Dover, NH

Dover is about 20 minutes away and offers a wider range of housing prices than Portsmouth. It has a growing downtown and good Route 16 access. Families who want more affordable New Hampshire options look here first.

### Berwick, ME

Berwick and South Berwick sit about 25 minutes from the shipyard in York County, Maine, with a small-town feel and lower price points for families who want more home for the money.

## BAH and the Cost of Living

If you live off base, the military helps cover your housing through BAH, or Basic Allowance for Housing. BAH is a monthly, tax-free payment that depends on your rank, dependent status, and your duty station's Military Housing Area, or MHA. Portsmouth Naval Shipyard falls within the Portsmouth, New Hampshire MHA. Because rates change each year, we do not publish a rate table here. Use the official DoD BAH calculator at [travel.dod.mil BAH Rate Lookup](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/) and confirm your rate before signing a lease or making an offer.

Housing costs on the Seacoast are among the higher in New England. The typical home value in Portsmouth, NH was about $804,373 in May 2026, per [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/53977/portsmouth-nh/) — a typical value, not a median sale price. In Kittery, ME, the typical value was about $690,835 in May 2026, per [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/396778/kittery-me/) — also a typical value, not a median sale price. Communities like Dover, Eliot, and Berwick offer lower price points. As of the week of June 18, 2026, [Freddie Mac](https://www.freddiemac.com/pmms) reported the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate at 6.47 percent. Whether buying or renting makes sense depends on your expected time at the command, so run the numbers for your own situation.

## Schools Near Portsmouth Naval Shipyard

The schools your children attend depend on where you live and which side of the state line you are on.

Families who live in Kittery are served by the [Kittery School Department](https://www.kitteryschools.com/), a small Maine district with roughly 966 students in grades pre-K through 12 and a student-teacher ratio of about 9 to 1.

Families in Portsmouth, NH are served by the Portsmouth School District, which operates elementary, middle, and high schools within the city. Newington students typically attend Portsmouth High School for secondary grades under a tuition arrangement.

Families in Eliot and Berwick, ME fall under York County district structures; York, ME families are served by York School Department for grades K-12. On the New Hampshire side, Dover families attend [Dover City Schools](https://www.dover.k12.nh.us/), one of the larger Seacoast districts.

Attendance zones and tuition arrangements can shift, so confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit. The [National Center for Education Statistics school district search](https://nces.ed.gov/ccd/districtsearch/) is a reliable starting point.

## Healthcare: Naval Branch Health Clinic Portsmouth

Medical care for PNSY personnel and their families is provided by the [Naval Branch Health Clinic Portsmouth](https://newengland.tricare.mil/Clinics/Naval-Branch-Health-Clinic-Portsmouth), a branch clinic of Naval Health Clinic New England. The clinic is located at 402 Goodrich Avenue in Kittery, Maine, on the shipyard itself. It provides primary care, occupational health, pharmacy, laboratory, radiology, physical therapy, dental, and optometry services for eligible beneficiaries, with general clinic hours Monday through Friday, 7 a.m. to 4 p.m.

The clinic does not have an emergency room — call 911 or go to your nearest civilian ER in an emergency. TRICARE networks with civilian providers across the Seacoast for specialty and urgent care needs.

Your military health coverage runs through TRICARE, the health care program for service members and their families. Set up your TRICARE plan early, update your information in DEERS, and arrange any referrals before you arrive so there is no gap in care.

## Climate and Things to Do

The Seacoast has a coastal New England climate: four distinct seasons, mild ocean influence, and real winters. According to the [National Weather Service office in Gray, Maine](https://www.weather.gov/gyx/), which covers both New Hampshire and western Maine, expect warm summers, colorful falls, cold winters with moderate snow, and crisp springs. Proximity to the ocean moderates temperatures, but plan for nor'easters in winter.

The region rewards exploration. [Strawbery Banke Museum](https://www.strawberybanke.org/) in downtown Portsmouth is a ten-acre outdoor history museum tracing the neighborhood from the late 1600s through the 20th century. [Odiorne Point State Park](https://www.nhstateparks.org/find-parks-trails/odiorne-point-state-park) in Rye, NH is a 330-acre coastal park with walking paths, rocky shore, historic WWII fortifications, and wide Gulf of Maine views. Great Bay National Estuarine Research Reserve offers hiking and wildlife watching on the tidal estuary. The beaches of York and Ogunquit are a short drive south, and Boston is about an hour away.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What MHA is Portsmouth Naval Shipyard in for BAH?

PNSY falls within the Portsmouth, New Hampshire Military Housing Area for BAH purposes. Your rate depends on your rank and whether you have dependents. Look up your exact figure with the official [DoD BAH calculator](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/) using your rank and a local ZIP code.

### Is Portsmouth Naval Shipyard in Maine or New Hampshire?

The shipyard is physically in Kittery, Maine, on Seavey Island. It is named for Portsmouth, NH, which sits directly across the Piscataqua River. Families live on both sides of the state line. For BAH purposes, the command falls under the Portsmouth, NH MHA.

### Where do military families live near Portsmouth Naval Shipyard?

Common choices on the Maine side include Kittery (closest), Eliot, York, and Berwick. On the New Hampshire side, Portsmouth, Newington, Rye, and Dover are popular. The decision often comes down to school preference, state tax structure, and how much commute time you can absorb.

### Where do I get healthcare near Portsmouth Naval Shipyard?

On-base primary care is available at [Naval Branch Health Clinic Portsmouth](https://newengland.tricare.mil/Clinics/Naval-Branch-Health-Clinic-Portsmouth), located at 402 Goodrich Avenue in Kittery, ME. The clinic does not have an emergency room. TRICARE networks with civilian providers across both New Hampshire and Maine for specialty and urgent care needs. Set up your plan and update DEERS early.

### What is the weather like on the Seacoast?

Coastal New England with four seasons: warm summers, colorful falls, cold winters with moderate snow, and crisp springs. The [National Weather Service in Gray, Maine](https://www.weather.gov/gyx/) publishes local climate normals for month-by-month averages.
