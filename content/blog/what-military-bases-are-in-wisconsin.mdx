Wisconsin has a deep military history dating back to its early territorial days. Settlers formed militias to protect their communities, and in **1851**, these militias formally became the **Wisconsin National Guard**. The state played a significant role in the **Civil War**, contributing over **90,000 troops** to the Union Army. Today, Wisconsin is home to **one major military installation** that continues to serve as a vital training center for U.S. forces.

### **Fort McCoy**

**Fort McCoy** was established in **1942**, built on land that had been used as a **Civilian Conservation Corps (CCC) camp** during the Great Depression. As World War II intensified, the U.S. Army identified the need for additional training facilities and converted the area into a **military training center**.

During the war, Fort McCoy served multiple purposes:

- **Training Grounds** – Provided advanced training for troops before deployment.
- **Prisoner of War Camp** – Held thousands of **German and Italian soldiers** captured in Europe.
- **Japanese POW Facility** – Became the **largest permanent holding site** for Japanese prisoners of war in the continental United States.

Following World War II, Fort McCoy continued to evolve. Throughout the **Cold War**, it remained a key **training and mobilization center** for U.S. forces, ensuring troops were prepared for deployment.

Today, the base serves as one of the Army’s **primary training locations for large-scale military exercises**. It hosts **Active Duty, National Guard, and Reserve units**, providing a **realistic training environment** for forces preparing for global missions.
