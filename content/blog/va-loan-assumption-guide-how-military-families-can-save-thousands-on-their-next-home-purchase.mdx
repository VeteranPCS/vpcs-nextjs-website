PCS season is expensive enough without overpaying for a mortgage. If you're house hunting in today's high-interest environment, there's a powerful but underutilized strategy that could save your military family thousands: VA loan assumptions.

Whether you're active duty, a veteran, or even a civilian spouse, assuming an existing VA loan could be your ticket to significant savings. Here's everything military families need to know about this valuable homebuying option.

## What Is a VA Loan Assumption?

A VA loan assumption allows you to take over an existing VA mortgage from the seller, inheriting their interest rate, loan balance, and repayment terms. Instead of securing a new mortgage at today's higher rates, you "assume" the seller's existing loan—potentially saving hundreds per month.

The [Department of Veterans Affairs](https://www.va.gov/housing-assistance/home-loans/loan-uses/purchase-loans/): VA loans are assumable by qualified buyers, making this option available to both military and civilian purchasers.

## Key Benefits for Military Homebuyers

### Lock in Lower Interest Rates

Many VA loans originated between 2020-2022 carry interest rates between 2.5-3.5%. The [Freddie Mac Primary Mortgage Market Survey](https://freddiemac.com/pmms): Current 30-year fixed rates average 6.5-7% as of early 2025. Assuming a lower-rate loan could save you $300-500 monthly on a $300,000 mortgage.

### No Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI)

VA loans never require PMI, regardless of down payment amount. The [Consumer Financial Protection Bureau](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/ask-cfpb/what-is-private-mortgage-insurance/): Conventional loans typically require PMI when putting down less than 20%, adding $150-300 monthly to payments.

### Reduced Closing Costs

VA loan assumptions typically involve fewer fees than new mortgages. You'll avoid origination fees, appraisal costs, and other new-loan expenses while enjoying a streamlined closing process.

## Qualification Requirements

To assume a VA loan, you must demonstrate:

**Credit Score:** Most lenders require 620+ credit scores, though requirements vary by lender **Income Stability:** Proof of steady employment and ability to handle monthly payments **Debt-to-Income Ratio:** Typically 41% or lower, following standard VA guidelines **Lender Approval:** The current loan servicer must approve the assumption

The [VA.gov Housing Assistance](https://www.va.gov/housing-assistance/home-loans/loan-uses/purchase-loans/): Assumption process typically takes 45-60 days, often faster than new loan origination.

## Understanding VA Entitlement Impact

Here's something crucial for military buyers: if you're assuming a VA loan as a non-veteran, the seller's VA entitlement remains tied to that loan. However, if you're a veteran or active duty service member, you can substitute your own entitlement, freeing up the seller's benefits for future use.

The [Military OneSource](https://www.militaryonesource.mil/financial-legal/personal-finance/): This entitlement substitution can be a powerful negotiating tool when dealing with military sellers who need their VA benefits restored.

## The Assumption Process: Step by Step

**Step 1:** Identify homes with assumable VA loans (your VeteranPCS agent can help locate these opportunities)

**Step 2:** Verify loan assumability with the current lender—not all VA loans may be assumable due to specific loan terms

**Step 3:** Submit assumption application package including credit report, income verification, and financial statements

**Step 4:** Complete lender underwriting process (typically 4-6 weeks)

**Step 5:** Close on the assumption and take over monthly payments

## Costs and Fees

VA loan assumptions involve minimal fees compared to new mortgages:

**VA Funding Fee:** 0.5% of the loan balance (waived for disabled veterans) **Processing Fees:** Typically $300-500 to the loan servicer **Credit Report and Verification Costs:** Usually under $200

The [VA.gov Funding Fee Information](https://www.va.gov/housing-assistance/home-loans/funding-fee-and-closing-costs/): These costs are significantly lower than typical closing costs on new mortgages, which average 2-3% of the loan amount.

## Strategic Considerations for Military Families

### PCS Timeline Planning

VA loan assumptions work best when you have adequate time for processing. If you're facing a quick PCS move, discuss timeline expectations with your VeteranPCS agent early in your house-hunting process.

### Negotiating Power

Sellers with low-rate VA loans often receive multiple offers. As a military buyer who can substitute VA entitlement, you may have competitive advantages over civilian buyers in negotiations.

### Geographic Hotspots

Areas with high military populations—like Norfolk, San Diego, Colorado Springs, and San Antonio—often have more assumable VA loans available due to frequent PCS moves.

## When VA Loan Assumptions Make Sense

Consider this strategy when:

- Current mortgage rates significantly exceed the assumable loan rate (2+ percentage points)
- You're buying in a competitive military market where sellers value VA entitlement restoration
- You have adequate time for the assumption process before your PCS report date
- The home's purchase price aligns with your military housing allowance (BAH) calculations
