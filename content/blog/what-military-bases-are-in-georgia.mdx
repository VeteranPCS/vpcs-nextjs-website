Georgia has played a pivotal role in U.S. military history, from the American Revolution to modern-day defense operations. With **thirteen active military installations**, the state remains a key hub for national security, training, and logistics. Whether you are **PCSing to Georgia, considering a move, or just exploring military history**, this guide provides an overview of the major military bases in the state and their significance.

## **Overview of Georgia’s Military Bases**

Georgia is home to **Army, Navy, Air Force, and Marine Corps** installations, each serving a distinct purpose. From **Fort Stewart’s mechanized warfare training** to **Kings Bay’s ballistic missile submarines**, these bases contribute to military readiness and national security.

### **Active Military Bases in Georgia**

- **Dobbins Air Reserve Base**
- **Fort Gordon** (formerly Fort Eisenhower)
- **Fort Stewart**
- **Fort Benning** (formerly Fort Moore)
- **Hunter Army Airfield**
- **Marine Corps Logistics Base (MCLB) Albany**
- **Moody Air Force Base**
- **Naval Submarine Base Kings Bay**
- **Robins Air Force Base**

Let’s take a closer look at each installation and its role.

## **Army Bases in Georgia**

### **Fort Gordon (formerly Fort Eisenhower)**

Located near **Augusta, GA**, Fort Gordon has evolved from a World War II training camp to the U.S. Army’s **premier cyber warfare and intelligence training hub**. It houses the **U.S. Army Cyber Command, the Cyber Center of Excellence, and NSA operations**.

**Key Functions:**

- Cyber and electronic warfare training
- Signal Corps education
- Intelligence operations

### **Fort Stewart**

Fort Stewart, located in **southeastern Georgia**, is home to the **3rd Infantry Division** and serves as one of the Army’s largest **maneuver training bases**. Established in the 1940s, it has remained a critical post for **tank and artillery training**.

**Key Functions:**

- Mechanized warfare training
- Rapid deployment operations
- Home of the 3rd Infantry Division

### **Fort Benning (formerly Fort Moore)**

Fort Benning, in **Columbus, GA**, was renamed in 2023 in honor of **Lieutenant General Hal Moore**. The name was reverted back to Fort Benning in 2025. The base is a premier **infantry and airborne training center**, playing a key role in producing highly skilled soldiers.

**Key Functions:**

- U.S. Army Infantry training
- Airborne and Ranger training
- Officer Candidate School (OCS)

### **Hunter Army Airfield**

Located in **Savannah, GA**, Hunter Army Airfield operates alongside Fort Stewart as a major **aviation training and deployment center**. Originally established as an airstrip, it became vital during **World War II and the Vietnam War**.

**Key Functions:**

- Helicopter pilot training
- Rapid deployment airfield
- Home of the 3rd Combat Aviation Brigade

## **Air Force Bases in Georgia**

### **Dobbins Air Reserve Base**

Dobbins ARB, in **Marietta, GA**, started as a World War II **B-29 bomber training base** and later became a critical **Air Force Reserve installation**.

**Key Functions:**

- Airlift operations
- Air Force Reserve training
- Joint-force training

### **Moody Air Force Base**

Located in **Valdosta, GA**, Moody AFB specializes in **combat search and rescue training**. Since **World War II**, it has remained a premier training base for **fighter pilots and pararescue teams**.

**Key Functions:**

- A-10 and HH-60 helicopter training
- Combat Search and Rescue (CSAR) operations
- Special Operations Forces support

### **Robins Air Force Base**

Robins AFB, near **Warner Robins, GA**, is **one of the largest Air Force maintenance and logistics hubs** in the country. Since **World War II**, it has supported **aircraft repair, logistics, and sustainment missions**.

**Key Functions:**

- Aircraft maintenance and sustainment
- Home of the Air Force Sustainment Center
- Fighter, airlift, and helicopter maintenance

## **Navy and Marine Corps Bases in Georgia**

### **Naval Submarine Base Kings Bay**

Kings Bay, located in **southeastern Georgia**, is home to the **Atlantic Fleet’s Trident ballistic missile submarines (SSBNs)**. It was originally an Army base but was transferred to the Navy in the **1970s**.

**Key Functions:**

- Homeport for nuclear-powered submarines
- Strategic deterrence operations
- Submarine maintenance and support

### **Marine Corps Logistics Base (MCLB) Albany**

MCLB Albany has been **the Marine Corps’ primary logistics hub** since the 1950s. It plays a **critical role in equipping and supplying Marines** around the world.

**Key Functions:**

- Logistics and equipment maintenance
- Depot-level repairs for Marine Corps vehicles
- Supply chain management

## **Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)**

### **1. What is the largest military base in Georgia?**

**Fort Stewart** is the largest Army installation in Georgia, covering over **280,000 acres**. It is home to the **3rd Infantry Division** and a primary site for **maneuver training**.

### **2. What Air Force bases are in Georgia?**

The major Air Force bases in Georgia are:

- **Dobbins Air Reserve Base** (Marietta)
- **Moody Air Force Base** (Valdosta)
- **Robins Air Force Base** (Warner Robins)

### **3. What is the primary Navy base in Georgia?**

**Naval Submarine Base Kings Bay** is the **only Navy installation** in Georgia. It is home to **Ohio-class ballistic missile submarines** that support the U.S. strategic deterrence mission.

### **4. Are there Marine Corps bases in Georgia?**

Yes, **Marine Corps Logistics Base Albany** is the main **Marine installation** in the state. It is responsible for **Marine Corps supply and logistics operations**.
