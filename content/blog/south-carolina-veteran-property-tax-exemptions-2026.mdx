If you serve in the military or have served, a South Carolina veteran property tax exemption can erase the property tax on your home, and the state recently made it even easier to claim. South Carolina is a popular place to land after a PCS, which is short for Permanent Change of Station, the official military move from one duty station to another, with Fort Jackson, Joint Base Charleston, and Shaw Air Force Base drawing thousands of military families. This guide covers the main rules in plain language and points you to the state's own sources. Tax law changes, so treat these figures as a starting point and confirm the current rules before you file.

## The South Carolina Disabled Veteran Property Tax Exemption

South Carolina offers one of the cleaner property tax breaks in the country for severely disabled veterans. According to the [South Carolina Department of Revenue's exempt property page](https://dor.sc.gov/property/exempt-property), a veteran who has a total, permanent, and service-connected disability can claim a property tax exemption on their home and up to five acres of land, plus up to two private passenger vehicles. "Service-connected" means the disability is tied to your service. The VA, the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, is the federal agency that makes that rating.

This is a full exemption on the qualifying home, not a partial discount. The home must be your legal residence. A couple of other groups qualify on similar terms: Medal of Honor recipients and former prisoners of war can claim a property tax exemption on a home and up to one acre, plus up to two vehicles.

### A 2024 Expansion Made It Easier to Claim

South Carolina widened this benefit in 2024. As the [South Carolina Department of Revenue explains](https://dor.sc.gov/tax-tips/veterans-learn-more-about-sc-property-tax-exemptions), disabled veterans can now claim the exemption beginning with the year the disability occurs, and surviving spouses can claim it immediately, in the same manner as the veteran. The change also lets qualifying veterans claim the exemption retroactively for up to the previous two years, as long as they paid their property tax on time, so a veteran applying in a given year may be able to reach back two tax years.

### Surviving Spouses

The exemption can follow a surviving spouse. An eligible surviving spouse of a qualifying disabled veteran may claim the exemption on the home, and under the 2024 update can do so right away. The rules have conditions, so confirm yours with the Department of Revenue or the [South Carolina Department of Veterans' Affairs](https://scdva.sc.gov/veteran-consumer-resources).

## How It Works With Local Property Taxes

In South Carolina, property tax is assessed and collected locally, by counties, but this veteran exemption is granted by the state Department of Revenue rather than your county auditor. That is a helpful difference: you apply once through the state, and the exemption then flows to your county bill.

South Carolina also runs a separate Homestead Exemption for residents who are 65 or older, totally and permanently disabled, or legally blind. It is a different program from the disabled veteran exemption, it is handled by your county auditor, and a veteran who does not qualify as totally and permanently disabled may still benefit from it. Ask your county auditor which programs fit your situation, since you generally take the one that helps most rather than stacking them.

Local tax rates and a property's assessment still vary from place to place, so the dollars saved depend on where you live. ## Military Pay and South Carolina State Income Tax

South Carolina has a state income tax, but it treats military retirement income very kindly. A 2022 law (House Bill 3247) made military retirement pay 100 percent exempt from South Carolina income tax, effective for tax years after 2021, with no earned-income cap. In plain terms, the state does not tax your military pension. You can read more through the [South Carolina Department of Veterans' Affairs](https://scdva.sc.gov/veteran-consumer-resources).

Active-duty pay is treated differently. If South Carolina is your state of legal residence, the state generally taxes your active-duty pay, though certain deductions can apply. VA disability compensation is not part of your federal income, so South Carolina does not tax it. Because the details depend on your residency and duty status, confirm your situation before you file.

## A Note for Military Spouses: MSRRA

If you are a military spouse, the Military Spouse Residency Relief Act, known as MSRRA, may matter for where you pay income tax. MSRRA is a federal law that lets a service member's spouse keep a home state for tax purposes even after a PCS move pulls the family to a new state. Under the related federal rules, a service member, their spouse, or both can choose the service member's home state, the spouse's home state, or the service member's permanent duty station for residency.

So you do not automatically become a South Carolina resident just because your service member got orders here. If a spouse keeps a legal residence in another state, wages earned in South Carolina may be protected from state income tax. Because that choice affects both states' taxes, confirm yours before you file.

## How to Apply and Where to Verify

You apply for the disabled veteran property tax exemption through the South Carolina Department of Revenue. The easiest way is online through MyDORWAY, the state's free tax portal; you can also file the paper [Property Tax Exemption Application for Individuals, Form PT-401-I](https://dor.sc.gov/forms-site/Forms/PT401I.pdf). You will provide VA documentation of your total, permanent, service-connected rating. Surviving spouses apply the same way.

Because tax rules and forms can change, check the current requirements with the Department of Revenue before you apply. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do disabled veterans pay property taxes in South Carolina?

A veteran with a total, permanent, and service-connected disability can claim a full property tax exemption on their home and up to five acres, plus up to two private passenger vehicles, which can bring the property tax on that home to zero. Confirm your eligibility with the [South Carolina Department of Revenue](https://dor.sc.gov/property/exempt-property).

### Does South Carolina tax military retirement pay?

No. A 2022 law made military retirement pay 100 percent exempt from South Carolina income tax for tax years after 2021, with no earned-income cap. Active-duty pay may still be taxed if South Carolina is your state of legal residence.

### Can a surviving spouse keep the veteran's exemption?

In many cases, yes. An eligible surviving spouse of a qualifying disabled veteran may claim the exemption on the home, and under the 2024 update can do so right away. Review the conditions with the Department of Revenue.

### How do I apply for the South Carolina veteran exemption?

Apply through the Department of Revenue, either online through MyDORWAY or by filing Form PT-401-I. You will need VA documentation of your total, permanent, service-connected disability rating.

### Can I claim the exemption for past years?

Possibly. The 2024 expansion lets qualifying veterans claim the exemption retroactively for up to the previous two years, as long as property tax was paid on time. Ask the Department of Revenue how it applies to you.

Property tax rules and dollar amounts change from year to year, and your situation may have details a general guide cannot cover.
