As military families, we’re accustomed to the transient nature of our lifestyle. Permanent Change of Station (PCS) orders are part and parcel of our reality, presenting both challenges and opportunities. One such opportunity led me to Birmingham, Alabama—a vibrant city with a rich history, nestled in the heart of the Deep South. In this blog, I’ll share my experiences and insights about PCSing to Birmingham with the military.

**Education and Employment Opportunities**

Birmingham offers diverse education and employment opportunities for military spouses and dependents. The city is home to several colleges and universities, providing options for furthering education or pursuing career development. Additionally, many local businesses and organizations actively seek to hire military spouses, recognizing their adaptability and valuable skill sets. Programs like the Military Spouse Employment Partnership (MSEP) further facilitate job placements and career advancement for military spouses in the area.

**Cost of Living Considerations**

One of the factors that often influence PCS decisions is the cost of living in the new location. Fortunately, Birmingham offers a relatively affordable cost of living compared to many other metropolitan areas. Housing options range from family-friendly neighborhoods to urban lofts, catering to diverse preferences and budgets. Alabama also offers a notable tax benefit for military retirees: military retirement pay is fully exempt from state income tax (more on that below).

**Navigating Military-Friendly Resources**

Military families transitioning to Birmingham will find a wealth of resources tailored to their needs. From support services on base to local organizations dedicated to assisting military spouses and children, there’s no shortage of help available. Additionally, Birmingham’s proximity to military installations like Maxwell Air Force Base and Fort Rucker ensures easy access to essential amenities and healthcare services.

## Birmingham's Military and Veteran Connections

Birmingham is not a base town. There is no major active-duty installation inside the city. What Birmingham does have is a strong military and veteran community, and easy reach to several Alabama installations.

The biggest local anchor is the [Birmingham VA Medical Center](https://www.va.gov/birmingham-health-care/locations/birmingham-va-medical-center/), at 700 South 19th Street in the city's Southside district. It is a 313-bed facility that provides acute medical and surgical care to veterans from Alabama and nearby states. The wider [Birmingham VA Health Care System](https://www.va.gov/birmingham-health-care/locations/) also runs community-based outpatient clinics across the region, including in Bessemer, Huntsville, Gadsden, Jasper, and the Anniston-Oxford area, so routine care does not always mean a drive downtown.

If you want to stay close to an active-duty installation, a few are within reach. Maxwell Air Force Base, in Montgomery, sits about 82 miles south of Birmingham, according to [Military.com's Maxwell-Gunter base guide](https://www.military.com/base-guide/maxwell-air-force-base-and-gunter-annex). Maxwell is the home of Air University. To the north, Redstone Arsenal in Huntsville is the state's largest installation and a major hub for Army aviation and missile work, as noted in [MilitaryBases.com's Alabama list](https://militarybases.com/alabama/). Farther south, Fort Novosel (formerly Fort Rucker) is the Army's main helicopter training post. None of these is a short daily commute, but they keep Birmingham connected to commissary trips, medical referrals, and the wider Alabama military network.

Veteran roots run deep here. Census figures put the number of veterans in Jefferson County, where Birmingham sits, in the thousands, based on the [U.S. Census Bureau QuickFacts for Jefferson County](https://www.census.gov/quickfacts/jeffersoncountyalabama). That means veteran service organizations, support groups, and people who understand military life are not hard to find.

## Cost of Living and Housing in Birmingham

Money goes further in Birmingham than in many other metro areas, and that is a big part of why families settle here.

For homebuyers, the numbers are easy to like. The typical home value in the city of Birmingham was about $104,605 as of April 30, 2026, according to [Zillow's Birmingham home values page](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/10417/birmingham-al/). This is a typical value across the market, not a median sale price, and it can change with the season. Prices climb as you move into the suburbs. Across the broader [Birmingham-Hoover metro area](https://www.zillow.com/birmingham-metro-al_r394388/home-values/), the typical home value was around $206,767 as of April 30, 2026, again a typical value rather than a sale price. So a buyer can find a starter home well under the national average in the city, or step up to a larger suburban home and still pay less than in many coastal markets.

A note on taxes for those considering Alabama residency. Alabama does have a state income tax, but military retirement pay is fully exempt from it, according to the [Alabama Department of Revenue's list of exempt income](https://www.revenue.alabama.gov/individual-corporate/income-exempt-from-alabama-income-taxation/). For a retiring service member, that exemption can stretch a fixed income noticeably.

## Schools and Family Life

Birmingham families have several school options to weigh. Two of the largest public systems are the [Birmingham City School District](https://www.greatschools.org/alabama/birmingham/birmingham-city-school-district/) and the surrounding [Jefferson County School District](https://www.greatschools.org/alabama/birmingham/jefferson-county-school-district/), and ratings vary widely school by school on GreatSchools, so it pays to look at the specific school zoned to a home rather than the district as a whole. Many military families also look at suburban districts in places like Hoover, Mountain Brook, and Vestavia Hills, which tend to score higher.

A practical tip: ask your real estate agent to pull school zone maps before you commit to a neighborhood. PCS (Permanent Change of Station, the military term for a move to a new duty station or home) timing rarely lines up neatly with a school calendar, so knowing which school a house feeds into can save a mid-year transfer.

## Why Veterans Choose Birmingham

Put the pieces together and the appeal is clear. Birmingham offers home prices below the national average, no state tax on military retirement pay, a major VA medical center in town, and a growing job market anchored by health care, banking, and the University of Alabama at Birmingham. Add a low cost of living, a warm climate, and an established veteran community, and it is easy to see why so many service members choose Birmingham as a place to put down roots after the moves are done.

**Soaking in the Great Outdoors**

Nature enthusiasts will find plenty to love about Birmingham’s outdoor offerings. Whether you’re hiking the trails at Red Mountain Park, kayaking on the Cahaba River, or enjoying a leisurely picnic at Railroad Park, the city’s natural beauty beckons you to explore. And for those craving a quick escape, the breathtaking landscapes of Oak Mountain State Park are just a short drive away.

**Adapting to Southern Weather**

One aspect of life in Birmingham that took some getting used to is the weather. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters are relatively mild with occasional snowfall. However, the region’s temperate climate means you’ll have ample opportunities to enjoy outdoor activities year-round. Just be sure to pack accordingly and embrace the occasional weather-related surprises!

**Finding Home Away from Home**

PCSing to Birmingham, Alabama, has been a rewarding experience for me and my family. From the warm embrace of its residents to the abundance of cultural and recreational opportunities. While military life may be transient, the memories we’ve created and the connections we’ve forged in Birmingham will last a lifetime.

Whether you’re a seasoned military family or embarking on your first PCS journey, I encourage you to embrace the adventure and make the most of your time in Birmingham. Who knows? You might just find yourself falling in love with the Magic City’s charm and hospitality, just like we did.
