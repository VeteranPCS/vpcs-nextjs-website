Idaho's military presence is centered around **Mountain Home Air Force Base**, the state's primary installation. Located in **southwestern Idaho**, this base plays a critical role in **air combat training and operations**, supporting the **U.S. Air Force's global mission**. It is not only a key employer in the region but also a driving force in Idaho’s economy and military community.

## **Mountain Home Air Force Base**

**Strategic Location and Mission**

Mountain Home Air Force Base is strategically located in the **high desert terrain** of Idaho, providing an **ideal training environment** for air combat and tactical missions. The base is home to the **366th Fighter Wing**, known as the **Gunfighters**, a unit with a long history of preparing aircrews for **combat and air superiority missions worldwide**.

Originally established in **1943** during World War II, the base trained **bomber crews and fighter pilots** who later served in **Europe and the Pacific Theater**. Over the years, it has evolved into a **premier Air Force training center**, focusing on **fighter tactics, electronic warfare, and joint military operations**.

### **Key Missions at Mountain Home AFB**

**Fighter Training and Combat Readiness**

  - Mountain Home AFB primarily supports **F-15E Strike Eagle squadrons**, training pilots and aircrews for **air-to-air and air-to-ground combat**.
  - The base also hosts **multinational exercises** involving allied air forces, reinforcing **coalition warfare capabilities**.

**Global Deployment Support**

  - The **366th Fighter Wing** regularly deploys in support of **combat operations worldwide**, including missions in **Europe, the Middle East, and the Indo-Pacific**.
  - The base operates as a **rapid-response force**, ensuring U.S. airpower is available when needed.

**Electronic Warfare and Joint Exercises**

  - Mountain Home AFB is known for its advanced **electronic warfare training**, which prepares aircrews to operate in environments with **complex enemy radar and missile threats**.
  - The base hosts **annual joint-force exercises**, integrating **Army, Navy, Marine Corps, and international air units** to enhance battlefield coordination.
