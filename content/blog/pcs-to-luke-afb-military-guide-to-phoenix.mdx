Receiving PCS orders to Luke Air Force Base or planning to retire in the Phoenix area? ## Luke Air Force Base: Your New Duty Station

Located in Glendale, Arizona, Luke AFB is home to the 56th Fighter Wing, one of the Air Force's premier F-35 and F-16 training facilities. According to the [U.S. Air Force](https://www.luke.af.mil/About/Fact-Sheets/): The base hosts approximately 7,500 military and civilian personnel, making it a significant installation with a strong community presence in the West Valley.

With year-round flying weather and extensive training ranges, Luke AFB continues to fulfill its mission of training the world's greatest fighter pilots and combat-ready Airmen.

## Top West Valley Neighborhoods for Military Families

When PCSing to Luke AFB, your Basic Allowance for Housing (BAH) can go further in the West Valley compared to other major military installations. Here are the top cities to consider, each with their own distinctive character:

### Glendale

- **Commute to Luke AFB**: 5-15 minutes
- **Housing**: Mix of established neighborhoods and new developments
- **Notable features**: Home to State Farm Stadium (Arizona Cardinals), Westgate Entertainment District

### Surprise

- **Commute to Luke AFB**: 15-25 minutes
- **Housing**: Newer master-planned communities with amenities
- **Notable features**: Family-friendly atmosphere, growing retail centers

### Litchfield Park

- **Commute to Luke AFB**: 5-10 minutes
- **Housing**: Charming upscale communities with character
- **Notable features**: Historic Wigwam Resort, tree-lined streets

### Goodyear

- **Commute to Luke AFB**: 15-20 minutes
- **Housing**: Mix of established and newer communities
- **Notable features**: MLB Spring Training facilities, access to I-10

## School Options for Military Children

According to the [Arizona Department of Education](https://www.azed.gov/): The West Valley offers several highly-rated school districts with programs that understand the unique needs of military children.

Top school districts near Luke AFB include:

- **Dysart Unified School District** (Surprise, El Mirage)
- **Litchfield Elementary School District** (Litchfield Park, Goodyear)
- **Peoria Unified School District** (Peoria, North Glendale)

Many schools in these districts participate in the [Military Interstate Children's Compact Commission (MIC3)](https://www.mic3.net/), which helps ease transition challenges for military children moving between school systems.

For families interested in alternatives, the area also offers quality charter schools, private schools, and active homeschool communities.

## Weather Considerations for Your PCS to Phoenix

Moving to Phoenix requires preparation for the desert climate. According to the [National Weather Service](https://www.weather.gov/psr/):

- **Summer (June-September)**: Extremely hot with temperatures regularly exceeding 100°F (38°C)
- **Winter (December-February)**: Mild with daytime highs between 65-75°F (18-24°C)
- **Spring and Fall**: Pleasant temperatures, ideal for outdoor activities

For military families PCSing from cooler climates, the adjustment period typically takes about one season. Preparation tips include:

- Stay hydrated year-round
- Plan outdoor activities for early morning during summer months
- Consider energy-efficient housing to manage cooling costs
- Invest in UV protection for vehicles

## Transportation and Getting Around

The West Valley transportation network continues to expand to accommodate growth. Key transportation arteries include:

- **Loop 101 (Agua Fria Freeway)**: North-south corridor east of Luke AFB
- **Loop 303**: North-south corridor west of Luke AFB
- **Interstate 10**: East-west corridor connecting the West Valley to downtown Phoenix

According to the [Maricopa Association of Governments](https://www.azmag.gov/): The average commute time from West Valley communities to Luke AFB ranges from 10-25 minutes, significantly lower than the national average for military installations.
