Maine has a long and distinguished military history, dating back to the 1600s when its early settlers were involved in the French and Indian Wars. Since then, Maine’s citizens have played a role in every major U.S. conflict. Today, the state is home to one major military installation: **Naval Shipyard Portsmouth**.

### **Naval Shipyard Portsmouth**

Often referred to as **Portsmouth Navy Yard**, this installation holds the distinction of being the **oldest continuously operating shipyard in the U.S. Navy**. Established in **1800**, the shipyard was strategically built to support **warship construction and maintenance**, ensuring the Navy had the necessary infrastructure to maintain fleet readiness.

#### **Strategic Importance**

- Located on the **Piscataqua River**, the shipyard benefits from **deepwater access** and **swift currents** that prevent ice blockages, making it an ideal location for year-round operations.
- While it originally served as a **warship construction center**, its modern mission has shifted to **submarine maintenance and modernization**.

#### **Current Mission and Operations**

Today, **Naval Shipyard Portsmouth** specializes in:

- **Overhauling and refueling nuclear submarines** to extend their operational life.
- **Modernizing existing submarines** to integrate advanced technology and improve combat readiness.
- **Providing engineering and logistics support** to ensure the U.S. Navy maintains a **highly capable and mission-ready fleet**.

As one of the **most critical shipyards in the U.S.**, Portsmouth continues to play a key role in **national defense**, supporting both active-duty personnel and the civilian workforce that keeps the base running efficiently.
