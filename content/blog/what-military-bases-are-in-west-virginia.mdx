West Virginia has a long and proud military tradition dating back to the early **1730s**, when some of the first militias were formed in the region. Over the years, the state has played a crucial role in national defense, providing both training grounds and operational support for the U.S. military. Today, West Virginia is home to two major military installations, both serving the **Air National Guard**.

### **McLaughlin Air National Guard Base**

Originally established in the **1940s**, this base played a pivotal role in the formation of the **West Virginia Air National Guard in 1947**. It was later named after **Brigadier General James Kemp McLaughlin**, a decorated **World War II** pilot who was instrumental in shaping the state's Air Guard operations.

Today, McLaughlin Air National Guard Base is home to the **130th Airlift Wing**, which operates a fleet of **C-130H3 Hercules aircraft**. These aircraft provide essential **airlift capabilities** for missions around the world, supporting combat operations, humanitarian efforts, and disaster relief.

For more details, visit the official base website: **McLaughlin Air National Guard Base**

### **Shepherd Field Air National Guard Base**

The **West Virginia Air National Guard** was federally recognized in **1947**, with the **167th Fighter Squadron** as its first operational unit. Initially, the squadron operated out of Shepherd Field, which later became a **permanent facility in 1955** at the **Eastern West Virginia Regional Airport**.

By the **1970s**, the **167th transitioned from a Fighter Squadron to an Airlift Wing**, taking on a broader mission of **strategic airlift operations**. Today, it provides **global airlift support** using **C-130H3 aircraft**, similar to McLaughlin Air National Guard Base. These missions include **military personnel transport, cargo movement, and disaster relief efforts** both domestically and internationally.

For more details, visit the official base website: **Shepherd Field Air National Guard Base**
