If you have orders for a PCS to Keesler Air Force Base, welcome to the Mississippi Gulf Coast. PCS stands for Permanent Change of Station, the military term for an official move from one duty station to another. Keesler is one of the Air Force's busiest technical training installations, and it sits right on the edge of Biloxi, one of the more unique cities in the South. This 2026 guide is written for the airmen, officers, civilians, and families who are heading here to live and work.

A move is a lot to manage. Our goal is to make the Keesler part feel clearer. We will cover where to live, how housing pay works, schools, healthcare, the local climate, and a few things to do once you are settled.

## What and Where Is Keesler AFB?

Keesler Air Force Base is a U.S. Air Force installation in Biloxi, Mississippi, on the Gulf of Mexico. The base is the home of the [81st Training Wing](https://www.keesler.af.mil/Units/81st-Training-Wing/), the host unit for Keesler and the Air Force's electronics and technical training Center of Excellence. According to the [81st Training Wing fact sheet](https://www.keesler.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/360301/81st-training-wing/), the wing trains more than 28,000 students each year across programs in cyber operations, electronic warfare, intelligence, medical fields, and other specialties. It is one of the single largest employers on the Mississippi Gulf Coast.

In addition to the 81st Training Wing, Keesler is home to the [403rd Wing](https://www.403wg.afrc.af.mil/), an Air Force Reserve Command unit. The 403rd Wing's 53rd Weather Reconnaissance Squadron — known worldwide as the Hurricane Hunters — is the only Department of Defense organization that still flies manned aircraft directly into tropical storms and hurricanes. Their ten Lockheed-Martin WC-130J aircraft operate from Keesler and send real-time weather data to the National Hurricane Center during every Atlantic hurricane season.

## Where to Live When PCSing to Keesler AFB

Most families who move to Keesler either live in on-post housing or settle in one of the surrounding Gulf Coast communities. Your choice usually comes down to commute, schools, budget, and the lifestyle you want. The communities below represent the most common options.

![Approximate drive times from Gulf Coast communities to Keesler AFB, including Biloxi, D'Iberville, Ocean Springs, Gulfport, Long Beach, and Gautier.](/images/blog/pcs-to-keesler-afb-biloxi-2026-guide/where-to-live.png)

*Approximate one-way drive times to Keesler AFB. Times vary with traffic and which gate you use.*

### Biloxi

Biloxi surrounds Keesler on three sides. Living here puts you within 10 minutes of the base and gives you easy access to the beach, restaurants, and city amenities. The housing stock is mixed, with older neighborhoods near the water and newer developments inland. If a short commute and walkable city life matter to you, Biloxi is worth a close look.

### D'Iberville

D'Iberville sits just north of Biloxi, about 12 minutes from the main gate. It has grown quickly in recent years, with newer shopping, restaurants, and residential neighborhoods. Families who want a suburban feel without a long drive often land here.

### Ocean Springs

Ocean Springs is about 15 minutes east, across the Back Bay of Biloxi. It is known for its arts scene, historic downtown, and strong schools. It tends to have a slightly higher cost of entry but draws consistent demand from military families.

### Gulfport

Gulfport is the largest city on the Gulf Coast, about 20 minutes west. It offers the widest range of housing options, a major port, a VA medical center, and a full slate of retail and services.

### Long Beach

Long Beach is a quiet residential city about 25 minutes west. It has a small-town feel, a solid local school district, and a calmer pace than Biloxi's casino corridor.

### Gautier

Gautier (pronounced GOH-shay) is about 25 minutes east. It is less dense than Ocean Springs and offers more land and newer construction for the money — a good option for families who want room to grow.

## Keesler AFB BAH and the Cost of Living

If you live off base, the military helps cover your housing through BAH, which stands for Basic Allowance for Housing. BAH is a monthly, tax-free payment. The amount depends on three things: your rank, whether you have dependents, and your duty station's Military Housing Area, or MHA. An MHA is the geographic zone the Department of Defense uses to set local housing pay. Keesler AFB falls within the Biloxi, Mississippi MHA.

Because rates change each year and vary by rank and dependent status, we do not publish a rate table here. To find your exact figure, use the official DoD BAH calculator: [travel.dod.mil BAH Rate Lookup](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/). Enter your rank and ZIP code to see the current rate. Always confirm your rate before signing a lease or making an offer.

For a sense of buying costs, the typical home value in Biloxi was about $238,011 as of May 2026, according to [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/51003/biloxi-ms/). That is a typical value, not a median sale price. As of the week of June 18, 2026, [Freddie Mac](https://www.freddiemac.com/pmms) reported the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate at 6.47 percent. Whether buying or renting makes more sense depends on how long you expect to be stationed here, so run the numbers for your own situation.

## Schools Near Keesler AFB

The schools your children attend depend on where you live. Several districts serve the communities around the base.

[Biloxi Public School District](https://www.biloxischools.net/) serves families living in the city of Biloxi itself. According to [NCES data](https://nces.ed.gov/ccd/districtsearch/district_detail.asp?Search=1&State=28&ID2=2800630), the district includes 10 public schools and serves approximately 5,764 students. It is also one of the higher-performing districts in Mississippi.

Families who settle in D'Iberville or other unincorporated parts of Harrison County fall under [Harrison County School District](https://www.harrison.k12.ms.us/), the larger county-wide system. Those in Ocean Springs are served by [Ocean Springs School District](https://www.oscsd.k12.ms.us/), which draws strong reviews from military families and has a solid high school. Families in Gulfport or Long Beach typically fall under [Gulfport School District](https://www.gulfportschools.com/) or [Long Beach School District](https://www.lbsd.k12.ms.us/), respectively. Gautier residents are served by Jackson County School District.

Attendance boundaries can shift year to year, so confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit.

## Healthcare: Keesler Medical Center

On-base medical care is centered at [Keesler Medical Center](https://keesler.tricare.mil/), operated by the 81st Medical Group. It is one of the largest Air Force medical facilities in the service, with approximately 42 specialties and a staff of nearly 1,360 military and civilian personnel. The center serves more than 27,000 enrolled patients, including nearly 7,500 active-duty members.

Your military health coverage runs through TRICARE, the health care program for service members and their families. Set up your plan early, update your information in DEERS, and request any referrals through [keesler.tricare.mil](https://keesler.tricare.mil/) before you arrive so there is no gap in care.

## Climate and Things to Do

The Mississippi Gulf Coast has a humid subtropical climate, with hot, humid summers and mild winters. The [National Weather Service Mobile/Pensacola office](https://www.weather.gov/mob/) covers this region. Annual rainfall averages roughly 60 inches, with the wettest months in late spring and summer. January lows average in the mid-40s Fahrenheit.

The Gulf Coast sits in a hurricane-prone corridor. The Atlantic hurricane season runs June 1 through November 30, with peak activity in August and September. Learn your base's evacuation procedures during in-processing, build a family emergency plan, and keep a go-bag ready. The 403rd Wing's Hurricane Hunters fly reconnaissance into tropical storms to help forecasters track them — this base takes severe weather seriously.

Beyond storm prep, there is a lot to explore. [Gulf Islands National Seashore](https://www.nps.gov/guis/index.htm) protects a string of barrier islands — including Ship, Horn, and Cat Islands in Mississippi — accessible by boat from Biloxi. The Davis Bayou Area on the mainland has hiking, camping, and fishing. The [William M. Colmer Visitor Center](https://www.nps.gov/places/000/william-m-colmer-visitor-center.htm) is a good first stop. Biloxi's beachfront drive, restaurants, and historic lighthouse round out the picture. Fall and spring offer the most comfortable outdoor conditions.

Keesler AFB falls within the Biloxi, Mississippi Military Housing Area for BAH purposes. Your rate depends on your rank and whether you have dependents. Look up your exact figure with the official [DoD BAH calculator](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/) using your rank and ZIP code.

### Where do military families live near Keesler AFB?

Common choices include Biloxi and D'Iberville for short commutes; Ocean Springs for a strong school district and community feel; Gulfport for the widest selection of housing; and Long Beach or Gautier for a quieter, more spacious setting. On-base housing is also an option, so ask your sponsor and the housing office about availability.

Yes. The Gulf Coast sees tropical storms and hurricanes during the Atlantic season, which runs June through November. Learn your base's emergency plans during in-processing, build a family evacuation plan, and keep supplies on hand. Keesler's tenant unit, the 403rd Wing Hurricane Hunters, flies reconnaissance directly into these storms — the base takes severe weather seriously.
