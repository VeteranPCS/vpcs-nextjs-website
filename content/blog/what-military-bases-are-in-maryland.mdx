Maryland sits at the heart of the nation's defense network, with bases that handle everything from cyber defense to medical care to weapons testing. According to the State of Maryland, **twenty military installations** support active-duty missions across the state, with work spread across the **Army, Navy, Air Force, and Coast Guard** (the term PCS, or Permanent Change of Station, means a military move from one duty station to another). Whether you are **PCSing to Maryland, weighing a move, or just curious about the state's military history**, this guide walks through the **key bases** and what each one does.

## Overview of Military Installations in Maryland

Maryland's bases cluster around the **Washington–Baltimore corridor** and the **Chesapeake Bay**. They support a **wide range of missions**, including **intelligence and cybersecurity, military medicine, weapons research, and pilot test work**. The state government notes that Maryland is "at the forefront of the nation's military preparedness," and several of its installations are among the most well-known in the country, per the [Maryland Department of Planning](https://militarycompatibility.maryland.gov/Pages/Military-Installations-in-Maryland/military-background-in-maryland.aspx).

## Army Bases in Maryland

### Fort George G. Meade

Fort Meade is **Maryland's largest employer** and one of the most important intelligence sites in the country. It is home to the **National Security Agency (NSA)**, which handles signals intelligence and cybersecurity, and **U.S. Cyber Command (USCYBERCOM)**, which defends Department of Defense networks and runs cyber operations. According to the [U.S. Army](https://home.army.mil/meade/), the post supports the NSA, U.S. Cyber Command, the Defense Information Systems Agency, the Defense Media Activity, and more than 100 other agencies.

**Key Missions:**

- Signals intelligence and cybersecurity
- Cyber operations and network defense
- Support for over 100 partner agencies

### Aberdeen Proving Ground

Established in **1917** on the shores of the Chesapeake Bay in Harford County, Aberdeen Proving Ground is the **Army's oldest active proving ground** (a proving ground is a site where weapons and equipment are tested). Today it is one of the world's leading **research, development, and testing centers** for military gear. According to the [DEVCOM Army Research Laboratory](https://arl.devcom.army.mil/who-we-are/locations/apg/), the post is a hub for Army research and supports more than 90 tenant organizations.

**Key Missions:**

- Weapons and equipment testing
- Army research and development
- Test and evaluation for combat systems

### Fort Detrick

Located in **Frederick**, Fort Detrick began as **Camp Detrick** in **1943**. Its early biological warfare mission ended in 1969, and the post has since grown into a campus focused on **advanced biomedical research**. According to the [U.S. Army Medical Research Institute of Infectious Diseases (USAMRIID)](https://usamriid.health.mil/), the institute develops vaccines, drugs, and diagnostic tests to protect service members from biological threats. The post also supports long-haul telecommunications for the White House and the Department of Defense.

**Key Missions:**

- Biomedical research and development
- Vaccine and drug development
- Medical materiel management

## Navy Bases in Maryland

### Naval Air Station Patuxent River

Known as **NAS Pax River**, this base sits in **St. Mary's County** near where the Patuxent River meets the Chesapeake Bay. It is the nation's **test and evaluation center** for new Navy and Marine Corps aircraft and weapons systems. According to the [Naval Air Systems Command](https://www.navair.navy.mil/PaxRiver), the base is home to NAVAIR headquarters and hosts more than 50 tenants, conducting over 200,000 air operations a year.

**Key Missions:**

- Aircraft test and evaluation
- Research and development for naval aviation
- Life-cycle support for Navy aircraft and weapons

### Naval Support Activity Annapolis (U.S. Naval Academy)

The **United States Naval Academy** in **Annapolis** was established in **1845** and is the second-oldest of the five U.S. service academies. According to the [U.S. Naval Academy](https://www.usna.edu/About/mission.php), its mission is to develop midshipmen "morally, mentally and physically" into leaders for the Navy and Marine Corps. Graduates earn a Bachelor of Science degree and a commission as a Navy ensign or Marine Corps second lieutenant.

**Key Missions:**

- Education and training of future officers
- Leadership development
- Commissioning Navy and Marine Corps officers

### Naval Support Activity Bethesda (Walter Reed)

Naval Support Activity Bethesda is home to the **Walter Reed National Military Medical Center**, the **nation's largest military hospital**. The base was formed after a 2005 base realignment combined the old National Naval Medical Center and Walter Reed Army Medical Center. According to [Naval District Washington](https://ndw.cnic.navy.mil/Bethesda/), the base supports the hospital and its tenant commands in patient care, medical research, and education.

**Key Missions:**

- Military medical care
- Medical research and education
- Support for wounded service members

### Naval Support Facility Indian Head

Located on the **Potomac River** about 20 miles south of Washington, D.C., this base is home to the **Naval Surface Warfare Center (NSWC) Indian Head Division**. According to the [State of Maryland](https://militarycompatibility.maryland.gov/Pages/Military-Installations-in-Maryland/nsf-indian-head-home.aspx), Indian Head manufactures propellants, pyrotechnics, and explosives that account for a large share of the deployed U.S. weapon explosives. It is the Defense Department's largest full-spectrum facility for energetics (the science of explosives and propellants).

**Key Missions:**

- Research and testing of energetics
- Manufacturing of propellants and explosives
- Ordnance and propulsion support

## Air Force Bases in Maryland

### Joint Base Andrews

Located in **Prince George's County**, Joint Base Andrews is best known as the **home of Air Force One**, the aircraft that carries the President. The base was formed in **2009** when Andrews Air Force Base merged with Naval Air Facility Washington. According to the [Maryland Department of Planning](https://militarycompatibility.maryland.gov/Pages/Military-Installations-in-Maryland/joint-base-andrews-home.aspx), the base serves as the main air transport hub for the President, Vice President, and senior leaders, and the **89th Airlift Wing** handles those special air missions.

**Key Missions:**

- Air transport for the President and senior leaders
- Special air mission airlift
- Fighter and airlift operations
