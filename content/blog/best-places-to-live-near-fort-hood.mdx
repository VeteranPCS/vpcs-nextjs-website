Fort Hood is one of the largest military installations in the world, home to over 45,000 active-duty soldiers and their families. If you are moving to Fort Hood, finding the right place to live is an important decision. This guide explores the best areas near the base that offer quality housing, strong communities, and access to amenities tailored to military families.

## **Why Live Near Fort Hood?**

Living near Fort Hood provides numerous benefits, including:

- A **strong military community** with camaraderie and support
- **Easy access to base amenities and services** designed for military families
- **Lower cost of living** compared to other parts of the country
- A **wide variety of housing options**, from suburban neighborhoods to small-town settings

## **Best Places to Live Near Fort Hood**

### **Killeen**

Killeen is the largest city near Fort Hood and offers a mix of **revitalized older neighborhoods and new housing developments**. With an expanding school system and increasing community investment, Killeen continues to grow as a desirable location.

**Pros:**

- **Affordable housing** options for both renters and buyers
- **Short commute to Fort Hood**
- **Growing amenities**, including shopping centers and entertainment venues

### **Harker Heights**

**Distance from Fort Hood:** 10 minutes

Harker Heights is known for its **excellent school district, safe neighborhoods, and suburban feel**. The area features rolling hills, valleys, and plenty of parks for outdoor activities.

**Pros:**

- **Highly rated schools** and family-friendly neighborhoods
- **Numerous parks and outdoor recreation** opportunities, including Dana Peak Park on Stillhouse Lake
- **Suburban environment** with easy access to shopping and dining

### **Belton**

**Distance from Fort Hood:** 20 minutes

Belton is a charming small town with **a historic downtown square, scenic parks, and access to Belton Lake**. The area is well-suited for families who enjoy outdoor recreation and a strong sense of community.

**Pros:**

- **Belton Lake** offers opportunities for boating, fishing, and paddleboarding
- **Quaint downtown area** with restaurants, shops, and walking trails
- Home to **Mary Hardin Baylor University**, a top-ranked Texas Christian university

## **Hidden Gems Near Fort Hood**

### **Salado**

**Distance from Fort Hood:** 30 minutes

Salado is a **small historic village** with a unique character. The town is known for its **antique shops, wineries, breweries, and boutiques**, making it an ideal place for weekend activities.

**Pros:**

- **Charming small-town atmosphere**
- **Scenic Salado Creek** runs through the center of town
- **Historic significance** as part of the Chisholm Trail

### **Nolanville**

**Distance from Fort Hood:** 13 miles

Nolanville is a **rapidly growing town** with new housing developments and a strong focus on community beautification. The town has received **Gold Star Affiliate recognition from Keep Texas Beautiful**.

**Pros:**

- **Newly constructed homes** and expanding infrastructure
- **Close proximity to Fort Hood**
- **Commitment to economic development and environmental sustainability**

### **Temple**

**Distance from Fort Hood:** 30 minutes

Temple is a **larger city with a top-ranked hospital system, a growing job market, and a revitalized downtown district**. The city is a great choice for families looking for **affordability, quality schools, and cultural attractions**.

**Pros:**

- **Home to Baylor Scott & White Medical Center**, one of the top hospitals in Texas
- **Affordable housing options** with strong investment potential
- **Flourishing downtown area** with restaurants, art, live music, and breweries
