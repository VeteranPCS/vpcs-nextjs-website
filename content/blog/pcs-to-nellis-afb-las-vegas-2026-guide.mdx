If you have orders for a PCS to Nellis Air Force Base, welcome to the Las Vegas valley. PCS stands for Permanent Change of Station, the military term for moving from one duty station to another. Nellis sits about 8 miles northeast of downtown Las Vegas, in Clark County, Nevada. It is a busy flying base with a strong community, set in the wide-open Mojave Desert.

A move is a lot to manage at once. Our goal is to make the Nellis part feel clearer. This 2026 guide walks you through where to live, how housing pay works, schools, healthcare, the local climate, and a few things to do once you are settled.

## What and Where Is Nellis Air Force Base?

Nellis Air Force Base sits in the northeast corner of the Las Vegas valley, in Clark County, Nevada. The host unit is the 99th Air Base Wing, which manages the day-to-day operations of the base, according to the [U.S. Air Force Warfare Center](https://www.nellis.af.mil/), the higher headquarters now based at Nellis.

Nellis is the home of the U.S. Air Force Warfare Center and the 57th Wing. The 57th Wing runs Red Flag, a large-force training exercise held over the nearby Nevada Test and Training Range, where U.S. and allied aircrews train against realistic threats. The 57th Wing is also the parent unit of the U.S. Air Force Air Demonstration Squadron, better known as the Thunderbirds. With all of this fighter activity, Nellis is often called the "Home of the Fighter Pilot."

Some airmen assigned to the Nellis area actually work at Creech Air Force Base, about 45 miles northwest in Indian Springs. According to [Creech Air Force Base](https://www.creech.af.mil/), Creech is the home of the 432nd Wing and the Air Force's remotely piloted aircraft mission. If your orders mention Creech, plan for a longer drive out of the valley.

## Where to Live When PCSing to Nellis AFB

Some families who move to Nellis live in on-base housing, while others settle in one of the nearby Las Vegas valley communities. On-base housing may have wait lists, so ask your sponsor and the housing office about current options early. For off-base living, your choice usually comes down to commute, schools, and budget.

### Sunrise Manor

Sunrise Manor sits right next to the base, often about 12 minutes from the gate. It is an established, mostly residential community with some of the shortest commutes to Nellis. Many families pick Sunrise Manor for the easy drive and the range of older and newer homes.

### North Las Vegas

North Las Vegas lies just west of the base, roughly 15 minutes from the gate depending on traffic. It is its own city, with growing neighborhoods, shopping, and a mix of home prices. Families often look here for newer housing close to the base.

### Aliante

Aliante is a master-planned area in northern North Las Vegas, about 18 minutes from the gate. It is known for its parks, trails, and newer family neighborhoods. Housing here can run a bit higher, but many families like the planned, suburban feel.

### Las Vegas

The city of Las Vegas spreads across the valley, with many neighborhoods around 20 minutes from the gate. It offers the widest range of homes, prices, and amenities, from older central areas to newer suburbs. Weigh the commute for the specific neighborhood you are considering.

### Henderson

Henderson sits southeast of the base, about 35 minutes from the gate with normal traffic. It is a large, well-regarded suburb with strong shopping, parks, and family neighborhoods. The longer commute is the trade-off for its amenities, so consider it against the drive.

### Summerlin

Summerlin is a master-planned community on the west side of the valley, roughly 40 minutes from the gate. It is popular for its parks, trails, and newer homes, and it sits close to Red Rock Canyon. Weigh the longer Nellis commute against the lifestyle and amenities here.

## Nellis AFB BAH and the Cost of Living

If you live off base, the military helps cover your housing through BAH, which stands for Basic Allowance for Housing. BAH is a monthly, tax-free payment. The amount depends on three things: your rank, whether you have dependents, and your duty station's Military Housing Area, or MHA. An MHA is the geographic zone the Defense Department uses to set local housing pay. Nellis falls within the Las Vegas, Nevada Military Housing Area.

Because rates change each year and vary by rank and dependent status, we do not publish a rate table here. To find your exact figure, use the official DoD calculator: [travel.dod.mil BAH Rate Lookup](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/). Enter your rank and ZIP code to see the current rate. Exact BAH rates are verified separately, so always confirm yours before signing a lease or making an offer.

The typical home value in Las Vegas was about $436,000 in June 2026, according to [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/18959/las-vegas-nv/). That is a typical value, not a median sale price. As of the week of June 18, 2026, [Freddie Mac](https://www.freddiemac.com/pmms) reported the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate at 6.47 percent. Whether buying or renting makes more sense depends on how long you expect to be stationed here, so run the numbers for your own situation.

## Schools Near Nellis AFB

The schools your children attend depend on where you live in the valley. Almost all public schools in the area are run by the [Clark County School District](https://www.ccsd.net/), which is Nevada's largest school district and one of the largest in the country.

Because the district is so large, attendance zones can be hard to predict from an address alone. Clark County School District offers a [zoning lookup](https://www.ccsd.net/zoning) that shows the assigned school for a specific home. Boundaries can shift, so confirm the assigned school for any address before you commit.

## Healthcare: Mike O'Callaghan Military Medical Center

On-base medical care for the Nellis community runs through the [Mike O'Callaghan Military Medical Center](https://nellis.tricare.mil/), a joint Department of Defense and Department of Veterans Affairs facility. That means active-duty members, military families, and veterans are cared for under one roof, and the two systems share resources. The center provides primary care, a range of specialty services, and a Level III trauma center.

Your military health coverage runs through TRICARE, the military health program. Set up your TRICARE plan early in your PCS. Update your information in DEERS, choose or transfer your plan, and request any referrals before you arrive so there is no gap in care. DEERS stands for the Defense Enrollment Eligibility Reporting System, the database that confirms who is eligible for military benefits. For care the center does not provide, you may be referred to providers in the larger Las Vegas area. Getting this squared away early is one of the easiest ways to reduce stress after you land.

## Climate and Things to Do

Las Vegas has a hot desert climate. Summers are very hot and dry, and winters are mild. The [National Weather Service office in Las Vegas](https://www.weather.gov/vef/) issues the local forecasts and posts heat warnings in the summer months. Plan for high heat from late spring through early fall, carry water, and take the desert sun seriously.

The area offers plenty to do close to home. [Red Rock Canyon National Conservation Area](https://www.blm.gov/programs/national-conservation-lands/nevada/red-rock-canyon), managed by the Bureau of Land Management, sits about 17 miles west of the Las Vegas Strip and offers a scenic drive, hiking, and rock climbing among red sandstone cliffs. [Lake Mead National Recreation Area](https://www.nps.gov/lake/index.htm), America's first national recreation area, spreads across 1.5 million acres with two large reservoirs for boating, fishing, and camping. [Valley of Fire State Park](https://parks.nv.gov/parks/valley-of-fire), Nevada's oldest state park, is known for bright red sandstone and ancient petroglyphs, though many trails close in summer for heat safety. And of course, the Las Vegas Strip adds shows, dining, and entertainment a short drive away.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What MHA or area is Nellis AFB in for BAH?

Nellis falls within the Las Vegas, Nevada Military Housing Area for BAH purposes. Your rate depends on your rank and whether you have dependents. Look up your exact figure with the official [DoD BAH calculator](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/) using your rank and ZIP code.

### Where do military families live near Nellis AFB?

Common choices close to the base include Sunrise Manor and North Las Vegas, which have the shortest commutes, plus Aliante for newer planned neighborhoods. Some families choose Henderson or Summerlin for their amenities, though those mean a longer drive. On-base housing is also an option, so ask your sponsor and the housing office.

### Where do my kids go to school near Nellis AFB?

Almost all public schools in the Las Vegas valley are run by the [Clark County School District](https://www.ccsd.net/), Nevada's largest district. The assigned school depends on your home address, so use the district's [zoning lookup](https://www.ccsd.net/zoning) and confirm the school for any specific address before you commit.

### Where do I get healthcare at Nellis AFB?

On-base care runs through the [Mike O'Callaghan Military Medical Center](https://nellis.tricare.mil/), a joint Department of Defense and Department of Veterans Affairs facility with primary care, specialty services, and a trauma center. Some care may be referred to providers in the larger Las Vegas area. Set up your TRICARE plan early, update DEERS, and arrange referrals before you arrive so your care continues without a gap.

### What is the weather like at Nellis AFB?

Las Vegas has a hot desert climate with very hot, dry summers and mild winters. The [National Weather Service office in Las Vegas](https://www.weather.gov/vef/) issues the local forecasts and posts heat warnings during the summer. Plan for high heat from late spring into early fall, stay hydrated, and limit time outdoors during the hottest parts of the day.
