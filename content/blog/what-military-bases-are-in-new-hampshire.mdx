New Hampshire has a small but important military footprint. The state is home to **two major active installations**, supporting the **Air National Guard** (part of the Air Force) and the **Space Force**. Whether you are **PCSing to New Hampshire** (Permanent Change of Station, the military term for an official move to a new duty station), thinking about a move, or just curious about military history, this guide gives you a clear overview of the **key military bases** in the state.

## Overview of Military Installations in New Hampshire

New Hampshire's two active installations cover very different missions. One is an **air refueling wing** that keeps aircraft flying around the world, and the other is a **satellite tracking station** that helps command and control spacecraft. Both are spread across the southern part of the state, and both keep military families moving in and out on PCS orders each year. New Hampshire's footprint is small, so it is easy to get a clear picture of where service members are stationed.

### Active Military Bases in New Hampshire

- **Pease Air National Guard Base** (157th Air Refueling Wing)
- **New Boston Space Force Station**

## Air National Guard Bases in New Hampshire

### Pease Air National Guard Base

Pease Air National Guard Base, near **Portsmouth and Newington** on New Hampshire's Seacoast, is home to the **157th Air Refueling Wing** of the New Hampshire Air National Guard. The wing's mission is **air refueling**, which means its aircraft fly fuel to other planes in midair so they can stay in the sky longer and travel farther. The 157th flies the **KC-46A Pegasus**, the newest tanker in the Air Force fleet, and operates a fleet of **12 of these aircraft**. The KC-46A can refuel other planes, carry cargo, and move patients during medical evacuation flights. The wing's flying unit is the **133rd Air Refueling Squadron**.

**Key Missions:**

- Air refueling for Air Force, Navy, Marine Corps, and partner aircraft
- Cargo and aeromedical evacuation support
- Home and overseas emergency response for New Hampshire and the nation

Learn more on the [157th Air Refueling Wing About Us page](https://www.157arw.ang.af.mil/About-Us/) and the [KC-46A Pegasus fact sheet](https://www.157arw.ang.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Display/Article/866395/kc-46a-pegasus/).

## Space Force Bases in New Hampshire

### New Boston Space Force Station

New Boston Space Force Station sits in **Hillsborough County** in south central New Hampshire, near the town of New Boston. It was transferred from the **Air Force to the Space Force in the summer of 2020**. The station began as a satellite tracking site in **1959**, and its hillside is dotted with large white domes, called **radomes**, that protect its antennas. The station is run by the **23rd Space Operations Squadron**, which operates the **largest remote tracking station** in the worldwide Satellite Control Network. From here, crews help track satellites, send them commands, and receive their data, keeping the nation's spacecraft working as planned.

**Key Missions:**

- Satellite command, control, and tracking
- Operating the largest Satellite Control Network tracking station
- Supporting assured access to space for the joint force

See the official [New Boston Space Force Station page](https://www.petersonschriever.spaceforce.mil/New-Boston-SFS-New-Hampshire/) and the [23rd Space Operations Squadron fact sheet](https://www.spaceforce.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Fact-Sheet-Display/Article/3742329/23rd-space-operations-squadron/).

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### 1. What is the largest military base in New Hampshire?

**Pease Air National Guard Base**, near Portsmouth, is the state's largest active installation. It is home to the 157th Air Refueling Wing, which flies a fleet of **12 KC-46A Pegasus tankers**. **New Boston Space Force Station** is the state's other active base.

### 2. How many military bases are in New Hampshire?

New Hampshire is home to **two major active installations**: Pease Air National Guard Base and New Boston Space Force Station. Together they support the Air National Guard and the Space Force.

### 3. What does Pease Air National Guard Base fly?

Pease ANGB flies the **KC-46A Pegasus**, the newest air refueling tanker in the Air Force fleet. The 157th Air Refueling Wing operates **12 of these aircraft** to refuel other planes in flight, carry cargo, and support medical evacuation.

### 4. Is Portsmouth Naval Shipyard in New Hampshire?

No. Despite its name, **Portsmouth Naval Shipyard is located on Seavey Island in Kittery, Maine**, just across the Piscataqua River from Portsmouth, New Hampshire. It is a Maine installation, not a New Hampshire one.
