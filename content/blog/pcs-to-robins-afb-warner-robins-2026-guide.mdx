If you have orders for a PCS to Robins Air Force Base, welcome to Middle Georgia. PCS stands for Permanent Change of Station, the military term for moving from one duty station to another. Robins AFB sits just outside Warner Robins, Georgia, in Houston County, and it is one of the Air Force's most important industrial installations. This 2026 guide covers what the base does, where to live, how housing pay works, schools, healthcare, the local climate, and things to do once you are settled.

## What and Where Is Robins AFB?

Robins Air Force Base is located in Houston County, Georgia, just south of the city of Warner Robins and about 18 miles south of Macon. It is the largest single-site industrial complex in Georgia, employing a workforce of almost 22,000 civilians, contractors, and military members, according to the [Robins AFB About Us page](https://www.robins.af.mil/About-Us/).

The [78th Air Base Wing](https://www.robins.af.mil/Units/78th-Air-Base-Wing/) (78 ABW) is the host wing. The installation's dominant tenant is the [Warner Robins Air Logistics Complex](https://www.robins.af.mil/Units/WR-Air-Logistics-Complex/) (WR-ALC), operating under Air Force Materiel Command (AFMC). Through approximately 6,000 employees, the WR-ALC provides depot maintenance and engineering support to major weapon systems including the F-15, C-5, C-130, C-17, and Special Operations Forces (SOF) aircraft. Robins is also home to the [461st Air Control Wing](https://www.robins.af.mil/Units/461st-Air-Control-Wing/), the [116th Air Control Wing](http://www.116acw.ang.af.mil/) of the Air National Guard, and Headquarters Air Force Reserve Command, among more than 50 mission partners forming what the installation calls Team Robins.

## Where to Live When PCSing to Robins AFB

Most families who move to Robins AFB choose between on-post housing and one of the surrounding communities. Your decision usually comes down to commute, schools, budget, and the feel you want. Ask your sponsor and the housing office about current on-post availability and wait times when you get your orders.

### Warner Robins

Warner Robins grew up alongside the base and remains the most popular choice for Robins families. A commute of about 10 minutes puts you close to work, and the city has shopping, dining, and services in every direction.

### Centerville

Centerville sits just north of Warner Robins, about 10 minutes from the main gate. It has a quieter suburban feel with established neighborhoods and quick access to shopping. For families who want to stay close without being in the middle of the city, it is a natural fit.

### Bonaire

Bonaire is a fast-growing community about 12 minutes from the gate, southeast of the base. It has seen significant new construction and draws families who want newer homes with more space.

### Kathleen

Kathleen is a smaller community southwest of the base, about 15 minutes away, with a more rural feel and larger lots. Families who want more land often look here.

### Byron

Byron sits northwest of the base just off Interstate 75, about 15 minutes out. It offers easy highway access and a small-town feel, and puts you closer to the Peach County area.

### Perry

Perry is the seat of Houston County, about 20 minutes south, with a historic downtown and a strong sense of community. The Georgia National Fair is held here every October.

### Macon

Macon is a larger city about 25 minutes north, in Bibb County. It offers the most in dining, arts, and medical services in the region and suits families who want a bigger-city setting and can absorb a longer commute.

## Robins AFB BAH and the Cost of Living

If you live off base, the military helps cover your housing through BAH, which stands for Basic Allowance for Housing. BAH is a monthly, tax-free payment that depends on your rank, dependent status, and your duty station's Military Housing Area (MHA). Robins AFB falls within the Warner Robins, Georgia MHA. Because rates change each year and vary by rank, we do not publish a rate table. Look up your exact figure at the official [DoD BAH Rate Lookup](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/) and confirm it before signing a lease or making an offer.

For a sense of buying costs, the average home value in Warner Robins was about $213,783 in May 2026, according to [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/131154/warner-robins-ga/) — a typical value, not a median sale price. As of the week of June 18, 2026, [Freddie Mac](https://www.freddiemac.com/pmms) reported the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate at 6.47 percent. Housing costs here are well below national averages, which can make BAH go further than at many other duty stations. Whether buying or renting makes sense depends on how long you expect to be stationed here.

## Schools Near Robins AFB

The schools your children attend depend on where you live, since several school systems serve the communities around the base.

[Houston County School District](https://www.hcbe.net/) serves Warner Robins, Centerville, Bonaire, Kathleen, and Perry. The district has about 2,000 students whose parents are in the military and works directly with the [Robins AFB School Liaison Program](https://www.robins.af.mil/Units/78th-Air-Base-Wing/School-Liaison-Program/) to ease PCS transitions. Georgia's HB 224 gives active-duty military families additional school transfer options, and multiple Houston County schools have earned the state's Military Flagship School Award. The district has a dedicated [military family support page](https://www.hcbe.net/militaryfamilysupport) with enrollment resources and contact information.

Families in Macon fall under [Bibb County School District](https://www.bcsdk12.net/), and those in Byron are served by Peach County Schools. Boundaries can shift, so confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit.

## Healthcare: The 78th Medical Group

On-base medical care is provided by the [78th Medical Group](https://robins.tricare.mil/) at Building 700 on the installation. With more than 400 assigned personnel, the 78th MDG serves over 47,000 eligible beneficiaries with primary care, preventive care, mental health, pediatrics, pharmacy, dental, vision, and emergency medicine.

Your coverage runs through TRICARE. Set up your plan early — update your information in DEERS, choose or transfer your plan, and request referrals through the [78th MDG appointments page](https://robins.tricare.mil/Getting-Care/Appointments-Referrals) before you arrive so your care does not lapse.

## Climate and Things to Do

Warner Robins has a humid subtropical climate. According to the [National Weather Service Atlanta/Peachtree City office](https://www.weather.gov/ffc/clisumlst), Central Georgia summers are long and warm, with average afternoon highs in the lower 90s and 70 to 80 days per year reaching 90 degrees or above. Winters are mild, with daytime highs in the mid-50s to lower 60s and overnight lows in the mid-30s. Snow is rare, averaging less than one inch per year. Rain falls on about 120 days annually, with thunderstorms common in spring and summer. Plan for heat and humidity from May through September.

When you are ready to explore, there is a lot close by. The [Museum of Aviation](https://museumofaviation.org/) sits right at Robins AFB and is the second-largest aviation museum in the country. Admission is free. Four climate-controlled buildings house around 85 historic Air Force aircraft and exhibits across 51 acres, open Monday through Saturday from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. and Sunday from 1 p.m. to 5 p.m.

About 25 minutes north, [Ocmulgee Mounds National Historical Park](https://www.nps.gov/ocmu/) in Macon protects one of the most significant archaeological sites in the southeastern United States, with earthwork mounds dating to the Mississippian Period around 900 CE. Trails follow the Ocmulgee River and admission is free. The region also offers boating and fishing at Lake Tobesofkee west of Macon and hiking along the Flint River at Sprewell Bluff.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What MHA is Robins AFB in for BAH?

Robins AFB falls within the Warner Robins, Georgia Military Housing Area. Your rate depends on rank and dependent status. Look up your exact figure with the [DoD BAH calculator](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/).

### Where do military families live near Robins AFB?

Warner Robins and Centerville are closest, about 10 minutes out. Bonaire, Kathleen, Byron, and Perry are popular in the 12-to-20-minute range. Macon is a bigger-city option about 25 minutes away. Ask your sponsor and housing office about on-base availability when you get orders.

### Which school district serves Robins AFB families?

Houston County School District covers Warner Robins, Centerville, Bonaire, Kathleen, and Perry. Macon families fall under Bibb County School District, and Byron families under Peach County Schools. Confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit.

### Where do I get healthcare at Robins AFB?

On-base care is provided by the [78th Medical Group](https://robins.tricare.mil/) at Building 700. Set up your TRICARE plan early, update DEERS, and arrange referrals before you arrive so your care does not lapse.

### What is the weather like in Warner Robins?

Humid subtropical. Summers are long and hot, with highs in the lower 90s from May through September. Winters are mild, with highs in the mid-50s to lower 60s and rare snow. The area sees about 45 to 50 inches of rain per year, per the [National Weather Service Atlanta/Peachtree City office](https://www.weather.gov/ffc/clisumlst).
