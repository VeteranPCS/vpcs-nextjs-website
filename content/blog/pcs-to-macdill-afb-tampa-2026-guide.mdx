If you have orders for a PCS to MacDill Air Force Base, welcome to Tampa, Florida. PCS stands for Permanent Change of Station, the military term for moving from one duty station to another. MacDill sits on a peninsula in south Tampa, in Hillsborough County, with water on three sides. It is a busy base with a big mission, and the Tampa Bay area around it is full of beaches, neighborhoods, and things for families to do. This 2026 guide walks you through what to expect when you move here, including where to live, how housing pay works, schools, healthcare, and daily life in the area.

A move is a lot to manage at once. Our goal is to make the MacDill part feel clearer. We will cover where to live, how housing pay works, schools, healthcare, the local climate, and a few things to do once you are settled.

## What and Where Is MacDill Air Force Base?

MacDill Air Force Base sits on the Interbay Peninsula in south Tampa, Florida, with Tampa Bay wrapping around it. According to the [6th Air Refueling Wing](https://www.macdill.af.mil/About-Us/Fact-Sheets/Fact-Sheet-View/Article/231802/6th-air-refueling-wing/), the 6th Air Refueling Wing (6 ARW) is the host unit at MacDill. The wing is part of Air Mobility Command, and it flies the KC-135 Stratotanker. In plain terms, those are the planes that refuel other aircraft in midair, which lets U.S. and allied flights reach much farther.

MacDill is also home to two major military headquarters. The same fact sheet notes that the base supports U.S. Central Command (USCENTCOM) and U.S. Special Operations Command (USSOCOM), along with dozens of other mission partners. Because of those headquarters, MacDill draws service members from every branch, not just the Air Force. That mix shapes the local community and the kinds of families you will meet here.

## Where to Live When PCSing to MacDill AFB

Some families who move to MacDill live in on-base housing, while others settle in one of the nearby Tampa Bay communities. On-base housing may have wait lists, so ask your sponsor and the housing office about current options early. For off-base living, your choice usually comes down to commute, schools, and budget. One thing to keep in mind: MacDill sits at the end of a peninsula with limited road access, so rush-hour traffic in and out of the gates can be heavy.

### South Tampa

South Tampa is the closest area to the base, often about 12 minutes from the gate. It includes established neighborhoods near the water and shorter commutes than almost anywhere else. Housing costs run higher here, but many families pick South Tampa for the easy drive and the close-in feel.

### Tampa

The wider city of Tampa sits to the north, roughly 20 minutes from the gate without heavy traffic. It offers the most variety in homes, jobs, and things to do, from downtown neighborhoods to quieter suburbs. Weigh the longer commute against the wider choice of housing and amenities.

### Brandon

Brandon lies east of Tampa, about 30 minutes from the gate when traffic cooperates. It is a large suburban area with newer neighborhoods, shopping, and family-friendly streets. Many families look here for more space and a suburban pace at a more manageable price.

### St. Petersburg

St. Petersburg sits across the bay to the southwest, roughly 35 minutes from the gate. It is a coastal city with its own downtown, waterfront parks, and beaches nearby. The commute crosses a bridge, so check current traffic patterns, but many families love the water access.

### Riverview

Riverview is south of Brandon, about 35 minutes from the gate. It has grown quickly with newer subdivisions and good value for the money. Families often look here when they want a newer home and more square footage for the budget.

### Apollo Beach

Apollo Beach sits south of the base along the bay, roughly 40 minutes from the gate. It is a quieter waterfront community with canals, parks, and a slower pace. The longer commute is the trade-off, but families who want bay living often feel it is worth it.

## MacDill AFB BAH and the Cost of Living

If you live off base, the military helps cover your housing through BAH, which stands for Basic Allowance for Housing. BAH is a monthly, tax-free payment. The amount depends on three things: your rank, whether you have dependents, and your duty station's Military Housing Area, or MHA. An MHA is the geographic zone the Defense Department uses to set local housing pay. MacDill falls within the Tampa, Florida Military Housing Area.

Because rates change each year and vary by rank and dependent status, we do not publish a rate table here. To find your exact figure, use the official DoD calculator: [travel.dod.mil BAH Rate Lookup](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/). Enter your rank and ZIP code to see the current rate. Exact BAH rates are verified separately, so always confirm yours before signing a lease or making an offer.

Tampa is a popular and growing market, so it helps to know what homes cost. The typical home value in Tampa was about $375,000 in June 2026, according to [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/41176/tampa-fl/). That is a typical value, not a median sale price. As of the week of June 18, 2026, [Freddie Mac](https://www.freddiemac.com/pmms) reported the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate at 6.47 percent. Whether buying or renting makes more sense depends on how long you expect to be stationed here, so run the numbers for your own situation.

## Schools Near MacDill AFB

The schools your children attend depend on where you live. The whole MacDill and Tampa area falls under [Hillsborough County Public Schools](https://www.sdhc.k12.fl.us/), one of the largest school districts in the country. The district runs elementary, middle, and high schools across Tampa, Brandon, Riverview, and the surrounding communities, along with magnet and school choice programs.

Because one district covers such a large area, the assigned school can change from one neighborhood to the next. Attendance zones can also shift over time. Confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit to a home.

## Healthcare: The 6th Medical Group at MacDill

On-base medical care for the MacDill community runs through the [6th Medical Group](https://macdill.tricare.mil/), which provides primary care and a range of outpatient services for eligible patients. For care the clinic does not provide, such as inpatient stays and some specialty and emergency services, you may be referred to hospitals in the larger Tampa area. Veterans in the region can also receive care through the [James A. Haley Veterans' Hospital](https://www.va.gov/tampa-health-care/locations/james-a-haley-veterans-hospital/) in Tampa.

Your military health coverage runs through TRICARE, the military health program. Set up your TRICARE plan early in your PCS. Update your information in DEERS, choose or transfer your plan, and request any referrals before you arrive so there is no gap in care. DEERS stands for the Defense Enrollment Eligibility Reporting System, the database that confirms who is eligible for military benefits. Getting this squared away early is one of the easiest ways to reduce stress after you land.

## Climate and Things to Do

Tampa has a humid subtropical climate. Summers are hot, humid, and rainy, while winters are mild and dry. The area also sits in the path of hurricane season, which runs from June through November. The [National Weather Service office in the Tampa Bay area](https://www.weather.gov/tbw/) issues local forecasts and storm updates, and it is worth following closely once you arrive so you know how to prepare for tropical weather.

The Tampa Bay area gives families plenty to do. The Gulf beaches near Clearwater and St. Petersburg are a short drive away, and the Tampa Riverwalk downtown lines the water with parks, museums, and restaurants. Busch Gardens, a large theme park in Tampa, is a popular family outing, and fishing is easy to find on the bay and the Gulf. For a quieter trip, [Egmont Key National Wildlife Refuge](https://www.fws.gov/refuge/egmont-key), an island at the mouth of Tampa Bay reachable only by boat, offers beaches, birds, and history. For a family that likes the water and the outdoors, this region has a lot to offer.
