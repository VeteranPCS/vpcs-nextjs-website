If you have orders for a PCS to Tyndall Air Force Base, welcome to the Florida Gulf coast. PCS stands for Permanent Change of Station, the military term for moving from one duty station to another. Tyndall AFB sits just east of Panama City on a narrow peninsula between St. Andrews Bay and the Gulf of Mexico. It is in the middle of a $4.7 billion rebuild following Hurricane Michael in 2018 and is on track to become the Air Force's first 21st-century "Installation of the Future."

This 2026 guide covers the essentials: mission and aircraft, where to live, BAH, schools, healthcare, and life on the Gulf coast.

## What and Where Is Tyndall AFB?

Tyndall Air Force Base is located along the Florida Panhandle, roughly 12 miles east of downtown Panama City in Bay County. The base occupies a peninsula between St. Andrews Bay to the north and the Gulf of Mexico to the south.

The host unit is the [325th Fighter Wing](https://www.tyndall.af.mil/About/Fact-Sheets/Article/314996/325th-fighter-wing/), under Air Combat Command (ACC) and Fifteenth Air Force. According to the official fact sheet on tyndall.af.mil, the wing's mission is to project unrivaled combat airpower. The wing is currently transitioning to the F-35A Lightning II, a fifth-generation stealth multirole fighter. When the transition is complete, the 325th FW will operate three F-35A squadrons, becoming the Air Force's newest operational fighter wing. The F-22 Raptor training mission that was previously at Tyndall relocated to Joint Base Langley-Eustis in 2023.

The wing also includes the 325th Operations Group, 325th Maintenance Group, 325th Mission Support Group, and 325th Medical Group, plus 19 tenant organizations.

The rebuild is a defining feature of Tyndall right now. Hurricane Michael made direct landfall in October 2018, causing widespread destruction. New facilities and a modernized layout are going up across the installation — an unusual situation for any incoming family to navigate, but also a sign of significant long-term investment in the base.

## Where to Live When PCSing to Tyndall AFB

Most families who move to Tyndall either live in on-post housing or settle in one of the communities surrounding Panama City. Your choice usually comes down to commute, schools, budget, and the feel you want. Because the base is still rebuilding after Hurricane Michael, ask your sponsor and the housing office about on-post availability and wait times before you commit to anything.

### Callaway

Callaway is one of the closest communities to the main gate, about 10 minutes away. It is a practical, working-family town with established neighborhoods and short commutes — a common first choice for incoming service members.

### Parker

Parker is also about 10 minutes from the base, near the shore of St. Andrews Bay. It is small and close-knit, with a mix of housing options for families who want a short commute and a quieter setting.

### Springfield

Springfield sits just north of Panama City, roughly 12 minutes from Tyndall. It offers a mix of established homes and newer development at a practical price for the area.

### Panama City

Panama City proper is the county seat and the largest urban center nearby, about 15 minutes from the base. It has the widest range of housing, shopping, restaurants, and services. Most families looking to rent or buy start their search here.

### Lynn Haven

Lynn Haven is about 20 minutes from Tyndall, on the north shore of North Bay. It has a reputation for well-kept neighborhoods and good schools and is popular with families who want a little more space without a long drive.

### Panama City Beach

Panama City Beach sits across the Hathaway Bridge from Panama City, about 30 minutes from Tyndall. It offers the area's most famous white-sand Gulf beaches. Housing runs higher than the rest of Bay County, but some families choose it for the waterfront access and lifestyle.

### Mexico Beach

Mexico Beach is a small, quiet community roughly 30 minutes east of Tyndall. It was heavily damaged by Hurricane Michael and has been rebuilding. For families who want a slower pace and direct Gulf access, it is worth a look, though services are limited compared with Panama City.

## Tyndall AFB BAH and the Cost of Living

If you live off base, the military helps cover your housing through BAH, which stands for Basic Allowance for Housing. BAH is a monthly, tax-free payment tied to three things: your rank, whether you have dependents, and your duty station's Military Housing Area, or MHA. An MHA is the geographic zone the Department of Defense uses to set local housing pay. Tyndall AFB falls within the Panama City, Florida Military Housing Area.

Because rates change each year and vary by rank and dependent status, we do not publish a rate table here. To find your exact figure, use the official DoD calculator at [travel.dod.mil BAH Rate Lookup](https://www.travel.dod.mil/Allowances/Basic-Allowance-for-Housing/BAH-Rate-Lookup/). Enter your rank and ZIP code to see the current rate. Always confirm your actual rate before signing a lease or making an offer.

For a sense of buying costs, the typical home value in Panama City was about $277,784 in June 2026, according to [Zillow](https://www.zillow.com/home-values/46978/panama-city-fl/). That is a typical value, not a median sale price. The Panama City Beach market runs considerably higher, so your specific neighborhood will matter. As of the week of June 18, 2026, [Freddie Mac](https://www.freddiemac.com/pmms) reported the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate at 6.47 percent. Whether buying or renting makes more sense depends on your family's timeline and budget, so run the numbers for your own situation.

## Schools Near Tyndall AFB

The schools your children attend depend on where you live. The entire Bay County area is served by [Bay District Schools](https://www.bay.k12.fl.us/), the county's public school system. According to the National Center for Education Statistics ([NCES](https://nces.ed.gov/ccd/districtsearch/district_detail.asp?Search=2&details=1&DistrictID=1200090&ID2=1200090)), Bay District Schools operates 49 schools serving grades pre-K through 12 across the county with a staff of more than 3,700.

Bay District Schools was significantly affected by Hurricane Michael in 2018, with many facilities since rebuilt or replaced. Boundaries are assigned by address and can change, so confirm the assigned school for any specific home before you commit.

## Healthcare: 325th Medical Group

On-base medical care at Tyndall AFB is provided by the [325th Medical Group](https://tyndall.tricare.mil/), which offers primary care and a range of health services for eligible patients. The 325th MDG's website at tyndall.tricare.mil lists primary care, flight medicine, mental health, and other services.

Your coverage runs through TRICARE, the health care program for service members and their families. Update your DEERS record, choose or transfer your plan, and arrange any referrals before you arrive. Appointment and referral information is on the [325th MDG site](https://tyndall.tricare.mil/Getting-Care/Appointments-Referrals).

## Climate and Things to Do

Panama City sits in the Florida Panhandle under a humid subtropical climate — warm, humid summers and mild winters. According to the [National Weather Service in Tallahassee](https://www.weather.gov/tae/climate), July and August highs regularly reach the low 90s Fahrenheit, while January and February nights can dip into the 30s with occasional cold fronts.

Every incoming family should understand hurricane season. The Atlantic season runs June 1 through November 30, with peak activity in August through October. Tyndall AFB took a catastrophic direct hit from Hurricane Michael in October 2018 — the same storm that triggered the current $4.7 billion rebuild. Learn your unit's hurricane preparation and evacuation plan before storm season arrives.

Outside of storm season, the Panhandle is a beautiful place to live. The water near Panama City is known for an unusually clear, emerald-green color produced by white quartz sand and shallow depths close to shore.

[St. Andrews State Park](https://www.floridastateparks.org/parks-and-trails/st-andrews-state-park) sits at the tip of the peninsula just west of Tyndall. According to the Florida State Parks website, the park has more than 1.5 miles of white sand beaches, some of the best-preserved coastal dunes in the region, fishing on the jetties, pontoon boat rentals, and daily boat trips to Shell Island. The park is open every day from 8 a.m. until sundown.

Panama City Beach offers the full resort experience — boardwalks, restaurants, and Gulf beaches. For something quieter, the area east of Tyndall toward Apalachicola includes undeveloped state forest and miles of largely empty coast.

### Where do military families live near Tyndall AFB?

Common choices include Callaway and Parker, which are closest to the base at about 10 minutes; Springfield and Panama City for more options and services; Lynn Haven for a family-friendly suburb with good schools; and Panama City Beach for those who want to live on the Gulf. On-base housing is also available, but ask your sponsor about wait times given the ongoing rebuild.

### Which school district serves Tyndall AFB families?

Bay District Schools covers all of Bay County, including Callaway, Parker, Springfield, Panama City, and Lynn Haven. The district operates 49 schools for pre-K through 12th grade. Confirm the assigned school for any specific address before you commit.

### Where do I get healthcare at Tyndall AFB?
